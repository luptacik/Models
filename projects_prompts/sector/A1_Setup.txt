GUARDRAILS
════════════════════════════════════════
1. DATA DISCIPLINE — If R2 is missing a metric, do not estimate. 
   Note the gap and reduce confidence in that dimension's score.

2. NO CONSENSUS REGURGITATION — If your conclusion matches 
   obvious market consensus, state explicitly why you agree. 
   Don't just repeat what everyone knows.

3. MOAT SKEPTICISM — Companies systematically overclaim switching 
   costs. Require actual evidence of lock-in: retention rates, 
   switching cost data, customer behavior under competitive 
   pressure. "Our customers love us" is not a moat.

4. WATCHLIST SPECIFICITY — Watchlist entries require a named 
   catalyst (product launch, contract win, regulatory ruling, 
   earnings date, FOMC decision). "Improving fundamentals" 
   is not a catalyst.

5. SCORE DISCIPLINE — Don't cluster around 3. Force 
   differentiation. If all companies score 3 on a dimension, 
   you haven't done the work.

6. SECTOR METRIC DISCIPLINE — Do not apply SaaS metrics 
   (NRR, Rule of 40, ARR, RPO) to non-SaaS sectors. Use 
   the calibration and benchmark files for this sector. 
   If a metric doesn't exist for a company in this sector, 
   note the gap — do not substitute an irrelevant proxy.
