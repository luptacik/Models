**Gap Analysis:** The market is implying **16.2% CAGR for 10 years** at base-case WACC/terminal-margin. This matches FY2026 consensus growth but assumes that rate is sustained for a decade. Given:

**Sustaining 16% CAGR for a decade requires stacking MRDIMM + HBM4E + DDR6 + CKD adoption perfectly.** That is achievable in the bull case, but it is NOT a "roughly fair" assumption — it is a bullish assumption embedded in the current price. At lower terminal margins (40%), implied CAGR rises to 17.5% — decisively above any credible long-term growth narrative for a company with 3-supplier ceiling constraints in its anchor product.

**Verdict:** Current price is pricing in the bull-case growth narrative as if it were a base case. This is the primary mispricing signal.
