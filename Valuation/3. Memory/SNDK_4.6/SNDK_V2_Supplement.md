## SUPPLEMENT 1: HISTORICAL NAND CYCLE ANALOG

### Industry Revenue Pattern

| Period | Phase | Industry NAND Rev | YoY Change |
|--------|-------|-------------------|------------|
| 2017 | Late upcycle | ~$57B | +44% |
| 2018 | **PEAK** | ~$63B | +11% |
| 2019 | Downcycle | ~$42B | **−33%** |
| 2020 | Trough/Early | ~$47B | +12% |
| 2021 | Upcycle | ~$67B | +43% |
| 2022 | Peak→Down | ~$55B | −18% |
| 2023 | **TROUGH** | ~$37B | **−33%** |
| 2024 | Recovery | ~$68B | +84% |
| 2025 | Upcycle | ~$87B | +28% |
| 2026E | Supercycle | ~$130B+ | +50%+ |

**Key pattern:** Peak-to-trough revenue declines of 33% are the **norm**, not the exception. The 2018→2019 decline was −33%. The 2021→2023 decline was −45%. Average cycle length peak-to-peak is ~3.5 years. Average peak-to-trough duration is 12–18 months.

### Analog Mapping to SNDK

**If the 2017–2019 analog applies:**
- Peak revenue: FY2027 ~$26.5B (consensus)
- Trough revenue (2 years later): $26.5B × 0.67 = ~$17.7B
- Trough SNDK EPS: ~$15–25 (vs. $90.46 consensus FY2027)
- At 10x trough P/E: **$150–$250/share**

**If the 2021–2023 analog applies (deeper downcycle):**
- Peak revenue: FY2027 ~$26.5B
- Trough revenue: $26.5B × 0.55 = ~$14.6B
- Trough SNDK EPS: ~$5–15
- At 10x trough P/E: **$50–$150/share**

### "Supercycle" Claims vs. Historical Base Rates

The current "supercycle" thesis rests on HBM capacity absorption preventing NAND oversupply, enterprise SSD for AI being structural rather than cyclical, and disciplined capex. However, supercycle claims have been made at every peak — in 2017 ("first ever structural undersupply"), 2021 ("pandemic-driven permanent demand shift"), and now 2026. **Every prior claim was wrong.**

This time IS different in one respect: HBM absorbing DRAM wafer capacity does structurally tighten NAND supply. But SNDK has **no HBM exposure** — it benefits only from the secondary effect (less NAND capacity available because Big 3 are converting wafers to HBM). This is a weaker, more indirect tailwind than what MU enjoys as a direct HBM producer.

## SUPPLEMENT 2: MARGIN NORMALIZATION BRIDGE

### Gross Margin Decomposition (Q4 FY2025 → Q3 FY2026E: +39.6pp)

| Driver | Contribution | Cyclical vs. Structural |
|--------|:---:|:---:|
| NAND ASP surge (contract +55–60% QoQ × 2Q) | +25–28pp | **100% CYCLICAL** |
| BiCS8 cost-per-bit reduction | +5–7pp | Structural |
| Mix shift (datacenter ↑, enterprise ↑) | +3–4pp | Mostly structural |
| Fab utilization (100% vs. under-utilized) | +2–3pp | Cyclical |
| Fixed cost leverage (rev 2x, opex flat) | +2–3pp | Partially structural |
| **Total** | **~39.6pp** | **~65% cyclical** |

### Normalized Through-Cycle Margins

| Metric | Trough (Q4 FY25) | Peak (Q3 FY26E) | **Normalized** |
|--------|:-:|:-:|:-:|
| Gross Margin | 26.4% | 65–67% | **35–42%** |
| Operating Margin | 5.3% | ~52% | **15–22%** |
| FCF Margin | 2.6% | ~30%+ | **10–18%** |

**The critical insight:** ~25–28pp of the 40pp gross margin expansion is pure ASP-driven cyclicality. NAND ASPs have **never** sustained supercycle levels. When the cycle turns, two-thirds of the margin expansion reverses. The structural improvements (BiCS8, mix shift, JV leverage) add 11–13pp vs. trough, implying a normalized GM of 37–40%.

**Valuation cross-check at normalized economics:**
- Mid-cycle revenue: ~$20B
- Normalized FCF margin: ~14%
- FCF: ~$2.8B
- At 20x FCF: EV = $56B → **$363/share**
- This is consistent with the DCF base case ($307) and SoP ($344).

## SUPPLEMENT 4: KIOXIA-SANDISK MERGER SCENARIO

**Scenario B — Kioxia acquires SNDK:**
- At 20% premium: $1,092/share → $171B equity value
- Kioxia cannot finance this — **not realistic** at current levels

**Scenario C — SK Hynix interference (post-2028):**
- SK Hynix exercises convertible, creates auction dynamics
- Could push SNDK to $1,000+ short-term but antitrust risk is very high

**Key insight:** The merger makes far more financial sense when SNDK is at $300–$500 (mid-cycle). At $910, the combined entity would trade at an unjustifiable 9x+ revenue. **M&A optionality is inversely correlated with the current price** — it adds value when the stock is cheap, not when it's expensive.

## SUPPLEMENT 5: TURBOQUANT COMPRESSION RISK

### Direct Revenue Impact

| Variable | Estimate |
|----------|----------|
| AI-related NAND revenue | ~$1.5–2.0B/yr (~10–13% of FY2026E) |
| TurboQuant demand reduction | 30–50% of AI NAND |
| Revenue impact | −$450M to −$1.0B annually |
| EPS impact | −$2 to −$5/share |

### Second-Order (Narrative) Risk

The direct fundamental impact is moderate (3–7% of revenue). But the **sentiment risk** is severe — if the market interprets TurboQuant as signaling AI memory demand is peaking, multiple compression from 6.5x → 4x NTM revenue would imply a stock price of ~$560–$580 (−35–38%). This is exactly what happened on March 25–26: the selloff was 20–30%, driven by narrative, not fundamentals.

**Asymmetric exposure:** SNDK is MORE exposed than MU (NAND vs. HBM) and MORE exposed than Rambus/Montage (interface chips are agnostic to memory type). This makes SNDK the highest-risk memory name for any TurboQuant-related narrative shift.
