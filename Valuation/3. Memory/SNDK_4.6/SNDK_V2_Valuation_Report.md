## KEY ANALYTICAL TENSIONS

### The Peak-Cycle P/E Trap

The most important finding from this analysis is the divergence between P/E-based valuation and all other methods:

| Method | Fair Value (Base) | vs. Current |
|--------|-------------------|-------------|
| P/E on FY2027 EPS at 10x | $905 | вҲ’1% (вүҲ fair) |
| Forward DCF | $307 | вҲ’66% |
| Trading Comps (EV/Rev) | $672 | вҲ’26% |
| M&A Comps | $303 | вҲ’67% |
| Sum-of-Parts | $344 | вҲ’62% |
| PE/LBO Floor | $278 | вҲ’69% |

The P/E on FY2027 EPS looks fair because FY2027 represents **peak-cycle earnings** ($90.46 EPS). But memory earnings at cycle peaks are transient вҖ” they have historically collapsed 50вҖ“80% in subsequent downturns. Applying a "fair" 10x multiple to peak earnings is a classic valuation trap in cyclical industrials. The through-cycle methods (DCF, SoP, M&A, LBO) all converge on $280вҖ“$350 because they account for the inevitable downcycle. The market is implicitly assuming peak earnings persist вҖ” that has never happened in NAND history.

### What Must Go Right for $910 to Be Fair

1. The NAND supercycle extends through at least 2028 (AI capex sustains 30%+ CAGR)
2. Enterprise SSD for AI inference becomes a structural, not cyclical, demand driver
3. Kioxia-SanDisk merger creates pricing discipline that flattens the cycle
4. Google TurboQuant and similar compression techniques do NOT materially reduce NAND demand
5. No macro recession or AI capex correction in 2026вҖ“2028

All five must occur simultaneously. The probability of all five is meaningfully below 50%.
