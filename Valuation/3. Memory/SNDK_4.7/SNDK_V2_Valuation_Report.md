### WHY METHODS DIVERGE

Significant divergence between LBO floor ($39–$89), M&A ($121–$229), DCF ($137–$604), SoP ($249–$436), and Trading Comps ($202–$729). This is informative, not error:

1. **Cycle sensitivity:** DCF and LBO price in full cycle mean reversion. Trading comps at peak NTM revenue do not. This gap reflects the divergence between peak-cycle and normalized earnings power.

2. **Strategic vs. financial value:** M&A bounds what someone would pay to own the cash flows. Trading comps bound what the public market pays for exposure. At supercycle peaks, public markets pay premiums that strategic buyers refuse.

3. **Time horizon:** DCF spans 7 years + terminal. Trading comps are NTM. SNDK's fair value depends profoundly on cycle duration — if the supercycle extends through FY28, trading comps are right. If it rolls over in late FY27, DCF is right.

**The convergence that matters:** *All methods, including the most generous (Trading Comps bull at $729), price SNDK below $910.* The divergence is about magnitude of overvaluation, not direction.
