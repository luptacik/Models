## ━━━ EXECUTIVE SUMMARY ━━━

**Triangulated price target: $28 (bear) / $45 (base) / $66 (bull)**
**Current price $128 sits above every single method's bull case.**
**Conviction: 4/5** that the stock is materially overvalued.

Palantir is operationally the best business in the entire Data Analytics / AI Platforms sector — Rule of 40 of 127, 70% revenue growth, 51% adjusted FCF margins, $7.2B net cash, a genuinely differentiated ontology-layer moat, and management execution that has produced four consecutive beat-and-raise quarters with a FY26 guide that crushed consensus by ~$1B. None of that is in dispute. The valuation problem is that the market price is already discounting an outcome more extreme than this operating reality.

At $128, the enterprise value is $322B on $7.41B of FY26 consensus revenue — a 43.5x EV/NTM Revenue multiple and an 80x EV/NTM adjusted FCF multiple. The reverse DCF solves the question directly: to justify $128, the market is implying Palantir grows revenue at ~39% CAGR every year for the next decade, even under a generous 35% terminal FCF margin and 10% WACC. Consensus has FY27 at +42.6% and then no public estimates exist — but sector-wide fade curves and Palantir's own second-derivative suggest growth will be in the high-20s to low-30s by FY28-29 and in the teens by FY31. The market-implied 39% 10-year CAGR is not impossible, but it leaves zero margin of safety: any deceleration trajectory that looks even remotely like a normal enterprise software fade curve produces a fair value below $60.

Every other method agrees. Forward DCF (blended 60% adjusted FCF / 40% FCF-ex-SBC to honor the 15.3% SBC-to-revenue ratio) produces $52 base case under assumptions that are generous to bullish — FY26 at the company guide of +61%, FY27 at consensus +42%, and a 7-year fade to 13% that sustains 50%+ FCF margins the whole way. Trading comps land at $40 even after applying a regression-extrapolated 12.4x NTM revenue multiple that is already a 40% premium to the 9-10x multiples SNOW and DDOG trade at. Sum-of-parts produces $44 even when the US Commercial segment is valued at 22x forward revenue (a hyper-growth SaaS multiple) and US Government at 12x (Booz Allen / Anduril-style defense tech). The PE/LBO floor is mechanical and unreliable at this scale (no PE fund can write a $300B equity check), but for what it's worth it produces $27.

The pattern is consistent: every method built from fundamentals up produces a price in the $25-$75 range. The current price is 70-80% above the midpoint. This is not a data gap or a method disagreement problem — this is a tightly clustered result across independent methods, which is what conviction looks like. The only way $128 is defensible is if you believe PLTR becomes a $60B+ revenue business by 2033 (similar to where Oracle is today) with durable 50%+ FCF margins. That is a real scenario, but it is the bull-of-bulls case, not the base case.

Business quality and valuation verdict must be held separate here. PLTR is a genuinely excellent company. It is also, at $128, the most expensive stock in the sector by a factor of 3-4x on every multiple that matters, with management insiders (Karp, Thiel) having liquidated over $4B in shares since 2024 and another $288M in early March 2026 — a signal that warrants weight.

## METHOD 1 — REVERSE DCF

**Question:** What 10-year revenue CAGR must PLTR achieve to justify the current EV of $322.2B?

**Assumptions:**
- Terminal FCF margin: 35% (within the sector "FCF at scale" benchmark of 25-35% for mature SaaS; PLTR is currently at 51% so 35% is a fade)
- Terminal growth: 3%
- WACC: 10% (US-domiciled, zero debt, high liquidity — standard)
- Starting FCF margin: 51% (FY25 adjusted)
- Margins fade linearly from 51% to 35% over 10 years

**Result:** **Market-implied 10-year revenue CAGR = 39.2%**

### Gap analysis

| Reference | Growth |
|---|---|
| FY25 actual | 56% |
| FY26 company guide | 61% |
| FY26 consensus | 65.5% |
| FY27 consensus | 42.6% |
| Market-implied (reverse DCF) | **39.2% every year for 10 years** |

Consensus only extends to FY27. If the market is pricing 39% CAGR through 2035, it is implicitly assuming that PLTR sustains 35%+ growth deep into FY30-FY33, at a revenue scale of $30-60B. For context, no enterprise software company in history has sustained 35%+ growth past $30B in revenue — not Salesforce, not ServiceNow, not Oracle, not Microsoft's cloud business in isolation.

### Sensitivity — implied 10-yr revenue CAGR (%)

| WACC \ Terminal FCF margin | 25% | 30% | 35% | 40% |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 9% | 40.7% | 38.3% | 36.2% | 34.4% |
| 10% | 43.8% | 41.3% | **39.2%** | 37.4% |
| 11% | 46.7% | 44.1% | 42.0% | 40.1% |
| 12% | 49.4% | 46.8% | 44.6% | 42.7% |

Across the entire plausible grid, the implied CAGR never drops below 34%. Even the most bullish corner (9% WACC, 40% terminal margin) still requires a ten-year 34% CAGR — a rate no SaaS company has sustained from a similar starting point.

**Verdict:** The reverse DCF is the single cleanest mispricing signal in this entire file. The market is pricing a growth outcome that would require PLTR to replicate or exceed the best decade in enterprise software history, starting from a revenue base that is already four times what most companies reach before decelerating.

## KEY RISKS TO THE VALUATION VIEW

**Risks that could make the current price correct:**
1. **Sovereign AI TAM expansion.** If the US DoD and allied governments dramatically expand AI infrastructure spending in 2026-2028 (plausible under current administration priorities), PLTR's government segment could sustain 50%+ growth much longer than modeled. This is the single strongest bull argument.
2. **Ontology as a standard.** If the Foundry ontology becomes a de facto enterprise data standard (similar to how Oracle's relational DB became the standard in the 1990s), PLTR could command durable above-peer multiples for a decade-plus.
3. **Reflexivity / momentum.** Richly-valued momentum names can compound their overvaluation for extended periods. Being right on intrinsic value and wrong on stock price is common in short time frames.

**Risks that confirm the overvaluation view:**
1. **Insider selling at scale.** Karp >$4B sold since 2024; Thiel $288M sold Mar 2, 2026; CTO Sankar $22M sold Feb 20, 2026. Zero open-market purchases by any insider. This is the largest concentrated insider liquidation in the sector.
2. **International commercial at +2% FY25.** The business that is supposed to replicate the US commercial explosion globally is effectively flat. This is the empirical test of whether AIP is a US phenomenon or a global one, and so far the answer is "US only."
3. **Hyperscaler displacement risk.** Azure AI, AWS Bedrock, Google Vertex AI continue to improve and are priced aggressively below AIP. The longer PLTR maintains 50%+ growth, the more incentive hyperscalers have to invest in directly competitive offerings.
4. **Multiple compression math.** If PLTR's growth decelerates to "only" 35% in FY27 (vs consensus 43%), the multiple re-rates from 43x toward 20-25x. A 20x multiple on FY27 revenue of $9.5B = $190B EV = ~$76/share. A re-rating to normal growth comps alone produces a 40% drawdown without any operational disappointment.
