## METHOD 1: REVERSE DCF

**Purpose:** What growth rate does the current price imply?

### Setup
- Current EV: RMB 18,098M ($2,496M)
- Current Revenue: RMB 8,267.5M
- Current FCF Margin: ~48.7% (estimated)
- Assumptions: Terminal FCF margin 35%, terminal growth 3%, WACC 11%

### Result

**→ Implied Revenue CAGR: -10.0%**

The market is pricing in **negative 10% annual revenue decline** over 10 years for a company currently growing at 12.4%, with management guiding 7–8% in Q1'26 (seasonally weak) and expecting acceleration thereafter. This is an extreme mispricing signal.

To justify the current price, Kanzhun's revenue would need to **shrink by 10% annually for a decade** — implying the company effectively goes into permanent secular decline, losing its dominant #1 position with 2x the MAU of its nearest competitor and 85% gross margins.

The operating value per ADS is only $5.71, implying the market views the business itself as barely worth its single-year free cash flow.
