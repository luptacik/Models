## METHOD 1: REVERSE DCF

**Current EV:** RMB 14.0B (Market Cap RMB 27.0B - Net Cash RMB 13.0B)
**Assumptions:** Terminal FCF margin 25%, terminal growth 3%, WACC 11%

**Implied revenue CAGR: -6.1%**

This is the single most important finding of this valuation. The market is pricing Cloud Music as if revenue will *decline* at ~6% annually for a decade. This is dramatically below:
- Actual music services growth: +12.0% (FY2025)
- Subscription revenue growth: +13.3% (FY2025)
- Consensus NTM growth: ~7% (blended, with social entertainment drag)

**Interpretation:** The total revenue decline of -2.4% in FY2025 is entirely driven by the intentional wind-down of social entertainment (RMB 5.0B → 1.8B over 3 years). Music services — which is 77% of revenue and growing 12% — is being obscured by this declining segment. As social entertainment approaches its floor (~RMB 1.0B by FY2028), total revenue growth should accelerate to the underlying music services growth rate.

**→ STRONG UNDERPRICING SIGNAL.** The market is pricing the declining headline number and ignoring the quality of the underlying growth.

## KEY ANALYTICAL OBSERVATIONS

**1. The social entertainment drag is temporary.** Social entertainment has fallen from RMB 5.0B (FY2022) to RMB 1.8B (FY2025) — a 64% decline. At the current ~30% annual decline rate, it reaches ~RMB 1.0B by FY2028 and stabilizes. Once this headwind abates, total revenue growth should converge toward the 8-12% music services growth rate. The market appears to be treating the -2.4% headline revenue decline as permanent rather than transitional.

**2. Paid penetration is the underappreciated story.** Back-calculated estimates suggest Cloud Music's paid penetration may have reached ~29-31%, potentially *above* TME's 22.5%. Against a global benchmark of Spotify's 39.4%, there is still meaningful room for expansion. However, ARPPU dilution (from ~RMB 6.9 to ~6.3-6.5) is the trade-off — the company is growing subscribers faster than it can monetize each one.

**3. Net cash is nearly half the market cap.** RMB 12-14B in net cash against a RMB ~27B market cap means the enterprise value is only ~RMB 14B — or ~1.8x trailing revenue. For a company with 22% operating margins and 12% segment growth, this is cheap by almost any measure.

**4. The key risk is governance, not fundamentals.** NetEase's 90% voting control means Cloud Music's capital allocation, dividends, and strategic direction are not in minority shareholders' hands. The massive intercompany receivable (~RMB 9-12B) means cash is held at the parent level. This governance discount is real and permanent — but the stock appears to be pricing in more governance risk than warranted given NetEase's own high-quality track record.
