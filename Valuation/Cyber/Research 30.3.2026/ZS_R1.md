# Zscaler, Inc. (ZS) — Comprehensive Company Research Dossier

**Zscaler is the dominant pure-play cloud security platform**, processing 500B+ daily transactions through its Zero Trust Exchange for ~45% of the Fortune 500. The company posted Q2 FY2026 revenue of **$815.8M (+26% YoY)**, Non-GAAP EPS of **$1.01**, and ARR of **$3,359M (+25%)**, beating consensus handily. Yet the stock trades at ~$132, down **~36% YTD** and ~60% from its late-2025 highs near $330, reflecting valuation compression, SBC concerns, and competitive anxiety around Palo Alto Networks' platformization and Microsoft's E5 bundling. With **$1.8B net cash**, accelerating organic net new ARR (+10% 2-year stack), and AI security ARR exceeding **$500M**, the fundamental trajectory is strong even as the market reprices growth multiples. This dossier covers all 10 modules with cross-referenced data from SEC filings, earnings releases, and financial databases.

## Module 9: SSE dominance intact but PANW and Microsoft are closing in

### Market position and analyst recognition

Zscaler holds the **#1 SSE market share** globally per Dell'Oro Group and has been named a **Gartner Magic Quadrant Leader for SSE for four consecutive years**, with the **highest score on "Ability to Execute"** in the May 2025 MQ. The company was named a **Forrester Wave Leader for SASE** and **Zero Trust Platforms** (both Q3 2025), with Forrester calling Zscaler "the 800-pound gorilla of SSE." In the Gartner MQ for SASE Platforms, Zscaler was positioned as a **Visionary** (not Leader) due to its non-traditional approach of replacing rather than integrating SD-WAN.

The SASE market reached **$2.4B in Q3 2024** per Dell'Oro, with the top six vendors holding 72% share. Zscaler's ~$3.4B ARR run-rate would make it the largest pure-play SASE/SSE vendor.

### Competitive differentiation

**Versus Palo Alto Networks Prisma Access:** The core architectural divide is Zscaler's **cloud-native proxy** (users connect directly to apps, never touching the corporate network) versus PANW's **firewall-extended-to-cloud** approach (connecting users to a cloud-hosted network then applying security). Zscaler eliminates lateral movement by design; PANW maintains network routing constructs. PANW's advantage is the **single-vendor SASE** narrative with integrated SD-WAN and the massive NGFW installed base for conversion. PANW is the biggest direct threat.

**Versus Microsoft Entra/Defender:** Microsoft's E5 bundling makes security capabilities near-zero marginal cost for existing customers, creating a powerful TCO argument. However, Microsoft's SSE capabilities are newer and less mature — Gartner Peer Insights rates Microsoft **4.3 stars (378 reviews)** versus Zscaler's **4.7 stars (1,127 reviews)**. Zscaler's purpose-built inline inspection and broader PoP coverage (160+) maintain a functional edge. Microsoft is the biggest **indirect** long-term threat.

**Versus others:** Netskope leads in DLP/CASB but lacks Zscaler's breadth. Cloudflare excels in performance and mid-market but has less enterprise security depth. Fortinet offers strong SMB value via custom ASICs but is not cloud-native. Cisco's post-Splunk integration creates a networking-security-analytics bundle with massive channel leverage.

### Customer stickiness and pricing power

Switching costs are **very high** — Zscaler sits inline in all internet/cloud traffic flows, making replacement require full network security re-architecture. Customers on average **triple their initial purchase within four years** per CFO Kevin Rubin. The Z-Flex program (cumulative $650M TCV at 4-year average terms) further deepens lock-in. Non-seat-based metered usage now exceeds **25% of new ACV** and is growing **100%+ YoY**, creating a consumption-driven expansion wedge. While DBNRR declined from ~122% to ~114%, this partially reflects structural shifts toward larger upfront bundles rather than pure pricing weakness — Non-GAAP gross margins remain stable at **80%** with no pricing pressure noted.

## Module 10: Near-term catalysts and the risks that keep bears engaged

### Catalysts over the next two quarters

**Q3 FY2026 earnings on May 28, 2026 (confirmed)** will be the next major catalyst. Applying the historical ~2% revenue beat pattern to the $835M guidance midpoint implies potential revenue of ~$852–855M, which would represent ~26% YoY growth. EPS guidance of $1.00–1.01 implies potential ~$1.12–1.14 actual.

**Red Canary integration** entered its next phase on February 1, 2026, with teams consolidated into respective Zscaler organizations. Red Canary ARR guidance was raised to ~$130M for FY2026 (from initial $95M). Integration of agentic AI automated runbooks and threat intelligence with Zscaler's Data Fabric for Security is the key technical milestone. Forrester has flagged the absence of a public integration timeline as a risk.

**Product momentum** includes: the SquareX acquisition (closed February 2026) extending Zero Trust into browser security; an AI Agent for ZDX "soon launching" per the Q2 call; AI Protect expanding (closed-loop AI red teaming + guardrails); and Zero Trust Branch achieving **45% new logo attach rate** in Q2. Zscaler is now processing **millions of MCP (Model Context Protocol) requests monthly**.

**Government catalysts** are significant: Zscaler serves **14 of 15 US Cabinet-level agencies** with **FedRAMP High authorization** (the only SASE TIC 3.0 solution with this clearance). **CMMC 3.0** requirements become mandatory in DoD contracts by **October 1, 2026**, directly benefiting ZS government revenue. DoD IL5 authorization for ZPA and expanded FedRAMP data centers (international PoPs in Zurich, Singapore, São Paulo) create additional tailwinds.

### Key risk factors

**Competition and bundling** remains the top risk. PANW's platformization strategy and Microsoft's E5 bundling offer compelling vendor consolidation narratives. However, Zscaler's organic net new ARR acceleration from +1% to +10% in H1 FY2026 suggests the competitive environment has not materially worsened.

**SBC and GAAP profitability** are persistent concerns. At **25-27% of revenue**, SBC drives continued GAAP operating losses despite 22% Non-GAAP margins. FCF ex-SBC is only ~4-5% of revenue on an LTM basis. With no buyback program and ~3-4% annual dilution, equity holders bear the full cost.

**DBNRR discontinuation** is a transparency yellow flag. While management offered replacement metrics, the decision to stop reporting a declining metric invites skepticism. Investors should monitor **organic net new ARR growth** as the primary replacement KPI.

**Convertible note maturity** on July 15, 2028 creates a medium-term refinancing event. With the conversion price at $439.52 — more than 3x the current stock price — the notes will almost certainly require cash repayment of $1,725M. Zscaler's ~$3.5B cash position and strong FCF generation ($900M+ annually) provide ample coverage, but the refinancing will consume significant liquidity.

**Customer concentration** is minimal — no single customer exceeds 10% of revenue. **Macro sensitivity** is moderate: cybersecurity spending is more resilient than general IT, and multi-year Z-Flex contracts (4-year average) provide revenue buffer, but management noted continued deal scrutiny in Q2. **AI disruption** cuts both ways — AI expands the attack surface (driving demand) but enables competitors to build faster. Zscaler's processing of ~1 trillion AI transactions in CY2025 positions it to capture this demand.

**Valuation at current levels** appears historically compressed: ~5.3x NTM revenue and ~29x NTM EPS versus 5-year averages well above those levels. The consensus price target of ~$265 implies the market views the stock as significantly undervalued, though the 36% YTD decline reflects legitimate concerns about growth deceleration, SBC dilution, and competitive intensity that could persist.

## Conclusion

Zscaler's fundamental trajectory is stronger than the stock price suggests. **Organic net new ARR inflected to +10% growth**, AI security ARR blew past targets at $500M+, and the Z-Flex platform selling motion is gaining traction with $650M cumulative TCV. The company's SSE leadership is validated by four consecutive Gartner Leader designations and the Forrester "800-pound gorilla" characterization. The balance sheet is clean with $1.8B net cash and ample runway to repay the 2028 converts.

The bear case centers on three interlinked concerns: SBC running at 25-27% of revenue with no buyback offset renders GAAP profitability elusive and dilutes holders 3-4% annually; DBNRR discontinuation at 114% removes a key transparency metric during a competitive transition; and the PANW/Microsoft bundling narratives could structurally cap expansion rates. The Q3 FY2026 print on May 28 will be pivotal — investors need to see continued organic net new ARR acceleration and evidence of Red Canary integration progress to rebuild confidence. At ~5x NTM revenue, the market has already priced in meaningful deceleration; the question is whether Zscaler's expanding platform can re-accelerate fast enough to earn a higher multiple.
