# Cloudflare R1 company research dossier

**Cloudflare, Inc. (NYSE: NET) | Cybersecurity / Edge Security / Connectivity Cloud | FY ends December 31**

Cloudflare is a **$74B market cap connectivity cloud platform** operating a global edge network across 330+ cities, offering security, performance, and developer services. The company delivered **FY2025 revenue of $2,168M (+30% YoY)** with accelerating growth through the year, reaching **$614.5M in Q4 2025 (+34% YoY)**. At **26.4x NTM revenue**, NET trades at the highest multiple in the cybersecurity peer set, pricing in sustained ~28%+ growth and significant margin expansion. The dossier that follows populates all ten data modules required for valuation-grade analysis. Key tensions include gross margin compression (74.9% vs. 77.6% YoY in Q4), heavy SBC (~21% of revenue), a $1.29B convertible note maturing August 2026, and whether the AI/developer platform thesis can sustain growth acceleration against the premium valuation.

## Module 9: Competitive positioning snapshot

### Market position across key segments

Cloudflare holds a **dominant position in web infrastructure by adoption** — 79.9% of CDN-using websites rely on Cloudflare per W3Techs, and the company handles ~20% of all global web traffic. In DDoS protection, market share reaches **~82%** by deployed websites. Revenue-based CDN share still trails Akamai ($3.9B revenue), but Cloudflare is gaining rapidly in enterprise.

In **SASE/SSE**, Cloudflare is a newer entrant positioned as a **Gartner Visionary** (SASE Platforms MQ 2025) and named in the **Gartner SSE MQ for 3 consecutive years**. Zscaler and Netskope remain Leaders in SSE, with Fortinet leading in single-vendor SASE customer count (~1,500 vs. Cloudflare's ~400 enterprise SASE customers). However, momentum is strongly in Cloudflare's favor based on deal size expansion and government wins.

In **WAF**, Cloudflare earned the **Forrester Wave Leader** designation (Q1 2025) with the highest score in current offering — a 5.0/5.0 in 15 of 22 criteria. In **edge development platforms**, Cloudflare was also named a **Forrester Leader** (Q4 2023) with top marks.

### The architecture that creates the moat

Cloudflare's core differentiator is its **anycast, single-pass architecture** where every service runs on every server in every data center (330+ cities). This eliminates backhauling, enables sub-5ms cold starts via V8 isolates (vs. seconds for AWS Lambda@Edge), and provides a unified control plane across security, networking, and compute — something competitors built through M&A cannot replicate. The **free tier** drives massive adoption (millions of websites) that generates a **data flywheel**: more traffic improves threat intelligence, which improves the product, which attracts more customers.

### Biggest competitive threat

**Hyperscalers (AWS, Azure, GCP)** pose the greatest structural risk through bundling security with existing cloud services at low incremental cost. Their existing customer relationships and data gravity create powerful inertia. Mitigating this: Cloudflare's **multi-cloud neutrality** is increasingly valued, hyperscaler security products are generally inferior as standalone offerings, and some hyperscalers actually use Cloudflare's DDoS services for their own customers. **Zscaler** remains the most formidable pure-play competitor in Zero Trust/SSE given its deeper enterprise maturity and government relationships.

### Customer stickiness is high and increasing

Switching costs are elevated due to DNS delegation requirements, multi-product adoption surfaces, developer platform lock-in (Workers, R2, D1), and growing **"pool of funds" contracts** (low double-digit % of deals as of Q2 2025, up from <3% a year prior). These contracts commit customers to flexible spending across the Cloudflare platform, structurally increasing lock-in. The DBNRR recovery to 120% and consistently low churn confirm the stickiness thesis.

### Key risks ranked by severity

**1. Valuation risk (HIGH).** At **~27x NTM revenue** (vs. cybersecurity peer average ~13x), the stock prices in sustained 28%+ growth for years, meaningful margin expansion toward 20%+ operating margins, and successful AI/developer platform monetization. Any growth deceleration or margin disappointment could trigger significant multiple compression. Simply Wall St's DCF fair value estimate is ~$104 vs. ~$189 stock price.

**2. Gross margin compression (MODERATE-HIGH).** Non-GAAP gross margin declined from 78.7% (FY2024) to **75.8% (FY2025)**, with Q4 at 74.9% — sitting at the low end of management's 75–77% target range. Drivers include rising depreciation from network expansion and product mix shift toward Workers/AI consumption. If Workers/AI grows faster than SASE (likely), compression could persist. Management characterizes this as strategic and temporary; the trajectory warrants close monitoring.

**3. Convertible note maturity (MODERATE).** The $1.29B 2026 Notes become freely convertible May 15, 2026. At NET's current price above the $191.34 conversion price, this could result in share dilution (partially offset by capped calls). Cash is ample for retirement, but the decision between cash settlement and share conversion has dilution implications.

**4. SBC dilution (MODERATE).** Stock-based compensation was **$451.5M in FY2025 (20.8% of revenue)**, driving FCF ex-SBC to negative ($190.9M). Non-GAAP diluted shares are growing ~3.5% annually. No buyback program exists to offset this dilution.

**5. Hyperscaler competition (MODERATE).** AWS, Azure, and GCP increasingly bundle security and CDN with cloud services. Cloudflare's multi-cloud neutrality and architectural advantages provide meaningful defense, but bundle pricing pressure is a persistent threat.

**6. Customer concentration evolving (LOW-MODERATE).** No single customer exceeds 10% of revenue, but $100K+ customers now generate **73% of revenue** (up from 69% a year ago). The loss of multiple large contracts would be impactful.

**7. Macro sensitivity (LOW).** Revenue growth accelerated through 2025 despite macro uncertainty. The subscription model and free-tier funnel provide resilience. However, IT spending slowdowns could affect enterprise expansion deals.

## Conclusion

Cloudflare's data profile presents a company in rare form: **accelerating revenue growth** (26.5% → 33.6% through 2025), **re-inflecting DBNRR** (110% trough → 120%), **record customer additions**, and a **Rule of 40 score of 50** in Q4 2025. The $5B revenue run rate target by Q4 2028 appears achievable. Management's guidance track record (8 consecutive revenue beats with widening magnitude) is among the best in software.

The central investment tension is clear. The **~27x NTM revenue multiple** embeds extraordinary expectations — essentially requiring flawless execution on the AI/developer platform thesis while simultaneously expanding margins toward 20%+ operating and 25%+ FCF. Against this, gross margin compression (74.9% and falling), SBC at 21% of revenue yielding negative FCF ex-SBC, and a $1.29B convertible maturity in August 2026 create tangible near-term friction. The architecture is genuinely differentiated (single-pass anycast, V8 isolates, 330+ city network), the competitive moat is deepening through multi-product stickiness and developer lock-in, and the AI tailwind (4,000% inference growth) is real — but the stock must be modeled with discipline given the premium. The sector handoff note stands: **extreme valuation requires discipline on entry.**
