# CrowdStrike Holdings R1 Company Research Dossier

**CrowdStrike (NASDAQ: CRWD) achieved its strongest fiscal year ever in FY2026, posting $5.25B in ending ARR and a record $330.7M in Q4 net new ARR, firmly establishing itself as the first pure-play cybersecurity company to surpass $5B ARR.** The company's 97% gross retention rate held rock-steady through the July 2024 outage recovery, while net revenue retention reaccelerated to 115% by Q4 FY2026 — a counter-cyclical inflection at scale. FY2027 guidance calls for ~22% revenue growth to $5.87–5.93B with a target operating model path to 28–32% non-GAAP operating margins by FY2029. The critical valuation tension: **reported FCF margins of 26% look strong, but SBC-adjusted FCF margin has deteriorated to just 2.9%** — a gap that must narrow for the current ~15x EV/NTM Revenue multiple to hold. This dossier covers every metric needed for DCF, reverse DCF, trading comps, M&A comps, sum-of-parts, and LBO modeling.

### Discontinued metrics (important for modeling)

CrowdStrike **stopped disclosing total subscription customer count** starting Q1 FY2024. The last reported figure was **23,019 customers as of January 31, 2023**. Additionally, **5+ module adoption rate was dropped** from reporting starting FY2026 (last reported at 67% in Q4 FY2025). These shifts toward reporting platform-level metrics (modules, Flex) rather than customer counts suggest the company views per-customer metrics as less representative of its land-and-expand motion.

Fourth, **Falcon Flex contracts** averaging 31–35 months with built-in expansion incentives create multi-year commercial lock-in — 23% of Flex customers have already expanded ("reflexed") within months. Fifth, the **Enterprise Graph data layer** accumulates historical security telemetry that compounds in value over time.

CrowdStrike's fundamental trajectory is unambiguous — **the company is executing at an elite level**. Record net new ARR, reaccelerating NRR, stable 97% GRR, 50% on 6+ modules, and $9B RPO growing 38% all point to durable demand acceleration. The July 2024 outage is effectively behind the company, with CCP recipients actually showing above-average retention. The Falcon Flex model ($1.69B ARR, +120%) is transforming customer economics and deepening switching costs.

The critical analytical tension for any financial model is the **gap between reported FCF (26% margin, strong) and SBC-adjusted FCF (2.9% margin, alarming)**. This gap must narrow for the current premium to be justified. Management's FY2029 target of **34–38% FCF margin** combined with natural SBC leverage as the company scales toward $10B+ ARR provides a plausible path — but the bridge from 2.9% SBC-adjusted margins to true economic value creation remains the key variable. The $949M buyback capacity and decelerating dilution (3.2% → 2.0% → 1.4% guided) suggest management is aware of and addressing this concern.

For modeling purposes, the most decision-relevant data points are: **FY2027 revenue guidance of $5.90B at the midpoint (22% growth), FY2029 target operating model (28–32% non-GAAP operating margin, 34–38% FCF margin), $4.48B net cash, 260M guided diluted shares, and SBC at 22.8% of revenue trending downward as a percentage**. The stock's ~$370 price implies the market is pricing in sustained 20%+ growth for several years but discounting margin expansion — any evidence of operating leverage (Q4 FY2026's 25% non-GAAP margin was a positive signal) could drive rerating, while SBC stagnation or growth deceleration would justify further compression.
