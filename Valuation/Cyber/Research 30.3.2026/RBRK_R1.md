# Rubrik, Inc. (RBRK) — comprehensive company research dossier

**Rubrik reached a pivotal inflection point in Q4 FY2026, posting its first quarter of non-GAAP profitability ($0.04 EPS) while sustaining 46% revenue growth to $377.7M.** The company's transition from legacy data backup to cloud-native cyber resilience is nearly complete, with cloud ARR now **88% of subscription ARR** and growing 48% YoY. At ~$45/share (March 29, 2026), the stock trades at **~5.6x NTM revenue** — roughly half its post-IPO peak — despite accelerating free cash flow ($238M in FY2026, guided $265–275M in FY2027) and a Rule of 40 score of **66%**. This dossier covers all 10 modules with data sourced primarily from SEC 8-K filings, 10-K/10-Q annual and quarterly reports, and cross-referenced against earnings transcripts and financial data aggregators.

## Module 9: Competitive positioning and market dynamics

### Where Rubrik sits in the market

Rubrik was ranked **7th in the data protection market** by IDC in H1 2024, with an estimated **~14% market share** per RBC Capital Markets. The market remains highly fragmented. The most significant structural change is the **Cohesity-Veritas merger**, which created the market's largest player at ~19% share with $1.5B ARR and 12,000+ customers. Cohesity is planning a **2026 IPO** with NVIDIA backing.

Despite the competitive intensification, Rubrik reported **>90% competitive win rates** in Q4 FY2026 and channel feedback indicates Rubrik has the "leading brand and most market momentum." The Gartner Magic Quadrant for Backup & Data Protection (June 2025) positioned Rubrik as a **Leader for the 6th consecutive year** and **furthest in Completeness of Vision**. However, Forrester's Q4 2024 Data Resilience Solutions Wave ranked Rubrik as a Strong Performer (below Leaders Commvault, Cohesity, and Veeam), highlighting methodology differences between analysts.

### Rubrik's competitive moat

The company's core differentiator is its **zero-trust architecture with an immutable file system** that creates a native logical air gap — backups cannot be modified or deleted even if the network is compromised. This security-first approach distinguishes Rubrik from traditional backup vendors. Additional moat elements include the cloud-native SaaS platform (88% cloud ARR), AI-driven anomaly detection, the expanding identity resilience product line (900+ customers), and the **$10M ransomware recovery warranty** (never paid). The Predibase acquisition and Annapurna RAG platform position Rubrik for the emerging AI data governance opportunity.

### Switching costs and pricing power

Customer switching costs are **high**. Enterprise backup systems are deeply embedded in IT infrastructure — migration requires reprotecting all workloads, and backup data cannot be easily ported between platforms. Multi-year subscription contracts create contractual lock-in, and the expanding platform (security modules on top of backup) increases switching costs over time. Pricing evidence is strong: NRR consistently >120%, **non-GAAP gross margin expanded 400bps YoY to 83.7%** in Q4, and Rubrik reportedly commands a premium (~$650/TB vs. ~$500/TB for Cohesity). The $1M+ ARR customer cohort grew **>50% YoY**, indicating successful enterprise upselling.

### TAM

The cyber resilience / data resilience market is estimated at **$34.4B (2025)** growing to **$79.1B by 2030** (~18% CAGR) per 360iResearch. Rubrik's management sizes the total addressable opportunity at **$50B+** when including adjacent data security, DSPM, and AI governance markets.

## Module 10: Risk factors and catalysts

### Near-term catalysts (next two quarters)

**Q1 FY2027 earnings** are expected **June 16, 2026** (after close). Given the consistent 7–11% revenue beat pattern, the Street expects actual Q1 revenue of ~$375–385M vs. management's $365–367M guide. **FY2027 profitability inflection** is the most significant catalyst — management guided $0.07–$0.27 non-GAAP EPS, which would mark the first full year of profitability. Product catalysts include Rubrik Agent Cloud (moved from beta to GA with Fortune 500 POCs), the Predibase AI integration, Okta Recovery, and Rubrik Security Cloud Sovereign for regulated industries. **FedRAMP Moderate Authorization** (already achieved) opens the **$9.9B federal cybersecurity market**. Index inclusion (Russell 1000 or sector ETFs) becomes more likely as profitability materializes.

### Key risks assessed

**Competition from Cohesity-Veritas (HIGH risk)**: The merged entity holds 19% market share with $1.5B ARR and NVIDIA backing, and is planning a 2026 IPO. However, combined growth decelerated to mid-teens and integration risk is real. Rubrik's 90%+ win rates suggest no immediate share loss, but this remains the most significant competitive threat.

**Convertible debt and dilution (LOW-MODERATE risk)**: The $1.15B in zero-coupon convertible notes matures June 2030 — no near-term liquidity risk. The conversion price of ~$125/share is far above the current ~$45 stock price, making conversion unlikely near-term. Capped calls further mitigate dilution. The company holds $1.68B in cash/investments, more than covering the debt. Annual equity dilution of ~5.4% is meaningful but typical for high-growth software.

**Profitability dependency on growth (MODERATE risk)**: GAAP profitability remains elusive ($348.8M net loss in FY2026). Non-GAAP profitability is emerging but depends on sustaining high growth to generate operating leverage. SBC at 25% of revenue is still elevated. FCF ex-SBC remains deeply negative at ($91.6M).

**Macro and IT spending sensitivity (LOW-MODERATE risk)**: Cybersecurity is generally recession-resistant, but Rubrik's premium pricing and enterprise focus mean net new logo acquisition could slow in downturns. The stock has already declined ~57% from its 52-week high of $103, partly reflecting macro/tariff fears.

**Customer concentration (LOW risk)**: No single customer represents >10% of revenue. The business is diversified across 2,805 customers with >$100K ARR.

**Geographic concentration (MODERATE risk)**: Americas represent ~73% of revenue. International is growing but remains a smaller contributor.

**Leadership transition (MODERATE risk)**: CRO Brian McCarthy departed; Jesse Green was promoted to CRO effective immediately. Founder-CEO Bipul Sinha remains. The dual-class structure concentrates voting control.

**Short interest**: 13.0M shares (8.8% of float) — elevated but not extreme.

### Material rights revenue headwind

An underappreciated risk for FY2027 optics: FY2026 included approximately **~$52M in material rights revenue** from customers exercising or forfeiting subscription credits during the cloud transition. This revenue stream drops to ~$4M in Q1 FY2027 and becomes minimal thereafter. This creates a **~400bps headwind** to reported YoY revenue growth in FY2027 (21–22% reported vs. 27–28% normalized). Investors must adjust for this when comparing growth rates.

## Conclusion: what the numbers reveal

Rubrik's financial trajectory tells a clear story of a company transitioning from high-burn growth to durable, profitable scale. The **Rule of 40 score of 66%**, non-GAAP profitability inflection, and **$238M in FCF** in FY2026 mark genuine operational maturation. The cloud transition is nearly complete at 88% cloud ARR, and the expanding security product suite — now contributing 45% of NRR — creates a powerful land-and-expand flywheel.

Three data points stand out from this analysis. First, management's guidance conservatism is remarkably consistent — five consecutive quarters of 7–11% revenue beats create a high floor under FY2027 expectations, with the Street already pricing in $1.64B vs. the $1.60B guide. Second, the **total deferred revenue of $1.85B** (140% of trailing revenue) provides extraordinary forward visibility. Third, the stock's decline to **~5.6x NTM revenue** — down from ~15x at its 2025 highs — creates a historically wide gap between the average analyst target (~$89) and the current price (~$45), suggesting either meaningful upside or structural concerns about decelerating growth that analysts have not yet reflected.

The biggest open question is whether Rubrik can sustain **25%+ ARR growth** as the base scales past $1.5B while fending off a newly consolidated Cohesity-Veritas. The identity product's explosive growth (900 customers, doubling in three quarters) and AI data governance positioning via Predibase provide potential growth vectors, but these are nascent. For FY2027, the key milestones to monitor are whether normalized revenue growth holds above 25%, whether contribution margins expand to the guided 13%, and whether the CRO transition disrupts sales execution.
