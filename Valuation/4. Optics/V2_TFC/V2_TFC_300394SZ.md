## METHOD 1: REVERSE DCF

**Purpose:** What growth rate is the market pricing in?

**Assumptions:** Terminal FCF margin 22% (sector handoff: 15–25% for component companies; TFC's premium margins justify upper range), terminal growth 3%, WACC 12% (elevated from 10% SaaS default due to: China A-share risk premium, beta 2.16, geopolitical exposure, single-customer concentration, ChiNext retail sentiment volatility).

**WACC Rationale:** TFC's risk profile warrants 12%, not the standard 10%: (1) 62% revenue concentration on Fabrinet/NVIDIA supply chain, (2) ~80% overseas revenue creates currency and geopolitical exposure, (3) ChiNext listing on Chinese market with 2.16 beta, (4) cyclical sector with historical boom/bust patterns. Range-testing at 10–13%.

### Results

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Current EV | CNY 257,787M |
| Base Revenue (FY2025E) | CNY 5,300M |
| **Implied 10-Year Revenue CAGR** | **46.1%** |

| Consensus FY2026E growth | +74% |
| Consensus FY2027E growth | +29% |
| Sector through-cycle growth | 15–25% |

### Gap Analysis

The market is pricing in a **46% revenue CAGR sustained for 10 years**. This means TFC must grow from CNY 5.3B to approximately CNY 200B (~$28B) in revenue by 2035. For context, the entire global datacom optical component TAM today is ~$18B. TFC would need to capture roughly **15% of the total global optical component market** at maturity — starting from <0.5% today — while sustaining growth rates that consensus already expects to halve by FY2027E (from 74% to 29%).

The implied growth rate is roughly 60% *above* FY2027E consensus. **The market is pricing in a decade of sustained hypergrowth that sell-side analysts do not model beyond Year 2.** This is the single most damning mispricing signal.

### Why Methods Diverge (and Why the Divergence Reinforces the Conclusion)

The Forward DCF produces the lowest values (¥30–134 range) because discounting future cash flows at an appropriate risk-adjusted rate (12% WACC) dramatically compresses the present value of TFC's expected but distant profitability. The Trading Comps produce higher values (¥146–217) because the entire Chinese optical sector is trading at historically extreme multiples during this AI supercycle — comps anchor to a market-wide overvaluation. The fact that even sector-relative comps show 35–56% downside, while fundamentals-based DCF shows 60–91% downside, suggests **both the company and its sector peers are overvalued, but TFC is the most extreme.**

### Weighting Rationale

Trading Comps weighted at 30% because the peer set is well-defined (Chinese optical component companies at similar growth/margin profiles), though the sector itself may be overvalued, warranting a slight discount. Sum-of-Parts at 15% because TFC has a meaningful two-segment structure (passive vs. active) with different growth profiles and appropriate comparables. PE/LBO at 10% as a theoretical floor — unrealistic for this company type but informative. M&A at 5% due to very low takeout probability.
