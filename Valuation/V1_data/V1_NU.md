# Nu Holdings Ltd. (NYSE: NU) — R1 Company Research Dossier

**Bottom Line Up Front:** Nu Holdings is the world's largest digital banking platform by customers (**131 million** across Brazil, Mexico, and Colombia), generating **$16.3 billion in managerial revenue** in FY2025 (+45% YoY) with **$2.87 billion in net income** (+46% YoY) and a **33% ROE** in Q4 2025. The company operates at a **19.9% efficiency ratio** — roughly one-third of incumbent Brazilian banks — while still investing aggressively in Mexico, Colombia, and a nascent US expansion via OCC conditional bank charter approval (January 2026). Trading at ~$14–15 vs. consensus price targets of $18–21, the stock reflects FX/LatAm macro discount rather than fundamental deterioration. This dossier provides full data across all 10 modules for institutional valuation modeling.

**Reporting Basis:** IFRS in USD. Fiscal year ends December 31. In Q4 2025, Nu introduced a "Managerial P&L" framework that reclassifies certain IFRS line items (primarily grossing up revenue by ~3–4% and recharacterizing operating expenses); net income is identical under both frameworks. Both IFRS and managerial figures are provided below where relevant. All figures in USD thousands unless otherwise noted.

## Module 5: No forward guidance, but management signals ambitious long-term trajectory

### Confirmed: Nu does NOT provide forward revenue or EPS guidance

This is explicitly confirmed. CFO Guilherme Lago stated on the Q3 2025 call: *"That is one of the reasons why we are so hesitant to provide kind of guidance on both top-line as well as cost of risk."* CEO David Vélez reinforced: *"I would not guide in any way or form, as we have not done in the past, on kind of the P&L or net income."* **No formal Investor Day or Analyst Day has been held.**

### Long-Term Strategic Targets (from earnings calls)

Management frames strategic ambition qualitatively rather than numerically. Key directional targets from Q3'25 and Q4'25 earnings calls include the following. On **ROE**, management has delivered 28–33% over recent quarters while still investing heavily, implying **25%+ is a floor rather than a ceiling**. Adjusted ROE hit **35% in Q4'25**. On **efficiency**, the ratio reached a record **19.9%** in Q4'25, but CFO Lago cautioned that *"2026 is an investment year"* that will "likely put upward pressure on the efficiency ratio in the near term" while "structural drivers of operating leverage remain unchanged." On **ARPAC**, mature cohorts (8+ years) generate **~$27/month** vs. the current $15 average — management views the gap as embedded monetization runway without needing new customer adds. On **Mexico**, CEO Vélez characterized 2026 as an *"inflection year — the year we begin transitioning from a Latin American leader to a global digital banking platform."* No specific Mexico profitability date has been guided. On the **US charter**, the 12-month capitalization deadline implies full capitalization by ~January 2027, with the bank opening by ~July 2027. Cristina Junqueira is CEO of the US entity; Roberto Campos Neto (former BCB governor) chairs the US board. Rule of 40 or similar framework references: [NOT FOUND].

## Conclusion: The monetization story is underpriced relative to the structural opportunity

This dossier reveals a company at a distinctive inflection point. Nu has achieved extraordinary scale (**131M customers, $74.9B assets**) while maintaining operational efficiency that incumbent banks cannot replicate (**19.9% efficiency ratio**, **$0.80 cost to serve**, **~$19 CAC**). The ARPAC trajectory from $15 to $27+ in mature cohorts represents a **multi-year embedded revenue growth engine** independent of customer acquisition. Three non-obvious insights emerge from the data.

First, the managerial P&L framework introduced in Q4'25 is strategically significant — the ~$545M annual difference between IFRS and managerial revenue (3.4% of revenue) reflects tax-equivalency adjustments that make cross-border comparisons more intuitive, signaling management's growing sophistication in communicating with global institutional investors.

Second, the risk-adjusted NIM recovery from 8.2% (Q1'25 trough) to 10.5% (Q4'25) occurred *during a period of rising Selic rates*, suggesting Nu's pricing power and portfolio mix improvements can offset macro headwinds — a non-obvious positive for the upcoming rate-cutting cycle.

Third, the US charter represents an optionality call that is effectively unpriced at $14/share. With Cristina Junqueira as CEO, Roberto Campos Neto (former BCB governor) as board chair, and a July 2027 opening deadline, this is the most credible neobank US entry attempt to date — targeting deposits, credit cards, lending, and notably **digital asset custody**. The key modeling question for valuation is whether Nu's Brazilian playbook (land with savings/debit → credit card → lending → insurance → investments) can replicate in the world's largest financial services market.
