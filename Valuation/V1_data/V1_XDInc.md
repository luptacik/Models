# XD Inc V1 Company Research Dossier (2400.HK)

**Ticker:** 2400.HK | **Reporting Currency:** RMB (CNY) | **FY End:** December 31 | **Compiled:** April 5, 2026

XD Inc posted **FY2025 revenue of CNY 5,764M (+15% YoY)** and **net profit attributable to shareholders of CNY 1,535M (+89% YoY)**, beating its own guidance on both metrics. The company's dual-engine model — TapTap platform advertising and self-developed game operations — delivered dramatic margin expansion, with diluted EPS of **CNY 3.07** (vs. CNY 1.69 in FY2024). Yet underneath the headline strength lies a divergence: TapTap China's ARPU surged while its MAU growth stalled at low single digits, and online game MAU/MPU both declined over **20% YoY** in FY2025. The stock trades at roughly **19x trailing P/E** with zero debt, **~CNY 3.5–4B net cash**, and a **HK$400M buyback** executing through June 2026. The critical question for valuation is whether the Ragnarok 2 launch in mid-2026 can reignite the growth cycle or whether this is peak margin on a shrinking user base.

**Sector Scorecard (from completed sector analysis):** 18/25 — Sub-Sector Tailwind 4, Competitive Moat 3, Business Model Quality 4, Management Execution 4, Relative Valuation 3.

### Market position in Chinese mobile game distribution

TapTap operates in a market where Google Play is unavailable, creating a fragmented Android distribution landscape dominated by OEM pre-installed stores. TapTap's **44M MAU** makes it a meaningful but niche player relative to OEM stores with 300M+ MAU each.

| OPPO Software Store | ~19–25% | 50% commission | ~350M |
| Vivo App Store | ~15–20% | 50% commission | ~300M |
| Tencent MyApp | ~13–16% | 50% commission | ~172–400M |
| Xiaomi App Store | ~12% | 50% commission | — |

### The TapTap moat: three pillars of differentiation

TapTap's positioning as "China's Steam for mobile" rests on three reinforcing advantages. First, **zero commission** — developers keep 100% of game revenue versus the 50% "channel tax" at OEM stores, making TapTap the most economically attractive distribution channel. Second, **community trust** — TapTap's user ratings are considered the most authoritative in China's mobile gaming industry (per Frost & Sullivan), creating an information monopoly that drives discovery. Third, **platform neutrality** — TapTap explicitly promises not to jointly operate any games, maintaining independence that contrasts with Tencent, which both distributes and competes with third-party developers.

### Customer switching costs

**For gamers: MODERATE.** Users accumulate game libraries, social connections, community reputation, and personalized recommendation graphs. However, games can be downloaded from multiple stores — TapTap doesn't lock in game ownership the way Steam does for PC. Community engagement creates emotional, not contractual, switching costs.

**For developers: MODERATE-HIGH.** The zero-commission model creates a powerful financial incentive (50% more revenue per user). TapSDK integration for accounts, data, and community management creates technical switching costs. Accumulated user reviews and pre-registration campaigns are platform-specific assets.

### Pricing power evidence

TapTap ad revenue per user grew **+36% YoY and +23% QoQ** in H1 2025 despite maintaining only a single primary ad position on the homepage. This demonstrates strong demand-driven pricing power — advertisers are bidding up rates because TapTap's users have high gaming intent and conversion rates. The **83.6% TapTap gross margin** is among the highest in the Chinese digital advertising market.

However, TapTap ad revenue growth decelerated from **+38% in H1 2025 to ~+13% in H2 2025** (implied from full-year data), raising early questions about whether a monetization ceiling is approaching. ⚠️

### Biggest competitive threats

The most acute threat is **commission compression** — if OEM stores reduce their 50% commission rates (following global Apple/Google trends), TapTap's zero-commission value proposition narrows proportionally. Additionally, **Tencent** controls ~60% of China's gaming market and operates its own distribution; any strategic move by Tencent into community-driven distribution would be highly disruptive. Huawei's **HarmonyOS NEXT** (launched October 2024) creates a new non-Android ecosystem where TapTap must adapt.

## Conclusion: what the data reveals about the investment case

Three findings stand out from this dossier that are not obvious from headline numbers alone.

**The H2 2025 revenue cliff is the critical signal.** Revenue fell ~4% YoY in H2 2025 after growing 39% in H1, meaning virtually all of FY2025's reported 15% full-year growth was loaded into H1. This creates a challenging base-effect setup for H1 2026 and makes the **Ragnarok 2 launch timing** (expected mid-2026) the single most important variable for near-term estimates. If Ragnarok 2 slips to H2 2026 or underperforms, the FY2026 consensus revenue estimate of CNY 6.55B (+14%) may prove too optimistic.

**The margin story is real but partially one-time.** The jump from 16.2% net margin (FY2024) to 26.6% (FY2025) was driven by operating leverage on a fixed-cost base and a shift toward higher-margin self-developed titles. The **30.6% FCF margin** is exceptionally strong for a gaming company. However, some of this margin expansion reflects reduced marketing spend as older titles entered maintenance mode — the next launch cycle will likely require renewed S&M investment, potentially compressing margins temporarily.

**TapTap's monetization efficiency is underappreciated.** Ad revenue per user grew **36% YoY** in H1 2025 while MAU was essentially flat (+0.9%). This decoupling of revenue growth from user growth is the hallmark of a maturing platform with pricing power. With an **83.6% gross margin** on the TapTap segment and only a single ad position, there is significant room to increase ad load if needed — though the deceleration to ~13% growth in H2 2025 warrants monitoring.

The balance sheet provides a significant margin of safety: **zero debt, ~CNY 3.5–4B net cash** (~10–12% of market cap), **1.2% SBC dilution**, and a **HK$400M buyback** actively executing with share cancellation. At **~14x FY2026E earnings** (using CNY 3.98 EPS consensus), the stock prices in moderate growth with limited downside from the cash cushion — but also limited upside unless Ragnarok 2 delivers a convincing hit.
