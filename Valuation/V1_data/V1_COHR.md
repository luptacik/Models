# Coherent Corp (COHR) — Valuation-Grade R1 Research Dossier

**Prepared April 5, 2026 | Fiscal Year Ends June 30 | Most Recent Quarter: FQ2 FY2026 (Dec 2025)**

Coherent Corp is the broadest-platform optical and photonics company in the AI infrastructure supply chain, posting **$1.69B in FQ2 FY2026 revenue** (+17.5% YoY) with accelerating datacenter demand now comprising **72% of total revenue**. The company's strategic positioning was validated by NVIDIA's **$2B common stock investment** on March 2, 2026, accompanied by a multibillion-dollar purchase commitment. Coherent entered the S&P 500 effective March 23, 2026. FY2025 full-year revenue reached **$5.81B**, and consensus estimates project **$7.07B for FY2026** (+22% YoY) with non-GAAP EPS of **$5.45**. The company has beaten guidance on both revenue and EPS for four consecutive quarters, with an average revenue beat of 3.3% and EPS beat of 9.3%.

## MODULE 4: Key operating metrics and technology KPIs

### Datacenter & Communications — the growth engine

**Datacenter & Communications revenue reached $1,208M in FQ2 FY2026** (+33.5% YoY, +10.8% QoQ), now representing 71.7% of total revenue, up from 63.1% a year ago. Within this segment, data center revenue alone grew **+36% YoY** and **+14% QoQ**, while communications grew **+44% YoY** and **+9% QoQ**. Management expects double-digit sequential growth in both FQ3 and FQ4 FY2026, and has stated that **FY2027 revenue growth rate is expected to exceed FY2026's growth rate**.

### Technology and product metrics

**800G Transceivers:** Remain the largest portion of datacenter revenue. Revenue grew in FQ2 and is expected to grow YoY through CY2026. Strong bookings continuing. Company does not break out revenue by data rate. ⚠️

**1.6T Transceivers:** Ramping across multiple customers using both EML-based and SiPh-based designs. **200G VCSEL-based 1.6T** expected to ramp in H2 CY2026. 1.6T gross margins are expected to be **higher than 800G** due to higher ASPs and early lifecycle premium. "Tremendous amount of orders" providing forward visibility.

**3.2T and Beyond:** Demonstrated at OFC 2026 (March 2026). 400G/lane optical links using both EML and SiPh PIC. Founding member of **XPO MSA** (12.8T liquid-cooled optics, 204.8T per rack unit).

**Co-Packaged Optics (CPO):** Received an "exceptionally large purchase order" from a market-leading AI datacenter customer. Based on new high-power CW laser produced on 6-inch InP. Initial revenue expected **late CY2026**, more meaningful in **CY2027+**. Management believes scale-up CPO opportunity will "dwarf" scale-out and will be "orders of magnitude larger."

**InP Wafer/Laser Capacity:** 6-inch wafer starts at **80% of target** to double internal InP capacity by Q4 CY2026 (ahead of schedule). Wafer starts **more than quadrupled** from Sep to Dec quarter. Production at Sherman, TX and Järfälla, Sweden. 6-inch yields **exceed** 3-inch production yields. Cost advantage: 6-inch produces **>4x chips at <½ the cost** vs 3-inch. By end CY2026, ~50% of internal capacity will be 6-inch.

**Optical Circuit Switch (OCS):** Backlog grew sequentially. **>10 customer engagements** (up from ~7). Majority of backlog toward 320×320 systems. Revenue growing sequentially through CY2026-2027. Addressable market: **>$2B** (management says this likely understates the opportunity).

### Order book and visibility

**Data center book-to-bill exceeded 4x in FQ2 FY2026** — a step function increase from prior quarter. Most of CY2026 is booked out. CY2027 "filling very, very quickly." Some customer forecasts extend into CY2028. CEO Jim Anderson: "Visibility of the business is the best it's ever been." Multiple long-term supply agreements with financial commitments (including CapEx investment) from customers. No specific dollar backlog figure is disclosed. ⚠️

## MODULE 9: Competitive positioning snapshot

### Market share across primary product categories

**Optical Transceivers (Pluggable):** Coherent holds an estimated **20-25% overall market share**, making it one of the top 3-4 suppliers globally alongside Innolight, Broadcom, and Lumentum. The global optical transceiver market reached ~$15.6B in 2025 and is projected to grow to $25B by 2029 (13% CAGR). For NVIDIA's 800G GB200 orders specifically, Chinese vendors (Innolight + Eoptolink) hold ~60% share, with Western vendors (Coherent, Lumentum, Broadcom) at ~40%. This ratio may shift favorably for Coherent with 1.6T.

**InP Lasers/EMLs:** Coherent is the **volume leader** in InP-based components for transceivers. Lumentum is the specialized performance leader in 200G EMLs. The industry faces persistent InP EML supply constraints — Lumentum expects **double-digit price increases on 200G EMLs in 2026** due to lack of second sources. Coherent's 6-inch InP ramp in Sherman, TX is a significant competitive moat.

**Silicon Photonics:** Coherent is vertically integrated, using SiPh in its own transceiver products and named as NVIDIA's collaborator on Spectrum-X silicon photonics. It is not primarily a merchant SiPh foundry. Merchant leaders include Tower Semiconductor, GlobalFoundries, and TSMC.

### Coherent's multi-platform advantage is genuinely unique

Coherent is the **only company producing InP lasers, SiPh components, VCSELs, AND transceiver modules** — enabling it to offer all three types of 1.6T transceivers (SiPh, EML, VCSEL). The 6-inch InP manufacturing transition produces **>4x chips at <½ the cost** versus 3-inch, creating a structural cost advantage that competitors like Lumentum are racing to match. The U.S. manufacturing base is increasingly favored for "Made in America" AI infrastructure supply chains. The NVIDIA $2B investment + multibillion-dollar purchase commitment creates locked-in demand visibility extending through CY2027-2028.

### Samsung SiPh foundry entry — overstated near-term threat

Samsung announced silicon photonics foundry "production readiness" at OFC 2026, with 224 Gbps/lane modulator performance on 300mm wafers. However, the timeline is critically important: **mass production targets 2028**, with turnkey CPO services in 2029. Near-term earnings impact is minimal. Samsung initially targets PICs, not lasers — Coherent's InP laser business is not directly threatened. Industry analyst consensus: "The near-term earnings impact across the optical complex is being overstated. A broader photonic-engine ecosystem can actually be a near-term tailwind, since more optical engine activity pulls demand for high-performance light sources."

### Customer switching costs and pricing power

Switching costs are **high for InP lasers** (6-12 month qualification cycles, limited suppliers, capacity-constrained market). Moderate for transceiver modules (more commoditized but still require hyperscaler qualification). The NVIDIA strategic lockup creates multi-year purchase commitments with pricing stability. InP supply constraints actually **support pricing power** industry-wide. Net ASP dynamic is mixed: 800G faces persistent erosion from Chinese vendor competition, but 1.6T commands premium ASPs and the mix shift is favorable.

## MODULE 10: Risk factors and near-term catalysts

### Catalysts — next two quarters

**FQ3 FY2026 Earnings (~May 6-13, 2026):** Guidance of $1.70B-$1.84B was above prior Street consensus (~$1.64B). Given the consistent 3.3% average revenue beat pattern, actual results likely at or above the high end of the range.

**NVIDIA Investment Completed:** $2B in cash received March 2, 2026. Multibillion-dollar purchase commitment provides demand visibility extending multiple years. Funds accelerate R&D and U.S. capacity expansion.

**S&P 500 Passive Buying:** Inclusion effective March 23, 2026. Mandatory index fund purchases largely complete by now (April 5). Coherent joined alongside Vertiv, Lumentum, and EchoStar.

**Industrial Recovery:** Management expects improving demand throughout FY2026-27, driven by OLED display equipment, semiconductor capex, and thermal materials for AI datacenter cooling. Apple multiyear partnership revenue begins H2 CY2026.

### Risks that warrant monitoring

**Customer Concentration / NVIDIA Dependency:** Datacenter & Communications is **72% of revenue** and accelerating. NVIDIA is now simultaneously a strategic investor, multi-billion-dollar customer, and development partner. Any slowdown in NVIDIA's GPU infrastructure buildout directly impacts Coherent. Hyperscaler capex concentration means a plateau in AI spending would have severe effects.

**800G ASP Erosion:** Chinese vendors (Innolight, Eoptolink) hold ~60% of NVIDIA's 800G orders and compete aggressively on price. Persistent ASP decline is partially mitigated by 1.6T mix shift and InP supply constraints, but risk intensifies if supply catches demand.

**Insider Selling Pattern:** 19 insider sales / 0 purchases over 6 months is a notable data point. However, most transactions are under pre-arranged 10b5-1 plans, total dollars are modest relative to market cap (~0.07%), and the CEO has not sold a single share while making open-market purchases.

**Cycle Risk:** The AI networking upcycle is approximately 18 months old. Management cites "unprecedented visibility" with bookings through CY2027-2028, and datacom optical spending grew ~60% YoY in 2025. However, extraordinary growth rates carry inherent reversion risk. The >4x book-to-bill may partially reflect over-ordering/double-booking.

**Tariff/Trade War Exposure:** Management states tariffs "did not materially impact FQ1 and may provide competitive advantage for U.S. manufacturing." Chinese competitors are shifting production to Thailand. Any Section 232 tariffs on optical components could disrupt supply chains but would likely benefit Coherent's U.S. manufacturing footprint. Net assessment: **moderate risk, potential net positive**.

**Samsung SiPh Competition:** Low near-term impact (mass production target 2028). Longer-term, if Samsung vertically integrates the full optical stack, it could compress the value chain. But Samsung's entry validates the strategic importance of silicon photonics.

**Valuation:** At ~$250, the stock trades at roughly 46x FY2026E non-GAAP EPS ($5.45) and ~33x FY2027E ($7.47). It is above the average analyst target (~$214) though below the Street high ($375). Beta of 1.83 means high sensitivity to any growth deceleration.

**Integration / Portfolio Risk:** Largely mitigated. CEO Jim Anderson has exited 33 sites, completed A&D divestiture ($400M to Advent, Sept 2025 ✓), and Munich division sale (to Bystronic, Jan 2026 ✓). Additional non-core divestitures may follow. ERP consolidation expected complete by end FY2026.

## Conclusion

Coherent Corp has transformed from a diversified photonics conglomerate into a focused AI-infrastructure powerhouse under CEO Jim Anderson. The numbers tell a clear story: **Datacenter & Communications revenue has grown from $905M to $1,208M in just four quarters**, book-to-bill exceeds 4x, and the backlog extends through CY2027. The NVIDIA $2B common stock investment at $256.80/share — with an accompanying multibillion-dollar purchase commitment — is a powerful validation of Coherent's technology platform and demand trajectory.

The financial profile is improving but not yet mature: non-GAAP operating margins of **19.9%** are tracking toward the >24% long-term target, FCF was negative in H1 FY2026 due to massive working capital and CapEx investment, and net debt remains at **$2.5B** (before the $2B NVIDIA cash infusion). The 6-inch InP manufacturing ramp — producing >4x chips at <½ the cost — is the company's most defensible competitive moat and is proceeding ahead of schedule.

Key uncertainties center on cycle duration (is >4x book-to-bill sustainable or does it reflect over-ordering?), the pace of 800G ASP erosion versus 1.6T mix shift, and the extreme concentration of growth in a single end market (AI datacenter) and a single strategic partner (NVIDIA). The stock's current valuation at ~46x forward earnings prices in substantial continued execution.
