# TFC Optical (300394.SZ): R1 equity research data dossier

**Suzhou TFC Optical Communication Co., Ltd. (苏州天孚光通信股份有限公司)** is a debt-free, high-margin "pick and shovel" supplier of precision optical components and engines riding the AI connectivity supercycle. FY2024 revenue hit **CNY 3.25B (+68% YoY)** with a **57% gross margin** and **41% net margin**, and FY2025 preliminary net income of **CNY 1.88–2.15B (+40–60% YoY)** was released January 21, 2026. The stock surged ~414% in 2025 and reached an all-time high of **¥389** in early March 2026 before correcting ~20%, trading well above the consensus analyst target of ~¥250. The company is **debt-free with CNY 2.7B net cash**, derives **62% of revenue from a single customer (Fabrinet/NVIDIA supply chain)**, and is ramping a Thailand factory toward full production in 2026 while positioning as a first-tier CPO supplier for NVIDIA. The FY2025 annual report is expected **April 23, 2026**, and an **H-share (Hong Kong) dual listing** was approved by shareholders on April 2, 2026.

*Fiscal year ends December 31. Currency: CNY. Reporting standard: Chinese GAAP (ASBE). All figures in CNY millions unless noted.*

This is the single most important operating metric in the dossier. **Fabrinet** (NYSE: FN), a Thailand-based contract manufacturer serving NVIDIA, Lumentum, and Coherent, accounts for **nearly two-thirds of TFC's total revenue**. TFC supplies Fabrinet with optical engines and passive components that Fabrinet then assembles into modules for NVIDIA's AI networking infrastructure. TFC is effectively an **indirect NVIDIA supplier** — the concentration surged from 19% to 62% in two years as AI datacenter demand exploded. End-customers identified through the Fabrinet channel and direct relationships include NVIDIA (GB200/GB300 optical interconnect, CPO), Amazon AWS (FAU for 1.6T, sampling stage), Microsoft Azure (CPO engineering verification), and Innolight (downstream module customer).

Management stated (June 2025) that CPO "will not make significant revenue contribution in the short term" but positions TFC for the **2026–2027 commercial deployment window** when NVIDIA's Spectrum-X and Quantum-X800 CPO switches scale.

## Module 9: TFC's upstream moat is real, but concentration is the Achilles' heel

TFC occupies the **upstream optical components and precision packaging layer** of the AI connectivity value chain. It is not a transceiver module maker — it supplies the critical sub-components (FAU, lenses, isolators, ceramic ferrules) and performs BOX-level silicon photonics packaging that module makers and OEMs need. This "pick and shovel" positioning means TFC benefits regardless of which downstream module maker wins share (Innolight, Eoptolink, Fabrinet), as they all need TFC's components.

### Competitive moat

TFC's core differentiator is **precision optical packaging at sub-micron tolerances**. The company broke the long-standing Japanese monopoly (Adamant/Kyocera) in ceramic ferrule technology and has built proprietary capabilities in silicon photonics hybrid integration with coupling efficiency **>95%** and optical engine yield **>90%** ⚠️. It was selected as an **official NVIDIA CPO supply chain partner** at GTC, positioning it in the first tier of CPO component suppliers globally. Its vertical integration — self-supplying AWG, FA, MT, and PM sub-components for higher-value optical engines — creates a cost and speed advantage that is difficult for competitors to replicate.

**Switching costs are high.** Optical component qualification cycles for hyperscaler programs run 6–12+ months. Once designed into an architecture generation (e.g., NVIDIA GB200, Quantum-X800), mid-cycle supplier displacement is extremely rare.

### Competitive landscape

| Competitor | Role | Relationship to TFC |
|---|---|---|
| **Innolight (中际旭创, 300308)** | Module maker (#1 in 800G) | Downstream customer; 5.7× TFC's R&D budget |
| **Eoptolink/新易盛 (300502)** | Module maker | Downstream customer |
| **Accelink/光迅科技 (002281)** | Vertically integrated (chips→modules) | Competitor in passive components, telecom-focused |
| **Fabrinet (FN, NYSE)** | Contract manufacturer/assembler | #1 customer (61.7% of revenue); symbiotic |
| **Coherent (COHR)** | Integrated photonics | Competitor in SiPh; TFC procures CW lasers |
| **Lumentum (LITE)** | Laser sources | Supplier of CW lasers to TFC |
| **Senko (Japan)** | FAU/connectors | Direct competitor in precision connectors |

TFC is ~**7× smaller by revenue** than Innolight but commands the **highest P/E multiple** (~120× TTM vs. ~75× for Innolight, ~53× for Eoptolink) due to its upstream certainty premium and higher margins. Its **57% gross margin** (FY2024) is the highest among listed Chinese optical peers.

### Pricing power

Revenue grew **68% in FY2024** while unit volumes grew only **15%**, implying a **~45% ASP increase** from mix enrichment toward optical engines. Passive device COGS grew only 5.9% while passive revenue grew 33%, demonstrating strong operating leverage. The company's addressable content per AI switch is estimated at **$7,000–10,000** in CPO/NPO architectures versus tens of dollars in traditional pluggable configurations.

## Module 10: Extreme valuation and Fabrinet concentration are the key risks

### Near-term catalysts

- **April 23, 2026:** FY2025 annual report release — will provide definitive revenue, segment, customer, and cash flow data
- **2026:** Thailand Phase 2 mass production ramp — capacity expansion for 1.6T optical engines and passive products
- **2026–2027:** CPO commercial volume deployment — NVIDIA Spectrum-X and Quantum-X800 switches scaling
- **Ongoing:** 1.6T optical engine volume acceleration (Goldman projects 190K units in 2026)
- **H-share (Hong Kong) dual listing:** Approved by shareholders April 2, 2026 — would broaden investor base and provide international capital access
- **SuperX JV (Feb 2026):** Singapore-based joint venture with NASDAQ-listed SuperX for AI datacenter optical connectivity solutions

### Key risks

**Valuation risk is the most pressing concern.** The stock trades **25–33% above** the consensus analyst target of ~¥250. At ~¥310, the TTM P/E is approximately **120×**, placing it in the **99th percentile** of its own trading history and well above all peers. The market is pricing in flawless execution on 1.6T ramp, CPO adoption, and Thailand production — leaving minimal margin for error. **Beta of 2.16** amplifies any negative catalyst.

**Fabrinet concentration (61.7% of FY2024 revenue)** creates existential single-customer risk. Any disruption — order shifts, pricing pressure, Fabrinet vertically integrating, or NVIDIA reallocating supply chain — could devastate TFC's revenue. The concentration *increased* from 19% to 62% in two years, and diversification efforts (direct NVIDIA CPO, AWS/Azure sampling) remain early-stage.

**Geopolitical/export control risk** is material given ~76–80% overseas revenue exposure. While TFC does not manufacture advanced chips, it serves the AI chip ecosystem and could be caught in US-China technology restrictions. The Thailand factory partially mitigates this but does not eliminate the risk. China's retaliatory rare earth controls (specifically targeting erbium for fiber optics) could also disrupt TFC's raw material supply.

**EML chip bottleneck** directly constrained Q3 2025 revenue (−3.2% QoQ). TFC does not self-produce the critical 200G EML laser chips it needs for optical engines, depending on Broadcom and Lumentum. This upstream dependency is expected to persist through at least H1 2026.

**A-share retail sentiment risk** is elevated. TFC is part of the "易中天" (Yi-Zhong-Tian) retail trading trio alongside Innolight and Eoptolink, creating meme-like momentum dynamics. The stock's **52-week range of ¥57–389** (a 580% spread) illustrates extreme volatility. ChiNext stocks historically exhibit sharp boom/bust cycles, and a large population of profit-sitting retail holders creates potential for rapid unwinds.

**Additional risks** include: Thailand factory execution risk (higher initial costs, 1–2 year ramp to full efficiency, margin dilution), currency risk (80% USD revenue vs. CNY costs; Q4 2025 FX losses already impacted earnings), technology transition risk (if downstream module makers vertically integrate components), and potential further insider/controlling shareholder selling.

## Conclusion: A genuine AI infrastructure franchise trading at a price that assumes perfection

TFC Optical is the rare Chinese company with a **defensible upstream position** in the AI optical connectivity value chain, **fortress-grade financials** (zero debt, 2.7B net cash, 50%+ gross margins), and direct exposure to the most powerful secular growth driver in technology (AI datacenter buildout). Its precision packaging capabilities, NVIDIA CPO partnership, and vertical integration create a moat that is difficult to replicate on a 1–2 year horizon. The FY2022–FY2025 financial trajectory — revenue compounding at **~60% annually** with expanding margins — is exceptional.

The investment debate centers entirely on **price versus value**. At ~120× TTM earnings, the stock discounts not just the confirmed 1.6T ramp and Thailand expansion, but also the speculative CPO opportunity and continued hyperscaler capex acceleration. The **62% Fabrinet concentration** is an underappreciated vulnerability that transforms what appears to be a diversified AI play into effectively a **single-customer bet on NVIDIA's optical supply chain architecture**. The H-share listing could provide a re-rating catalyst by broadening the investor base, but also risks unlocking new selling pressure. The FY2025 annual report on April 23 will be the next critical data point, potentially recalibrating the growth narrative if full-year revenue and margin trends confirm or challenge the preliminary profit guidance.
