# Lumentum Holdings (LITE): R1 Company Research Dossier

**Lumentum is the dominant EML laser supplier powering the AI optical networking buildout, with revenue accelerating to $665.5M (+65.5% YoY) in FQ2 FY2026 and a credible path toward a $2B quarterly run rate at 40% operating margins within 18–24 months.** The NVIDIA $2B convertible preferred investment and multi-billion-dollar purchase commitment, combined with an OCS backlog surging past $400M and EML capacity sold out through 2027, create an exceptionally rare supply-constrained growth profile. However, the stock trades at extreme multiples (trailing P/E ~210x, forward P/E ~59x NTM) after a +964% one-year return, with meaningful risks from customer concentration, technology displacement via silicon photonics, and heavy convertible debt overhang of $3.2B. The key near-term milestone is FQ4 FY2026 operating margin — if **35%+**, the path to the 40% target is confirmed.

## MODULE 9: COMPETITIVE POSITIONING SNAPSHOT

### Market share in EML lasers

Lumentum holds an estimated **~50–60% share of the global AI EML laser market** and is the **only supplier shipping 200G-per-lane EMLs at volume** as of early 2026. ⚠️ (Citigroup initiated Buy citing ~50% AI EML share). Key competitors include **Coherent (#2)**, Broadcom, Sumitomo Electric, Mitsubishi Electric, and Source Photonics. ✓

### EML technology moat — the key differentiator

The EML laser requires specialized InP (indium phosphide) fabrication with extremely low defect rates. Lumentum has invested over a decade in yield optimization across multiple fabs (Sagamihara, Caswell, Takao). **Capacity is fully sold out through 2027** under long-term agreements. ✓ The introduction of **200G differential EMLs** provides further competitive differentiation. EML output has increased **8x since FY2023**. ✓ The Qorvo Greensboro acquisition adds a fifth InP fab and enables a transition to **6-inch wafers** (vs. legacy 3-inch), a critical scale advantage that Coherent already possesses. ✓

### Biggest competitive threat: CW laser + SiPh as EML alternative

**Samsung announced production readiness for its SiPh foundry platform at OFC 2026 (March 17, 2026):**
- 2027: PICs and optical engines via thermo-compression bonding ✓
- 2028: Mass production with hybrid copper bonding ✓
- 2029: Turnkey CPO services (SiPh + HBM + foundry + packaging) ✓
- Samsung modulator technology has reached **224 Gbps/lane** (measured by imec) ✓

**Technical reality check:** EML devices cost **~$50–100+ per laser** vs. **~$8–10 per CW laser** in SiPh architectures. SiPh runs on CMOS-compatible 300mm wafers (highly scalable) vs. EML's specialized 3–6 inch InP wafers (constrained). However, EML maintains superior performance at 100G/200G per lane today. ✓

**Near-term (2026–2027): SiPh is NOT a credible replacement.** EMLs are the only commercially viable option at 200G/lane in volume. Medium-term (2028–2030): Growing threat as TSMC COUPE and Samsung platforms mature. LightCounting projects SiPh-based transceivers to exceed **50% of market in 2026** (up from 33% in 2024) and **60% by 2030**. ⚠️

**Lumentum's hedge:** UHP (ultra-high-power) 1310nm DFB laser (400mW at 50°C, ~4x standard CW) positions them as a premium external laser source supplier within SiPh ecosystems. This ensures participation even if SiPh displaces EML, though at lower ASPs. ✓

### OCS competitive landscape

Three technology approaches: **MEMS** (Lumentum — core customer Google), **Digital Liquid Crystal** (Coherent), **Direct Beam Steering** (Huber+Suhner/Polatis). Cignal AI tracks 20+ OCS suppliers. Lumentum's >$400M backlog across 3 hyperscale customers gives it first-mover advantage. OCS market forecast: **$2.5B+ by 2029**. ⚠️

### CPO competitive landscape

NVIDIA identified its laser partners at VLSI: **Lumentum (single high-power DFBs)**, Ayar Labs (DFB arrays), Innolume (quantum-dot combs), and Xscape/Enlightra/Iloomina (pumped nonlinear combs). ⚠️ Lumentum secured a **multi-$100M CPO/UHP laser order** with shipments in 2027. Broadcom has shipped 50,000+ CPO switches. ✓

### Customer switching costs

**Very high in the near term.** With all EML capacity booked under LTAs through 2027, customers cannot switch suppliers without risking multi-quarter delays. The InP fab qualification process takes 12–18 months. However, if SiPh matures, switching costs drop significantly as CW lasers are more commoditized. ✓

### Pricing power evidence

Non-GAAP gross margin expanded from **32.3% (FQ2 FY2025) to 42.5% (FQ2 FY2026)** — a **+1,020 bps improvement** in 4 quarters. ✓ Management noted price increases flowing through in the March quarter guide. 200G devices carry ~2x ASP vs. 100G. 1.6T transceiver margins are "significantly better than 800G." ✓ All evidence points to strong and expanding pricing power in the current supply-constrained environment.

**9. Tariff and trade exposure.** CFO characterized tariff impact as "quite minimal" due to manufacturing in Japan, UK, and Thailand (not China). However, rare earth mineral supply risk and potential Section 301 tariff escalation remain risk factors explicitly cited in SEC filings. ✓
