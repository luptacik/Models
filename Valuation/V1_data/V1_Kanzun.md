### Revenue segment notes

**Online recruitment services** (~99% of revenue) is the core. Enterprise customers pay for subscriptions (VIP accounts, job posting exposure, search cards, chat cards). **Other services** (~1% of revenue) are job-seeker value-added services, which declined from RMB 85.7M (FY2024) to RMB 74.8M (FY2025) as the company simplified offerings. No geographic revenue breakdown is disclosed — essentially all revenue is mainland China. The Hong Kong app (OfferToday) is nascent and not separately reported.

### SBC trajectory

SBC dropped **20.3% YoY** in FY2025 (RMB 912M vs. RMB 1,144M), and as a percentage of revenue declined from **17.8% → 15.5% → 11.0%** across FY2023–FY2025. This rapid normalization of SBC is a significant driver of GAAP margin expansion and makes the gap between GAAP and non-GAAP metrics narrower each year. The company has shifted from options to RSUs and appears to be moderating new grant rates.

## MODULE 9: COMPETITIVE POSITIONING SNAPSHOT

### Market share

BOSS Zhipin is **China's #1 online recruitment platform by MAU**, with roughly **2x the MAU of its nearest competitor**. QuestMobile data (April 2025): BOSS Zhipin 51.7M MAU vs. Zhaopin 26.6M, 51job 17.6M, Liepin 11.9M. ✓

China's online recruiting market was estimated at ~RMB 20.9B in 2023 (iResearch/Statista). BOSS Zhipin's FY2023 revenue of RMB 5.95B implies **~28% market share** by revenue, which has likely expanded given its FY2025 revenue of RMB 8.27B while competitors have stagnated or contracted. Online recruitment penetration remains low in China (~35% for job seekers, ~20–30% for blue-collar), providing significant headroom. ⚠️ Market size estimates vary by source.

### Key differentiator: the "direct boss chat" model

BOSS Zhipin pioneered the **mobile-native, AI-driven "Direct Recruitment Model"** in 2014 with three pillars: (1) mobile-first design (not a web platform migrated to mobile), (2) AI recommendation feed replacing search-based job boards, and (3) direct real-time chat between job seekers and hiring managers/business owners (the "bosses"). This model lowered barriers for SMEs without dedicated HR departments and created faster feedback loops vs. the traditional resume-upload-wait cycle. The platform facilitated **2.27 billion mutual consent exchanges** in FY2025 (+22.4% YoY). ✓

### Competitive landscape

| Competitor | Status | Market Position | Threat Level |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Zhaopin** | Private (SEEK/Primavera Capital) | #2 by MAU (26.6M) | Medium — declining relevance |
| **51job** | Private (DCP Capital-led, delisted 2022) | #3 by MAU (17.6M) | Low — fading |
| **Liepin** | Public (HK: 6100) | #4, high-end white-collar niche | Low |
| **LinkedIn China (InCareer)** | **SHUT DOWN Aug 2023** | Exited market | None |
| **Maimai** | Private | Professional social networking | Low |
| **58.com / Gangi Zhizhao** | Private | Blue-collar classified ads | Low |
| **AI-native startups** | Various | No significant scale | Low (current) |

The competitive field has **meaningfully consolidated**. LinkedIn exited China entirely. 51job and Zhaopin were both taken private amid declining relevance (SEEK sold its 76.5% Zhaopin stake for $540M in 2021, suggesting significant value erosion). No well-funded new entrant (e.g., ByteDance/Douyin) has been detected entering recruitment at scale. ✓

### Customer switching costs

Switching costs are **moderate-to-high** due to: (1) bilateral network effects across 250M+ job seekers and 20M+ enterprises, (2) proprietary AI trained on billions of interaction data points that improves with platform usage, (3) deep habit formation among SMEs for whom BOSS Zhipin was their first online recruitment experience, (4) the "Conch Plan" trust infrastructure for blue-collar job verification (costly to replicate), and (5) >50% of revenue coming from enterprises with <100 employees — highly fragmented with no collective bargaining leverage. ✓

### Pricing power evidence

Gross margin expanded from 82.2% (FY2023) → 83.1% (FY2024) → **85.1% (FY2025)**, demonstrating pricing power. Per-segment ARPPU rising among mid-size and key accounts, though blended ARPPU flattened due to faster growth in lower-ARPPU SME accounts. NRR is **not disclosed**. The combination of stable-to-rising per-segment ARPPU, 11.5% paid customer growth, and 12.4% revenue growth implies healthy retention. ✓
