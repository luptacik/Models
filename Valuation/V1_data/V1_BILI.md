# Bilibili R1 company research dossier

**Bilibili Inc (9626.HK / BILI) achieved its first full-year GAAP net profit of RMB 1.19 billion in FY2025**, capping a dramatic two-year profitability inflection that took gross margins from 24% to 37% and swung adjusted operating income from negative RMB 3.4 billion to positive RMB 2.4 billion. The company now sits on RMB 24.2 billion in liquid assets against RMB 9.6 billion in debt, generating over RMB 7 billion in operating cash flow annually. With advertising revenue crossing 33% of the mix and growing 23% YoY, a community moat anchored by 113 million DAUs spending 107 minutes daily, and management targeting 40–45% gross margins and 15–20% adjusted operating margins long-term, Bilibili has transitioned from a loss-making growth story to a profitable platform company. The key question is whether margin expansion can continue while revenue growth decelerates from 19% (FY2024) to the consensus-implied ~10% (FY2026).

**Fiscal year-end: December 31. Reporting currency: RMB (CNY). Dual-listed: 9626.HK (HKEX primary) and BILI (NASDAQ ADR, 1 ADS = 1 Class Z share).**

### Revenue characterization

- **Geographic**: Essentially **100% mainland China**. International gaming (Escape from Duckov on Steam, San Mou overseas launch) is nascent and immaterial to date. No geographic breakout disclosed ✓.
- **Recurring vs. one-time**: VAS (~39% of FY2025 revenue) contains ~25M premium subscriptions (~80% annual/auto-renewal), making it largely recurring. Advertising (33%) is campaign-based but increasingly sticky with a growing advertiser base (+20–35% YoY). Mobile Games (21%) are transaction/event-driven. IP Derivatives (7%) are largely one-time/project-based.

## MODULE 9: Competitive positioning snapshot

### Market position

Bilibili is a **niche-dominant platform** within China's ~RMB 1.15 trillion online audio/video industry. It does not compete on mass scale with ByteDance/Alibaba/Tencent (which command >60% of China's digital ad market combined) but holds the strongest position among **Gen Z / ACG-centric communities**. With **366 million MAUs** and **113 million DAUs**, Bilibili is China's largest medium- and long-form video community, often called the "YouTube of China."

### Competitive differentiation matrix

| vs. Competitor | Bilibili's Advantage | Bilibili's Disadvantage |
|---------------|---------------------|------------------------|
| **Douyin (ByteDance)** | Deeper engagement (107 min/day vs. ~45 min), community loyalty, mid/long-form content, danmaku culture | Massive scale gap (Douyin ~700M+ DAU vs. 113M), superior algorithmic ad targeting, e-commerce integration |
| **Kuaishou** | Stronger urban Gen Z penetration, higher cultural brand cachet, richer content taxonomy | Kuaishou has stronger lower-tier city presence and trust-based e-commerce |
| **iQIYI** | Lower content costs (PUGV model), higher engagement, gaming/advertising diversification | iQIYI has more professional long-form drama content and larger subscriber base historically |
| **YouTube** | Blocked in China — no direct competition; Bilibili is the default long-form UGC platform | YouTube has global scale and the most mature creator monetization ecosystem |

**Biggest competitive threat**: **Douyin/TikTok (ByteDance)** — its algorithmic short-video engine captures the largest share of user attention and ad budgets in China. Douyin's expansion into mid-form video and AI-driven advertising directly threatens Bilibili's ad revenue growth trajectory.

### Switching cost assessment: **MEDIUM**

Cultural lock-in (danmaku, creator relationships, ACG identity, official membership) creates **high emotional switching costs** for core users. However, video platforms are technically substitutable and users maintain multi-platform behavior. The 80% 12-month retention rate and 107 minutes/day engagement suggest strong stickiness for the core community, but Bilibili must continuously invest in content and creator tools to maintain this.

### Pricing power evidence

- **ARPU expanding**: Revenue per MAU rose from ~RMB 68 (FY2023) to **~RMB 83 (FY2025)**, a 22% improvement ✓
- **eCPM improving >10% YoY** through AI-enhanced targeting ✓
- **Advertiser base growing 20–35% YoY** ✓
- **Performance ad revenue growing ~27–30% YoY** ✓
- **AIGC ad materials** now account for 30%+ of performance ad covers ✓
- Premium membership pricing (~RMB 148/year) remains well below iQIYI (~RMB 258/year), leaving headroom for future price increases
- Net revenue retention not disclosed, but expanding advertiser base + increasing spend per advertiser implies positive NRR in advertising ⚠️

## MODULE 10: Risk factors and catalysts

### Near-term catalysts (next 2 quarters)

- **Q1 2026 earnings**: Expected late May 2026 (historically reported May 20–23 window)
- **Escape from Duckov expansion**: Console and mobile ports in development; new desert region announced at The Game Awards 2025. PC version already sold 3 million units by November 2025 with 93% positive Steam reviews ✓. Extremely capital-efficient hit (developed by 5-person Team Soda studio)
- **San Mou international launch**: Flagship SLG expanding to Hong Kong, Macau, Taiwan, Japan, Korea, and overseas markets in 2026
- **Game pipeline**: 4–5 new titles awaiting NPPA licensing approval, including Ncard San Guo (closed beta, expected Q1 2026 release)
- **AI monetization acceleration**: AI-related ad budgets grew ~180% YoY on the platform; AI tools for content creation being developed
- **Share buyback continuation**: ~US$68.8 million remaining under $200M program expiring November 2026
- **NPPA license pace**: 1,195 domestic + 80 import games approved through September 2025 — highest pace since 2020, favorable for Bilibili's pipeline

### Key risks ranked by severity

**1. VIE structure risk — HIGH**
ADS holders own equity in a Cayman Islands shell company with no direct ownership in China operating entities. Contractual VIE arrangements could be deemed non-compliant with PRC laws at any time. CSRC since 2023 requires advance filings for overseas listings, creating a semi-formal regime without full legal recognition of VIEs.

**2. Convertible note maturity and dilution — MODERATE (improved)**
Legacy notes nearly fully retired (only US$13.4M remaining). The 2030 Notes ($690M) could add ~29.1 million shares (~7% dilution) if fully converted at HKD 185.63 (~$23.80), which is approximately the current stock price. RMB 24.2 billion in liquid assets provides ample coverage for any remaining maturities.

**3. Gaming revenue lumpiness — MEDIUM-HIGH**
Q4 2025 gaming revenue fell 14% YoY on tough San Mou comps. Revenue depends heavily on blockbuster success of individual titles. Pipeline subject to NPPA regulatory approval introduces timing uncertainty.

**4. Geographic concentration — HIGH**
Revenue is **~100% China-dependent**. International expansion is nascent. China's youth population is declining long-term. Macro headwinds (property sector, youth unemployment, deflationary pressure) directly impact consumer spending and ad budgets.

**5. Content censorship and regulatory risk — MEDIUM-HIGH**
Danmaku system has drawn regulatory scrutiny. Data privacy regulations (PIPL, Cybersecurity Law) add compliance burden. Sudden regulatory changes could directly impact user engagement or content availability.

**6. Competition from Douyin — HIGH**
ByteDance's massive scale advantage in ad inventory and algorithmic targeting represents the most direct competitive threat to Bilibili's ad revenue growth, which is now the company's primary growth engine.

**7. AI — NET OPPORTUNITY but with execution risk**
Bilibili is actively deploying AI for ad targeting (eCPM +10%), content creation tools, and virtual companionship. AI-related ad budgets on the platform grew ~180% YoY. However, AI could also lower barriers for competitors to replicate Bilibili's content advantages.

**8. 2026 margin guidance may be aspirational ⚠️**
The 40–45% gross margin and 15–20% operating margin targets are "medium/long-term" without specific timelines. At the current ~100–200 bps annual improvement pace, achieving the midpoint would take 2–4 years. If management accelerates AI investment (signaled for 2026), near-term margin expansion could slow.
