# Century Huatong V1 Company Research Dossier

**Century Huatong (世纪华通) | 002602.SZ | Shenzhen Stock Exchange**
**Also known as: Shengqu Games (盛趣游戏) | Key subsidiary: DianDian Interactive / Century Games (点点互动)**
**Fiscal Year End: December 31 | Reporting Standard: CAS/PRC GAAP | Currency: CNY**
**Dossier Date: April 5, 2026**

---

Century Huatong has transformed from a scandal-plagued conglomerate into China's most successful overseas mobile game publisher, driven almost entirely by two titles — Whiteout Survival ($4B+ lifetime) and Kingshot ($812M in year one). Revenue surged **+68% YoY to ~¥38B** in FY2025, with net profit guided at **¥5.55–6.98B (+357–475%)**, marking the company's first full year of clean post-fraud financials. The stock tripled during its ST designation period and was added to the **MSCI Emerging Markets Index in February 2026**. However, the investment case carries a governance risk flag: controlling shareholder Wang Ji has **98.8% of his direct shares pledged**, the company's five-year financial fraud resulted in CSRC penalties and 80 ongoing investor lawsuits, and **~60%+ of gaming revenue depends on a single title** (Whiteout Survival). At ~14x FY2026E P/E, the market prices moderate growth continuation but embeds minimal premium for pipeline optionality or governance risk.

**⚠️ CRITICAL CORRECTION**: The sector handoff identifies Century Games as "FunPlus." This is **incorrect**. FunPlus sold DianDian Interactive (now Century Games) to Century Huatong in 2016 for ~$1B. FunPlus is a separate private company. State of Survival, King of Avalon, and Guns of Glory are **NOT** part of Century Huatong's portfolio. Additionally, the actual controlling shareholder is **Wang Ji (王佶)**, not Shao Henghui.

## MODULE 9: Dominant in overseas SLG, but concentration cuts both ways

### Market Position

Century Games (DianDian Interactive) is the **undisputed #1 Chinese overseas mobile game publisher by revenue**, ranked **#1 on PocketGamer's Top 50 Mobile Game Makers 2025** and **#2 global publisher by monthly revenue** (behind only Tencent) in July 2025 at $202.9M. The publisher has surpassed both NetEase and miHoYo in overseas mobile revenue rankings. Total publisher lifetime revenue exceeds **$6B** (as of September 2025).

In January 2026, Century Games had **4 titles simultaneously in Sensor Tower's Chinese overseas revenue Top 30**: Whiteout Survival, Kingshot, Tasty Travels, and Truck Star.

### Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | Key Title | Status vs. Century Games |
|---|---|---|
| **FunFly/FirstFun** | Last War: Survival (~$2.2B in 2025) | **Primary SLG rival**; led global revenue in multiple months |
| **Lilith Games** | Rise of Kingdoms | **Declining**; post-IDFA UA challenges, revenue fell ~50% from peak |
| **IGG** | Lords Mobile | **Smaller, stable**; HK$5.7B (2024) total revenue |
| **Top Games** | Evony | Steady but not at Whiteout/Kingshot scale |
| **Florere** | Last Z, Dark War | Emerging competitor in 4X survival |

The SLG space is increasingly dominated by Chinese publishers. Traditional leaders (Lilith, IGG) have been overtaken. FunFly's Last War: Survival is the closest rival, leading global monthly revenue in several months during 2025, though Whiteout Survival reclaimed the top spot in August. The two publishers together dominate the survival-SLG hybrid niche.

### Switching Costs and Pricing Power

Player switching costs in SLG games are **extremely high**: months/years of base-building investment, deep social bonds through alliances, and substantial financial commitment (SLG average ARPU of $11, with top players spending up to $1M annually). Whiteout Survival demonstrated clear pricing power in November 2025, when revenue surged **64.3% MoM** to $116.9M while downloads remained flat at ~6M — pure ARPU expansion driven by new monetization features (Chief Gear T4, Leading Glory System).

### Apple Commission Cut Impact

Apple reduced China developer commission from **30% to 25%** effective March 15, 2026. Assuming ~¥3–4B of annual iOS China revenue, this saves approximately **¥150–200M annually** for Century Huatong, flowing directly to gross margin. This applies only to the China mainland iOS storefront; overseas iOS commissions remain at 30%.

## MODULE 10: Governance overhang versus exceptional operational momentum

### Near-Term Catalysts

**FY2025 Annual Report (expected April 28–30, 2026)**: The first full-year clean financials post-ST removal. Key watch items include final audited revenue/profit (vs. ¥5.55–6.98B guided range), impairment charges, litigation provision updates, and 2026 pipeline game disclosures.

**MSCI Emerging Markets Index inclusion (confirmed February 2026)**: Century Huatong was one of the **three largest additions** to the MSCI EM Index in the February 2026 review. This triggers mechanical passive fund inflows and broadens the investor base.

**2026 pipeline launches**: Famished Wilderness (Don't Starve IP), Eternal Tower Classic (NCsoft co-dev), Rainbow Island Classic, and several overseas SLG titles in development. Success of any single title would meaningfully diversify revenue.

**Apple commission reduction**: ~¥150–200M annual margin improvement from the March 2026 commission cut on China iOS revenue.

### Governance Risk: Financial Fraud History

The fraud was systematic. Annual reports from **2018 through 2022** contained false records: goodwill manipulation across five years, a fabricated ¥330M software copyright transaction in 2020, and premature revenue recognition inflating 2020 profits by **22.2%**. The root cause was the ¥29.8B Shengqu Games acquisition (2019), which created ¥15.3B in goodwill and performance commitment pressure that incentivized earnings manipulation. The CSRC investigation launched in July 2023 culminated in penalties totaling ¥41.55M across the company and six executives. The **ST designation** was applied around November 7, 2024 and removed November 11, 2025 — a full year of trading restrictions during which the stock paradoxically tripled from ~¥4 to ¥17.61.

### Litigation Exposure

Approximately **80 investor lawsuits** are pending, with **~¥73M provisioned**. Claims cover shares purchased between April 27, 2019 and July 24, 2023. While provisions may prove adequate, the stock's crash from ~¥12 to ~¥3 during the fraud-disclosure period represents a potentially larger damages pool. However, the subsequent 600%+ recovery may reduce damage period calculations. A separate **ICC arbitration with Wemade (Legend IP)** carries a potential liability of **~¥1B** ⚠️.

### Hit Concentration Risk

This is the **single largest structural risk**. Whiteout Survival likely represents **60%+ of gaming revenue** (64% of August 2025 publisher revenue per AppMagic). Including Kingshot, two titles account for **~95% of Century Games' publisher revenue**. While SLG games have long lifecycles (6–8 years), any acceleration in Whiteout Survival's maturation curve or Kingshot's recent whale backlash (first revenue decline in February 2026) would directly threaten the earnings trajectory. The consensus growth deceleration from +68% (FY2025) to +22% (FY2026) to +9% (FY2027) reflects this lifecycle risk.

### Overseas Regulatory Risk

Revenue is heavily US-dependent (28% of Whiteout, 43% of Kingshot). The PAFACA legislation that forced TikTok's US ban could theoretically extend to Chinese gaming companies, though **Niko Partners assesses current risk as "low"** for non-ByteDance entities. The Tencent military designation by the US DoD (January 2025) sets a concerning precedent. A US ban on Century Games' titles would be **catastrophic** given revenue concentration, but probability remains low.

### Chinese Gaming Regulatory Environment

The domestic regulatory environment has **stabilized** since the 2021–2022 license freeze. The National Press and Publication Administration maintained steady license approval cadence throughout 2025, and China game revenue hit a record **¥350.8B in 2025** (+7.68% YoY). Minors' playtime restrictions remain strict but are now well-integrated into operations. Risk level: LOW.

### FX Risk

With >52% of revenue from overseas (primarily USD, KRW, JPY, EUR), a **5% CNY appreciation** would create approximately **¥1.5–2B in revenue headwinds**. Century Games has partial natural hedging through overseas operating costs (UA spend, staff in San Francisco, Berlin), but no formal hedging program has been disclosed.

### Technology Disruption: AI

AI is primarily an **opportunity** for Century Games. Management has implemented a company-wide AI initiative achieving **60–80% improvement in art production efficiency**. Kingshot's success was partly attributed to AI-generated ad creative diversity. VP Bob Slinn (FunPlus, separate entity but relevant perspective): "AI doesn't replace the essential magic ingredient of creativity required to make a game truly compelling." Analyst consensus views AI as enhancing margins for incumbents with data and scale advantages.

## Conclusion: Exceptional operator with unresolved governance questions

Century Huatong presents an unusual investment case: **world-class operational execution coexisting with world-class governance risk**. The company has built the most successful Chinese overseas mobile gaming franchise, generating ¥7.1B in trailing twelve-month free cash flow at 20.8% margins — metrics that rival the best global gaming companies. The MSCI EM inclusion in February 2026 validates the stock's institutional relevance, and the ¥13B+ buyback program signals management confidence.

Three factors should drive the valuation debate. First, **hit concentration**: Whiteout Survival at 38 months old is likely mid-lifecycle but showed post-peak moderation from $206M/month to ~$117M/month, while Kingshot's first decline in February 2026 with whale backlash warrants monitoring. Second, **governance trust**: Wang Ji's 98.8% share pledge, the five-year fraud history, unchanged senior leadership despite CSRC penalties, and ~¥73M+ in pending litigation all weigh on the appropriate discount rate for this name. Third, **pipeline optionality**: 2026 launches (Famished Wilderness, Eternal Tower, casual game expansion) are not priced into the consensus growth slowdown to +9% in FY2027 — successful titles would represent material upside.

At **~14x FY2026E P/E** with a **~22% revenue growth consensus** for FY2026, the stock appears to discount the governance risk while offering meaningful asymmetry if the pipeline delivers. The FY2025 annual report (expected April 28, 2026) is the next critical catalyst — audited financials, litigation updates, and pipeline disclosure should further clarify the risk-reward framework.
