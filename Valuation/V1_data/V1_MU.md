### 9B. Key Differentiator

HBM4 qualified at NVIDIA for Vera Rubin. First five-year strategic customer agreement signed — unprecedented in memory industry. ✓

### 9C. Biggest Competitive Threat

SK Hynix remains the HBM leader with ~43% market share and first-mover advantage. SK Hynix ADR listing (H2 2026) will create a direct publicly-traded HBM comparable for the first time. Samsung is attempting HBM recovery after yield issues. Long-term risk: if SK Hynix ADR prices at a premium to MU on growth-adjusted basis, it validates MU's discount; if at a discount, MU faces re-rating risk. ⚠️

### 9D. Customer Switching Cost Assessment

**HIGH.** Memory qualification cycles at hyperscalers and NVIDIA require 6–18 months. HBM integration into GPU packages requires co-design and thermal qualification. Five-year SCAs create contractual lock-in. Server DRAM/SSD qualification at enterprise OEMs involves 3–6 month cycles. Automotive qualification (AEC-Q100) requires 12–24 months. ⚠️
