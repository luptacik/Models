# BYD Company Limited: R1 research dossier

**BYD (1211.HK / BYDDY / 002594.SZ) is the world's largest NEV manufacturer by volume and the most vertically integrated automaker globally, but its FY2025 results revealed a company at an inflection point.** Revenue of RMB 804.0B (+3.5% YoY) and net profit of RMB 32.6B (−19% YoY) both missed consensus, while gross margin compressed 170bps to 17.7% amid China's price war. The stock has collapsed ~73% from its May 2025 all-time high of HK$465 to ~HK$94, trading at just ~1.0x P/Sales. BYD now faces a critical 2026: January-February domestic sales plunged −36% YoY after subsidy expiry, but the March 5, 2026 launch of Blade Battery 2.0 and Flash Charging 2.0 — plus three to four overseas factory openings — could catalyze a second-half recovery. The company's structural cost advantages from controlling batteries, semiconductors, motors, ADAS, and die-casting remain intact, and overseas revenue surged to **38.7% of total** in FY2025 (from 28.6% in FY2024), providing a path to margin recovery. Reporting currency: **CNY (RMB)**. Fiscal year-end: **December 31** ✓.

**⚠️ Sector handoff correction:** The handoff stated FY2025 deliveries grew "+~30% YoY." Confirmed data shows **+7.7% YoY** (4.60M vs 4.27M). The ~30% figure appears to conflate FY2024's growth rate (+41.3% YoY).

**⚠️ FY2025 cash flow note:** The annual report was released March 27, 2026, and financial databases have not yet ingested FY2025 cash flow data. TTM through September 2025 showed CFO of **RMB 118.0B** and CapEx of **RMB 142.8B**, yielding negative FCF of approximately **RMB −24.7B**. Q4 typically generates the strongest operating cash flow, so full-year CFO likely improved from the TTM figure, but the massive capex ramp suggests **FY2025 FCF was materially negative** — a stark reversal from FY2023/2024. The sharply reduced FY2025 dividend (RMB 0.358/share vs. RMB 3.974 in FY2024) corroborates significantly lower cash generation.

### Where the CapEx is going

BYD's capex is directed toward battery plants (including Gen 2 Blade Battery and a new 30 GWh sodium-ion plant in Xuzhou), domestic vehicle assembly factories across **30+ industrial parks**, overseas greenfield plants (Hungary, Brazil, Thailand, Indonesia, Turkey, Cambodia, Pakistan), semiconductor fabs (SiC and IGBT for own use), R&D facilities (new Shenzhen mega-campus for 60,000+ engineers, Budapest European R&D center), and Flash Charging infrastructure (~4,990 stations deployed as of March 25, 2026, targeting 20,000 by year-end). Management signaled capex will moderate to **~RMB 100B annually** over the next two years, roughly matching depreciation ⚠️.

### SBC note

BYD uses a "zero-cost ESOP" model where the company repurchases A-shares on the open market and transfers them to employees at RMB 0/share with performance vesting. This is not reported as a material SBC line item in the income statement under Chinese accounting standards. **SBC is immaterial relative to US EV/tech peers.** [NOT FOUND as discrete line item]

## MODULE 5: Management guidance and long-term targets

### FY2026 delivery guidance

**No formal public delivery target.** Unofficial market chatter suggests **5.0–5.5M units** for 2026, implying ~9–20% growth over FY2025's 4.6M ⚠️. JPMorgan's Nick Lai forecasts **6.5M units** globally, well above informal guidance. Management has noted output is currently **constrained by battery capacity** during the Gen 1→Gen 2 Blade Battery transition, with monthly production ramping by 30K–50K units incrementally through the year.

### FY2026 export target

Initial target (January 2026): **1.3M overseas vehicles**. Revised upward at March 30, 2026 analyst briefing to **1.5M overseas vehicles** (+43% YoY), with management expressing "high confidence" ⚠️. This is arguably the single most critical guidance item — management identifies overseas volume as the primary lever to restore profitability, as domestic car sales may be unprofitable in Q1 2026.

### FY2026 revenue / margin guidance

**None provided.** BYD does not give formal revenue or margin guidance. Key signals from March 2026 earnings call: overseas margins are "better than domestic" due to less intense competition; Wang Chuanfu pledged "no price war, but a war of innovation, product, and ecosystem"; capex expected to moderate to **~RMB 100B annually** over next two years.

### Long-term strategic ambitions

Wang Chuanfu articulated a **50/50 domestic-overseas revenue split** as the strategic aspiration: "We aim for a 50/50 split between overseas and domestic, making the company more resilient" ⚠️. BYD targets becoming **"the Toyota of global EV"**, aiming for ~7% global light-duty vehicle market share in 2026 (from ~3% in 2023) ⚠️.

### Technology roadmap

**Blade Battery 2.0** (launched March 5, 2026): LMFP cathode + silicon-carbon anode; **190–210 Wh/kg** energy density (vs ~150 Wh/kg Gen 1); 10C charge rate; **10%→70% in 5 minutes**; 3,000–3,500 cycle life; Denza Z9GT achieved **1,036 km** range (CLTC) ✓.

**Solid-state battery:** BYD developing sulfide all-solid-state battery at **400 Wh/kg**; small-batch installation targeted by **2027**; deployment in ~40,000 vehicles by 2030 ⚠️.

**Autonomous driving:** God's Eye 5.0 (LLM-powered) deployed; "Smart Driving for All" strategy unveiling planned for May 2026; long-term L4 autonomy targeted ⚠️.

## MODULE 9: Competitive positioning snapshot

### The vertical integration moat in numbers

BYD manufactures approximately **75% of its vehicle components in-house** (UBS teardown analysis), compared to Tesla's estimated 46% for China-produced Model 3 ⚠️. Per BYD's own claim: "Only the tires and windows are entirely outsourced." The in-house scope spans batteries (Blade/FinDreams), semiconductors (IGBT/SiC via BYD Semiconductor), motors (Fudi Power), ADAS (God's Eye), die-casting, raw materials (lithium mining in Brazil), software (BMS, infotainment, ADAS full stack), and even a proprietary shipping fleet.

**Quantified cost advantages:** God's Eye C ADAS hardware cost is **RMB 2,800 (~$385)** vs industry LiDAR setups at ~RMB 20,000 — a **7x cost gap** ⚠️. The battery cost advantage is estimated at **20–25%** vs traditional OEMs sourcing externally. BYD's supplier payment terms averaged ~155 days (vs Tesla's ~60 days), providing additional working capital benefit — though BYD standardized to 60-day terms starting June 2025 under regulatory pressure ⚠️.

### Domestic NEV share erosion is the key concern

BYD's domestic retail NEV share fell from **34.1% in FY2024 to 27.2% in FY2025** (−6.9pp), even as it remains the clear #1 ✓. The primary gainer was **Geely**, which surged from ~7.6% to 12.2% (+81% YoY volume growth). Xiaomi's entry captured ~3.8% share. Tesla China declined from 6.0% to 4.9%. The competitive intensity of China's market — with **129 EV brands** currently operating — remains extreme.

### Biggest competitive threats

**Primary: Geely Group** — surged to #2 with 12.2% domestic share; Galaxy/Zeekr brands gaining rapidly; has its own vertical integration ambitions. **Secondary: CATL** — as BYD's battery arm (FinDreams) grows external sales, it directly competes with CATL (45.2% global battery share in Jan 2026 vs BYD's 13.8%). **Tertiary: Xiaomi** — tech ecosystem brand appeal + aggressive pricing could erode BYD's urban customer base.

### Customer switching costs: LOW-MEDIUM, trending toward MEDIUM

The auto industry inherently has low switching costs, and China's price war has made consumers extremely price-sensitive. However, BYD's Flash Charging 2.0 ecosystem (only BYD-badged vehicles can use 1,500kW stations initially) and God's Eye ADAS data/software stickiness are building an ecosystem moat that could increase switching costs over time.

### Pricing power is constrained domestically but stronger overseas

BYD **slashed prices on 22 models by up to 34%** during 2025, and FY2025 auto gross margin compressed from 22.3% to 20.5% ⚠️. However, Q1 2025 gross margin of **20.7%** surpassed Tesla's 16.3%, demonstrating that BYD can achieve healthy margins when not aggressively cutting prices ✓. Overseas ASPs and margins are reportedly higher than domestic. The God's Eye ADAS strategy — offered free on ALL models, including the RMB 69,800 Seagull — is a deliberate market-share weapon that forces competitors to either match (sacrificing revenue) or cede market share.

## MODULE 10: Risk factors and catalysts

### Near-term catalysts (Q1–Q2 2026)

**Blade Battery 2.0 / Flash Charging 2.0 (launched March 5, 2026)** is potentially the most significant product launch since the original Blade Battery in 2020. The 10%→70% charge in 5 minutes at up to 1,500 kW eliminates range anxiety as a purchase barrier for the remaining ~50% of ICE holdouts. PSR analysis projects this could drive **40–50% growth in BYD's pure-EV sales in 2026**, shifting BEV/PHEV mix from 40/60 to 60/40 ⚠️. Consumer response: reports of surging dealership traffic post-launch.

**Factory openings:** Hungary (Szeged) full production before end of Q2 2026 — BYD's first European passenger car factory, tariff-exempt ✓. Turkey (Manisa) scheduled mid-2026, also EU customs union access ✓. Indonesia mass production beginning Q1–Q2 2026 ✓.

**EU minimum import price (MIP) mechanism:** Published January 12, 2026; could replace the 27% tariff, allowing BYD to retain the tariff margin as profit rather than paying it as duty. Negotiations ongoing. BYD stock gained 4.8% on the news ✓.

**Q1 2026 results** expected **April 29, 2026** ✓ (Investing.com/Quartr). Will be closely watched for March sales rebound after Jan-Feb trough.

**China stimulus:** Trade-in subsidies extended into 2026 (up to RMB 20,000); CAAM forecasts China NEV sales to grow **15.2% to 19M units** in full-year 2026 ✓.

### Key risks

**1. Domestic demand softness (HIGH RISK):** Full NEV purchase tax exemption → 50% exemption = ~RMB 15,000 additional cost per vehicle. This triggered massive pull-forward into Q4 2025 and crashed January 2026 NEV wholesale by −43% MoM. BYD's domestic retail NEV share fell from 14.4% (2024) to 7.1% (Jan-Feb 2026) ⚠️. Expected to be transitional (Q1 impact) rather than structural.

**2. EU/US tariff escalation (MODERATE-HIGH):** BYD faces a **27% total EU tariff** (17% CVD + 10% standard). Europe is the fastest-growing export market. The MIP mechanism could provide relief, but also could eliminate BYD's price advantage vs EU-produced vehicles. US market effectively closed with **100%+ tariffs** — but BYD has zero meaningful US passenger sales, so this is priced in.

**3. Margin compression from price war (HIGH):** FY2025 net profit fell 19% YoY; gross margin compressed 170bps. Q2 2025 gross margin hit **16.3%** — lowest in 3 years. With 129 EV brands in China, analysts predict only ~15 survive to 2030. Mitigation: vertical integration cost floor + rising overseas mix at higher ASPs.

**4. Lithium price recovery (MODERATE):** Lithium carbonate surged **17% MoM in December 2025** to CNY 103,000/ton. Goldman Sachs projects $13,250/ton in 2026; Ganfeng projects potentially CNY 150,000+/ton. BYD's mitigation: owned lithium mines, LFP chemistry (less cobalt/nickel exposure), Blade 2.0's higher energy density (less lithium per kWh).

**5. Geopolitical risk (MODERATE-HIGH):** US-China tensions could trigger expanded sanctions. BYD has deliberately de-risked (avoiding US-sanctioned chip suppliers like NVIDIA). Taiwan contingency remains a tail risk. Data security concerns for Chinese connected vehicles growing in EU/US. BYD is localizing aggressively (European HQ in Budapest, local sourcing) to mitigate.

**6. Geographic concentration (HIGH, IMPROVING):** China domestic share of revenue fell from **73.4% (FY2023) to ~61.4% (FY2025)** — rapid diversification, but still heavily China-dependent. Management's 50/50 target implies years of continued shift.

**7. FX risk (MODERATE):** With 38.7% overseas revenue, CNY/EUR and CNY/USD movements are increasingly material. Localized production (Hungary, Brazil, Thailand) provides natural hedging over time. No specific hedging strategy publicly disclosed.

**8. Balance sheet / capex funding (MODERATE):** Asset-liability ratio: 71.8% (Jun 2025). Total liabilities exceed RMB 600B. Working capital deficit widened to RMB 122.7B (Jun 2025). However, net operating cash flow remains positive, and BYD successfully raised ~HK$43.5B via equity placement. Management guides capex moderating to ~RMB 100B annually.

**9. Customer concentration: LOW RISK.** BYD sells to millions of retail consumers across 5 brands. No single customer represents >10% of revenue ✓.

**10. Technology disruption (MODERATE):** Solid-state batteries could leapfrog Blade 2.0 (BYD developing its own, targeting 2027). Tesla FSD China approval could shift competitive dynamics. BYD's defense: **RMB 63.4B R&D spend** (1.94x net profit), 120,000+ engineers, 29,201 patents, and the God's Eye data moat from fleet-wide deployment.

## Conclusion: what models need to know

BYD's investment case hinges on whether the FY2025 earnings deterioration represents a cyclical trough or the beginning of structural margin compression. Three dynamics will determine this. First, the Blade Battery 2.0 / Flash Charging 2.0 cycle is the most powerful product catalyst in BYD's history — its ability to reignite domestic demand and shift BEV/PHEV mix will be visible in Q2–Q3 2026 delivery data. Second, overseas expansion is now the primary margin lever: **38.7% of revenue and rising**, with higher ASPs and less price-war intensity than China. The trajectory toward 50/50 domestic-overseas by ~2028–2029 would structurally de-risk the business. Third, the price war is a double-edged sword: BYD's vertical integration moat allows it to survive (and even drive) consolidation among China's 129 EV brands, but at the cost of near-term margins.

For DCF modeling, the critical inputs are the **FY2025 negative FCF inflection** (driven by ~RMB 140–155B capex vs ~RMB 97–120B CFO), management's guidance that capex moderates to ~RMB 100B, and the FY2026E consensus range of RMB 847–971B revenue with **extremely wide dispersion**. The net cash position of ~RMB 78B (Q3 2025) provides a balance sheet cushion, and BYD's R&D expensing policy (98.2% expensed vs capitalized) means reported earnings significantly understate economic profitability relative to peers.

At **~1.0x P/Sales** and **~22.6x trailing P/E**, BYD trades at a structural 50–70% discount to Tesla/Western EV peers — consistent with the Chinese EV valuation framework. The 28 analysts covering the stock maintain a consensus **Strong Buy** with an average target of ~HK$125 (+33% upside), though estimate dispersion is extreme (HK$85–233) and the post-earnings revision cycle is still underway. The next decisive data point is Q1 2026 results, expected **April 29, 2026**.
