# Geely Automobile Holdings (0175.HK): Valuation-grade equity research data package

**Geely is China's #2 automaker by retail sales, delivering 3.02 million vehicles in FY2025 (+39% YoY) with revenue of RMB 345.2 billion and core net profit of RMB 14.41 billion (+36%).** The company's five-brand portfolio — Geely, Galaxy, Zeekr, Lynk & Co, and Polestar — spans the full price spectrum from entry-level to premium. Galaxy became the fastest NEV brand to 1 million annual sales in history, while Zeekr turned profitable in Q4 2025. The December 2025 Zeekr reabsorption, an accounting policy change to merger accounting, and ongoing restatements make financial comparisons complex. Consensus is overwhelmingly bullish (28–29 Buy, 1 Hold, 0 Sell), with an average price target of ~HK$26.7 implying ~12% upside from the current ~HK$23.82.

**Reporting currency:** RMB | **Fiscal year-end:** December 31 | **FY2025 results released:** March 18, 2026

Geely's **16.6% gross margin** compares to BYD's 17.7% (down from 19.4% in FY2024), Great Wall's ~17–18%, Li Auto's ~20.5%, and Tesla's 16.3%. Geely's **operating margin of ~4.2% (core basis)** exceeds the China auto industry average of ~3.4%, per Investing.com. ✓

### Brand portfolio effectiveness

Geely's multi-brand strategy covers all price segments from Panda Mini (entry) to ZEEKR 9X (>RMB 500K). **Galaxy's achievement of 1.24M units in its second full year of sales — the fastest any NEV brand reached 1 million annual sales — validates the platform-sharing approach.** The Xingyuan (EX2) became the **#1 selling car in China across all segments** at 465,775 units. ✓ The "One Geely" integration yielded measurable cost savings: admin expense ratio down 17.1%, R&D ratio down 13.5% YoY. ✓

The key risk is complexity cost. Despite 3M+ unit scale, gross margin expanded only ~70bps (15.9% → 16.6%). Institutional investors have questioned why margins haven't improved more. Management's answer: heavy R&D investment (RMB 21.8B) and strategic market share prioritization over short-term margin optimization.

### Zeekr in premium EV

Zeekr delivered 224K units with **19% gross margin** (Q3 2025) and turned profitable in Q4 2025 — a landmark for Chinese "new force" brands. Versus NIO (326K units, ~10% GM, loss-making), XPeng (429K units, approaching breakeven), and Li Auto (406K units, -19% YoY, profitable), **Zeekr is arguably the most capital-efficient new EV brand in China.** Its ZEEKR 9X holds the #1 position in large SUVs above RMB 500K.

### Customer switching costs

Switching costs are **low-to-moderate**, consistent with the highly competitive China auto market (~100+ brands). Geely's ecosystem advantages include Meizu/FlyMe Auto integration (2.26M+ installations, #1 in China), and the multi-brand "trade-up" path (Geely → Galaxy → Lynk → Zeekr) helps retain customers within the family. ✓

## Conclusion

Geely's FY2025 results confirm its emergence as China's **clear #2 automaker** — the first time it has claimed this position — driven by Galaxy's explosive ramp and a multi-brand platform strategy that is delivering both scale and improving (if modest) margins. The core net profit trajectory (**RMB 5.3B → 10.6B → 14.4B** over FY2023–25) demonstrates genuine operating leverage, while net cash of RMB 49.9B provides substantial financial cushion.

The most important data gaps for a valuation model are the FY2025 full cash flow statement (CFO/CapEx breakdown), specific debt instrument terms, and SBC full-year figures — all of which will be available once the full annual report is filed on HKEX. The accounting restatements from the merger accounting policy change and Zeekr consolidation create genuine complexity in year-over-year comparisons; any DCF model should anchor to the restated figures.

Three watchpoints merit the closest attention going forward: whether Galaxy can re-accelerate after Q1 2026's deceleration, whether the Ford partnership materializes (transformative for European market access), and whether the ~45% stock rally since mid-February adequately prices in the improving fundamental trajectory or has overshot near-term.
