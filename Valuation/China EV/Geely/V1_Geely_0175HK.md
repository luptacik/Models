## KEY RISKS TO THE THESIS

1. **Multi-brand margin trap:** Geely's 16.6% gross margin is 6th of 7 peers despite having the 2nd-highest delivery count. If the multi-brand structure creates persistent cost overhead that prevents margin convergence with BYD (20.5%), the P/S discount is structural rather than a mispricing.

2. **Galaxy deceleration:** Q1 2026 Galaxy deliveries showed YoY declines after 150% growth in FY2025. If Galaxy's growth rate normalizes to <20%, the revenue trajectory shifts meaningfully downward.

3. **EU tariff escalation:** The 28.8% tariff is currently manageable but could tighten if minimum price undertakings fail. The Ford partnership provides a hedge, but it's unconfirmed.

4. **Polestar drag:** Polestar (associate, not consolidated) posted a $1.2B H1 2025 loss. Further impairments or capital calls would impact Geely's equity income line.

5. **China demand normalization:** CAAM projects just +1% total vehicle growth for 2026. If domestic demand weakens beyond the subsidy-expiry adjustment, the +14% delivery target becomes aggressive.

6. **Geopolitical discount deepening:** US-China tensions, Taiwan escalation, or additional trade barriers could widen the structural discount on Chinese equities irrespective of fundamentals.
