## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

BYD is **roughly fairly valued** at HK$103.9, with a triangulated base case of HK$105 (+1.5%). The valuation reveals a fundamental tension: multiples-based methods (comps at HK$134, SoP at HK$131) see the stock as cheap relative to peers and its embedded asset value, while cash-flow-based methods (DCF base at HK$81) reflect the uncomfortable reality that BYD is currently burning cash on a massive capex cycle and has never sustained meaningful FCF margins. The resolution of this tension depends on whether FY2025 represents a cyclical capex peak (management guides normalization to ~CNY 100B/yr) or the beginning of a structurally capital-intensive phase as BYD builds overseas factories and battery capacity simultaneously.

The stock has collapsed ~73% from its May 2025 ATH of HK$465, creating a superficially attractive entry. But the decline is partially justified: FY2025 revenue grew just 3.5% (the slowest among major Chinese EV OEMs), net profit fell 19% YoY, and domestic sales plunged 36% YoY in Jan-Feb 2026 after subsidy expiry. The bear case (HK$53) is severe and non-trivial if the price war intensifies, EU tariffs tighten, and capex stays elevated. The bull case (HK$135) requires Blade Battery 2.0 to reignite domestic demand, overseas margins to expand, and capex to normalize — all achievable but unproven.

**Conviction: 3.5/5.** Not enough upside for a concentrated position, but the risk/reward supports conditional put-selling below HK$95 after Q1 2026 results confirm the domestic recovery trajectory.

**Purpose:** What growth rate is the market pricing in at HK$103.9?

**Assumptions:** Terminal FCF margin 6.0%, terminal growth 2.5%, WACC 11.0%, 10-year ramp from current FCF margin (-4.4%) to terminal, margin ramps linearly.

**Result: Implied revenue CAGR = 13.4%**

| FY2025 actual revenue growth | 3.5% |

**Gap Analysis:** The market is pricing in ~13.4% annual growth, which is almost exactly in line with FY2026 consensus. The market is NOT pricing in heroic growth — it's pricing in consensus execution. This suggests the stock is **roughly fairly valued on a DCF basis**, with upside coming from better-than-expected execution or downside from execution misses.

The DCF captures this economic reality, which is partially masked by the market's willingness to value BYD on revenue multiples and earnings (which include non-cash depreciation as a deduction but don't reflect the ongoing capex required to maintain the business).

### Positioning

On a growth-adjusted P/Sales basis (33.4x), BYD is by far the most expensive — reflecting the market's willingness to pay a "quality/moat premium" for vertical integration, but also reflecting the near-stagnation of FY2025 revenue.

BYD deserves a premium to Geely (0.5x) for its deeper moat and scale, but a discount to high-growth peers (1.4-1.7x) for its growth deceleration. Fair EV/NTM Revenue: 1.0-1.3x (mid 1.15x).

### Why DCF and Comps Disagree

The DCF base case (HK$81) and comps (HK$134) diverge by 65%. This is not a modeling error — it reflects a genuine analytical tension:

1. **DCF captures the FCF problem.** BYD is in a massive capex cycle (FY2025 capex ~CNY 145B vs ~CNY 110B CFO) building overseas factories, battery plants, and Flash Charging infrastructure. Even after normalization, auto companies structurally generate 4-6% FCF margins — far below the 15-25% typical of high-multiple tech companies. The DCF values BYD on what it actually generates in cash, and that number is not impressive relative to its market cap.

2. **Comps capture the strategic position.** On a multiples basis, BYD is the world's #1 EV maker with the deepest vertical integration moat, trading at a 50-70% discount to Tesla. Chinese EV OEMs trade at 0.5-1.7x P/Sales, and BYD at ~1.1x is below the peer median despite being the highest-quality franchise. The comps say the market is giving BYD insufficient credit for its competitive position.

3. **Resolution:** The truth is likely in between. The appropriate weight is DCF 40% / Comps 30% because BYD's near-term FCF trajectory is genuinely uncertain (FY2025 was deeply negative) and the capex normalization timeline is unproven. But the comps anchor is important because BYD's competitive position is durable and the market does value it on revenue/earnings multiples, not just DCF.

### CONVICTION SCORE: 3.5 / 5

**Rationale:** Methods partially converge but with significant dispersion. The reverse DCF provides reassurance that the market is pricing in achievable (~13%) growth, not heroic assumptions. However, conviction is capped because: (1) FY2025 FCF was deeply negative and the recovery path is unaudited consensus, (2) Jan-Feb 2026 domestic sales plunged 36% and recovery is unproven, (3) estimate dispersion is extreme (FY2026E revenue CNY 847-971B), and (4) terminal value dominates all DCF scenarios at >70%, meaning the valuation depends on long-term FCF margin assumptions that BYD has never sustainably demonstrated.
