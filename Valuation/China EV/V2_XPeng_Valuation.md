## VALUATION METHOD 1: REVERSE DCF

### Purpose
What revenue growth rate does the current HKD 67.75 share price imply? This answers whether the market is pricing XPeng as a growth company or as a mature, low-growth automaker.

### Methodology
Starting from XPeng's current enterprise value of CNY 83.9B, I solve for the constant revenue CAGR over 10 years that generates sufficient discounted free cash flow (including terminal value) to equal that EV.

### Assumptions
- **Current revenue:** CNY 76.7B (FY2025)
- **Current FCF margin:** ~6.5% (FY2025)
- **Terminal FCF margin:** 7% (auto sector — capital-intensive, lower than SaaS terminal margins)
- **Terminal growth rate:** 3%
- **WACC:** 12% (reflects China risk premium, EV sector volatility, geopolitical discount)
- **Margin ramp:** Linear from 6.5% to terminal over 10 years

### Results

### Interpretation
The Reverse DCF delivers the clearest signal in this entire analysis: **the market is pricing XPeng for near-zero growth.** Even at the most punitive assumptions (14% WACC, 5% terminal FCF margin), the implied CAGR is only ~15% — still well below consensus. At base case assumptions, the market implies ~8% annual growth for a company that grew revenue 88% last year and whose weakest consensus estimate (FY2028) still projects 15–20% growth.

This is consistent with the sector handoff observation that XPeng trades at ~1.4x trailing P/Sales — essentially a "low-margin automaker" multiple rather than a technology licensing platform multiple. The Reverse DCF suggests the stock is pricing in a structural scenario where: (a) ADAS commoditization destroys the premium positioning, (b) the VW partnership underwhelms, and (c) the domestic price war compresses margins permanently.

If you believe *any* of the consensus growth estimates are approximately right, the stock appears significantly undervalued at current levels.

XPeng is a barely-profitable, cash-generative growth company. A traditional LBO with leverage is unrealistic — no PE firm would lever a company with a single year of positive FCF in a price war environment. The appropriate framework is **growth equity** — pure equity investment, targeting 25% IRR.

## APPENDIX: WHAT MAKES XPENG DIFFERENT FROM A TYPICAL AUTO VALUATION

This is the first EV OEM in this valuation framework — prior analyses covered cybersecurity SaaS companies. Several key differences merit flagging:

1. **SBC is immaterial.** At 0.7% of revenue, SBC does not distort XPeng's FCF picture. This stands in stark contrast to cybersecurity names where SBC was 23–27% of revenue and required mandatory dual-track FCF valuation. No SBC adjustment is needed here.

2. **Terminal margins are structurally lower.** Auto manufacturing is capital-intensive with 5–10% terminal FCF margins versus 25–35% for SaaS. This fundamentally limits the DCF upside — a 7.5% terminal FCF margin (base case) is realistic for a premium EV maker with technology licensing, versus the 25%+ used for CRWD/ZS.

3. **The "China discount" is real and permanent.** The sector handoff calibrates a 30–50% structural discount versus Western peers. This is embedded in the WACC (12% vs. 10% for US SaaS) and in the comps analysis (Chinese EV P/Sales of 0.5–1.7x versus Tesla at 8–12x).

4. **Deliveries are the primary signal, not ARR.** Unlike SaaS where ARR/NRR drives forward visibility, EV companies report monthly delivery data that provides high-frequency real-time signal. The Q1 2026 delivery collapse (−33% YoY) is the dominant near-term concern and would not be visible in a quarterly-only reporting framework.

5. **Net cash is a meaningful portion of equity value.** XPeng's CNY 35.1B net cash represents ~30% of its market cap. In the bear case, net cash alone supports HKD 19.5 per share — roughly 29% of the current price. This provides a hard floor that cybersecurity SaaS companies (which are typically net-debt or minimal net cash) cannot match.
