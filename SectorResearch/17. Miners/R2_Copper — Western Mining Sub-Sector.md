# R3 Financial Metrics: Copper — Western Mining Sub-Sector

**18 companies profiled (FIL.TO excluded — delisted Jan 17, 2025).** Copper trades at **~$5.86/lb COMEX** (Apr 10, 2026) with a historically wide COMEX-LME spread due to US tariff arbitrage. Southern Copper and Antofagasta command the lowest net cash costs in the universe (**$0.58/lb and $1.19/lb** respectively), while 29Metals and First Quantum sit at the high-cost end amid operational disruptions. Two major supply shocks — FCX's Grasberg mud rush and IVN's Kamoa-Kakula seismic event — have removed **~600kt+ of annualized capacity**, tightening global balances. M&A hit an 18-year high in 2025 ($89B across 180 deals), headlined by BHP/Lundin's Filo Corp acquisition and the Anglo American–Teck merger of equals.

**Data vintage:** Most data reflects Q4 2025 / FY2025 results reported through March 2026. FX: USD/CAD ~1.39, AUD/USD ~0.71, GBP/USD ~1.35, USD/PLN ~3.63, USD/CLP ~920.

---

## Market context and copper price backdrop

LME copper hit an all-time high of **~$6.58/lb in January 2026** before correcting to ~$5.49/lb in late March on US-Iran conflict fears. The market has recovered to ~$5.86/lb as of April 10. The forward curve is in **contango** on both LME and COMEX, though COMEX inventories have swollen to a record **503,000+ mt** as traders front-run expected US tariffs on refined copper (anticipated mid-2026). Consensus 2026 copper forecasts span **$4.45–$5.90/lb**, with JP Morgan at $5.48/lb and Goldman Sachs more conservative at ~$5.17/lb. Structural deficits are expected from 2027 onward by most banks.

## Five fundamental divergences shaping this universe

**The cost curve separates winners from survivors.** The spread between the lowest-cost producer (SCCO at $0.58/lb net C1) and the highest (29M at $3.74/lb) is **$3.16/lb** — meaning at $4.50/lb copper, SCCO earns $3.92/lb margin while 29M earns just $0.76/lb. HBM achieves negative C1 costs through massive gold byproduct credits, making it effectively a gold mine that also produces copper. ANTO and SCCO's sub-$1.20/lb net costs give them the widest margin of safety in any price environment. The cost leaders (SCCO, CAML Kounrad, HBM, ANTO) are positioned to generate strong FCF even if copper falls 30% from current levels.

**Leverage tells two stories.** Five companies in the universe sit at net cash (LUN, SFR, AIS, ATYM, CAML), while FM.TO's **3.3x net debt/EBITDA** and **$5.8B total debt** represent existential risk if Cobre Panama remains permanently closed. FM spends ~$550–575M annually on interest alone — consuming a third of its EBITDA. TKO at 2.4x carries elevated leverage tied to Florence Copper construction that should self-cure as that project ramps. The contrast between LUN (net cash, $4.5B RCF being arranged) and FM (3.3x leveraged, covenant at 4.75x) is stark given their similar market caps (~$24B each).

**Producers versus developers present asymmetric risk profiles.** SLS.TO (Warintza) offers a credible path to production with a **26% IRR** and freshly approved EIA, but requires $3.7B in project financing with no cornerstone partner. NDM.TO (Pebble) is functionally uninvestable after the DOJ defended the EPA veto in February 2026 — a development that destroyed 39% of market cap in a single day. The developer premium now heavily favors SLS over NDM, with SLS trading at ~0.33x PFS NPV vs NDM at ~0.3x PEA NPV but with incomparably better permitting trajectory.

**Jurisdiction risk is being repriced in real time.** FM.TO (Panama closure, now 2.5 years running), IVN.TO (DRC seismic event, production cut), and NDM.TO (Alaska EPA veto) illustrate how political and geological risk can rapidly destroy value. Meanwhile, **ATYM (EU/Spain), AIS (Australia), ERO (Brazil), and CS/HBM (Americas diversified)** trade at lower multiples despite objectively safer operating environments. The market continues to apply a significant premium to IVN despite DRC risk, reflecting Kamoa-Kakula's extraordinary grade — but the March 2026 guidance cut (24% production reduction) has shaken confidence.

**Valuation dislocations reveal opportunity and caution.** AIS.AX trades at **~1.7x EV/EBITDA** and **4x forward PE** — either deep value or a market signal about asset quality and mine life. CAML.L at **~2.7x EV/EBITDA** with 75% Kounrad EBITDA margins and 5–7% dividend yield appears materially undervalued relative to its cash generation, though reserve depletion risk (only ~72kt recoverable Cu remaining at Kounrad) partially explains the discount. At the other extreme, SCCO's ~14–15x EV/NTM EBITDA reflects its scarcity premium (89% owned by Grupo Mexico, ~11% free float), 60+ year reserve life, and growth pipeline to 1.6Mtpa — but leaves little room for error. LUN.TO at ~12.6x prices in significant optionality from Vicuña, which could produce **>500kt Cu/yr** — potentially doubling LUN's production base.
