## Macro copper: record prices, tariff shock, and a fractured supply chain

Copper prices surged ~40% in 2025 — the largest annual gain since 2009 — driven by the Grasberg shutdown, tariff-driven US stockpiling, and structural demand from electrification and AI. **COMEX inventories ballooned past 503kt** (up from <100kt a year prior) as traders front-ran potential tariffs. The COMEX-LME arbitrage that fueled this trade has since narrowed to ~2 cents.

The April 2, 2026 Section 232 proclamation restructured copper tariffs fundamentally. Annex I-A products (refined copper, alloys) now face a **50% tariff on full customs value** — previously tariffs applied only to copper-content value. A de minimis exception covers products with <15% aggregate metal content, and US-smelted copper pays only 10%. The Commerce Department must report by June 30, 2026, after which the President may impose phased tariffs of **15% on cathodes starting January 2027, rising to 30% by January 2028**. Goldman Sachs' base case expects the 15% cathode tariff to be announced mid-2026.

The concentrate market is historically tight. Annual benchmark TC/RCs settled at **$0/tonne and $0/lb for 2026** — the lowest ever. The Argus weekly TC index deteriorated to **-$60.20/t by mid-March**, and Chinese smelters announced >10% production cuts. On the demand side, the structural thesis (EVs, grid, data centers, AI) remains intact — Wood Mackenzie projects copper consumption rising 24% to 42.7 Mtpa by 2035 — but near-term Chinese physical demand is soft.

Analyst 2026 copper price forecasts cluster around **$11,000–12,500/mt ($5.00–5.70/lb)**, with J.P. Morgan most bullish at $12,500/mt in Q2. The 2026 supply-demand balance is contested: ING sees a **600kt deficit**, J.P. Morgan estimates **330kt deficit**, while Goldman and Nornickel project modest surpluses of 160–300kt.

## 1. FCX — Freeport-McMoRan: Grasberg MOU de-risks the crown jewel

**Grasberg Block Cave restart and life-of-resource extension dominate the near-term outlook.** The September 2025 mud rush halted FCX's most valuable asset, cutting FY2025 copper production to **3.4B lbs** (vs. 4.2B in 2024) and gold to 1.0M oz (vs. 1.9M). By year-end, 97% of mud removal was completed. **GBC Production Blocks 2 & 3 are targeted for restart in April 2026**, with ramp-up to 100,000 tonnes/day by H2 2026 and ~85% of district production restored. The 2026 PTFI guidance of ~1.0B lbs copper and ~0.9M oz gold remains ~35% below pre-incident levels; normalization (1.6B lbs Cu, 1.3M oz Au annually) is expected from 2027–2029.

On **February 18, 2026**, FCX signed an MOU with the Indonesian government for a **life-of-resource extension** of PTFI's operating rights, previously set to expire in 2041. FCX retains its 48.76% ownership through 2041, then transfers 12% to government interests at no cost, reducing its stake to ~37% from 2042 onward. This removes the single largest regulatory overhang on the stock and secures multi-decade access to one of the world's richest copper-gold deposits.

The Americas Leach Innovation Initiative produced >200M lbs of copper in 2025 at costs below $1.00/lb, with a **2026 target of 300M lbs** (rising to 800M lbs by 2030). El Abra (Chile) added ~17.5B lbs of recoverable reserves for a potential multi-billion-dollar mill expansion, with heat trials expected in 2026 and a possible FID in late 2026.

Q4 2025 earnings (January 22) beat consensus handily — adjusted EPS of **$0.47 vs. $0.30 expected**. The quarterly dividend stands at $0.15/share. Q1 2026 earnings are due **April 23**. JPMorgan raised its target to $76 on April 9; consensus is Buy.

## Conclusion: convergence of catalysts in an unprecedented price environment

The 90-day window has delivered a remarkable density of material catalysts across the Western copper universe. The sector's three dominant themes are: **operational milestones** (Grasberg restart, Florence first cathode, Kamoa-Kakula smelter commissioning, Kansanshi S3 commercial production), **project-level inflections** (Vicuña PEA at $9.5B NPV, Furnas PEA at $2.04B, Taca Taca re-rating to $5.92B NPV, Copper World sanctioning approaching), and **regulatory/geopolitical shifts** (Section 232 tariff restructuring, KGHM tax reform, Cobre Panamá stockpile approval, FCX Indonesia MOU, Twin Metals legislative action).

With COMEX+LME inventories at 745kt and Chinese imports down 16%, the market is priced for a deficit that has not yet materialized in physical flows. The Commerce Department's June 30 report and potential cathode tariff announcement will likely be the next macro catalyst for the entire sector. Company-specific, the most impactful upcoming events are FCX's Q1 earnings on April 23 (first full quarter of Grasberg restart data), FM.TO's environmental audit mid-April, and the Vicuña/Copper World/Santo Domingo sanctioning decisions clustering in H2 2026.
