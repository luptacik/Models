### SCORE JUSTIFICATIONS — DIMENSION BY DIMENSION

**Sub-Sector Tailwind**

- **PLTR (5):** At the center of enterprise AI deployment. Government AI spending is a direct, non-discretionary tailwind. AIP is the infrastructure layer enterprises cannot bypass for complex AI at scale. US commercial revenue +137% YoY proves civilian adoption. Regulatory tailwinds (defense AI mandates) and data gravity from AI workloads directly increase platform consumption.
- **SNOW (5):** Data warehouse/lakehouse is the foundational layer for all enterprise AI. AI workloads increase data consumption directly. Cortex AI creates data gravity — data stays in Snowflake rather than being exported. 9,100+ accounts using AI features. RPO +42% YoY signals pipeline well ahead of recognized revenue.
- **DDOG (4):** AI workloads create incremental observability demand — more infrastructure to monitor. 29% revenue growth at $3.4B scale. Bookings +37% YoY signals acceleration. Not the direct AI infrastructure layer (scored 4 not 5) but a clear beneficiary.
- **NOW (4):** Enterprise workflow platform with 21% subscription growth at $13B scale. Now Assist AI at $600M ACV tracking to $1B+ is the fastest-scaling AI revenue in enterprise SaaS. Not directly in the data analytics stack (scored 4 not 5).
- **ZETA (4):** AI-powered marketing cloud riding enterprise AI adoption. 30% revenue growth with 34% FY26 guide. OpenAI strategic collaboration for Athena platform. AI is accelerating platform consumption — but marketing is a cyclical end-market (not 5).
- **MDB (4):** Atlas vector search positions MongoDB for AI application development (RAG, agentic workflows). 27% growth. But guidance deceleration to 17% FY27 and CRO departure signal near-term headwinds.
- **ESTC (4):** Search is foundational to RAG/agentic AI pipelines. 3,000+ AI customers. 18% growth. But smaller scale and lower margins limit the tailwind score.
- **VEEV (3):** Life sciences vertical is growing but not the fastest AI sub-sector. Vault CRM migration provides revenue visibility but is a replacement cycle, not new TAM creation. 16% growth is healthy but industry-specific, not AI-driven acceleration.
- **CRM (3):** Mature enterprise apps market growing ~10%. Agentforce ARR at $1.4B (+114% YoY) is meaningful but organic ex-Informatica growth is only ~7-8%. Not an AI infrastructure play — AI is a feature, not the platform.
- **PEGA (3):** Low-code/BPM is a growing category but not the highest-growth AI sub-sector. 17% ACV growth is solid but not exceptional. Blueprint AI-powered workflow design is compelling but early.
- **TWLO (3):** Communications platform benefiting from Voice AI adoption (+5x sequential RCS) but organic growth is only 8-9%. Not a direct AI infrastructure play.
- **PATH (3):** RPA is being disrupted by agentic AI — UiPath must prove it's the beneficiary, not the victim. Cloud ARR growing ~50% is positive but total ARR growth of 11% reflects legacy drag.
- **FICO (3):** Credit scoring monopoly with 29% Scores revenue growth driven by price increases, not AI. Software Platform ARR +33% is the AI signal but it's a minority of revenue. Regulatory headwinds (Senate investigation, FHFA) create uncertainty.
- **APPN (3):** Low-code process automation benefiting from government wins. 18% growth with profitability inflection. But historical execution inconsistency limits confidence in sustained tailwind capture.
- **AI (2):** Revenue collapsed -46% YoY. Despite "AI" in the name, the company is losing share to hyperscaler AI platforms and PLTR. Federal/defense bookings are growing (+134%) but commercial business is in freefall. The sub-sector is strong but C3.ai is not positioned to capture it.

**Competitive Moat**

- **PLTR (5):** Ontology layer means all enterprise data is modeled in Palantir's schema — replacing Palantir requires re-modeling years of institutional knowledge. Government classification (FedRAMP, IL-5, classified environments) took years to obtain and cannot be replicated quickly. Net dollar retention 139%. US commercial RDV $4.38B (+145% YoY). 180 deals ≥$1M in Q4 alone. This is the deepest moat in the sector.
- **VEEV (5):** Life sciences vertical monopoly with 84% subscription revenue. 125+ Vault CRM customers live, including 10 of top-20 biopharma globally committed. Regulatory compliance requirements in pharma create structural switching costs — changing your clinical trial data platform is a multi-year, FDA-audit-risk event. $6B FY2030 target from $3.2B implies management sees no credible competitor.
- **FICO (5):** FICO Score used in >90% of US lending decisions. 88% Scores segment operating margin. B2B pricing power demonstrated by 36% revenue growth driven primarily by per-score price increases from $0.60 to $10.00 over 5 years. Embedded in every mortgage, auto loan, and credit card decision. This is a regulatory-embedded monopoly.
- **SNOW (4):** Cross-cloud data sharing creates ecosystem lock-in — moving from Snowflake would break hundreds of partner data connections. Data Marketplace/Clean Rooms create network effects. RPO $9.77B (+42%) signals strong forward commitments. But open table format adoption (Iceberg) and Databricks growing 2x faster (55% vs 27%) prevent a 5. Switching costs are real but not as deep as PLTR's ontology or VEEV's regulatory lock-in.
- **DDOG (4):** 84% of customers use 2+ products; 55% use 4+ products. Multi-product adoption creates deep integration switching costs. 603 customers above $1M ARR (+31% YoY). Leading observability platform with broadest product surface area (20+ products). But open-source alternatives (Prometheus, Grafana) and cloud-native monitoring create competitive pressure at the low end.
- **NOW (4):** 98% renewal rate is exceptional. Enterprise workflow platform deeply embedded in IT operations at 2,100+ $1M+ ACV customers. ITSM market leader with expanding surface area (HR, Finance, Security). But Microsoft competition is real — KeyBanc flagged AI disruption risk and downgraded.
- **CRM (4):** Largest CRM platform with 150K+ customers. Informatica acquisition adds data integration moat. cRPO $35.1B (+16% YoY). Deep ecosystem (AppExchange, MuleSoft, Tableau) creates switching costs. But organic growth deceleration to ~7-8% suggests the moat isn't translating into acceleration.
- **MDB (4):** Atlas document database with vector search for AI applications. 121% NRR. Largest TCV deal in history (>$100M EA) signed in Q4. But serverless database competitors and CRO departure create near-term uncertainty. Open-source MongoDB Community edition creates a floor but also a ceiling on pricing power.
- **SNOW (4), DDOG (4), MDB (4):** All have high but not unassailable switching costs. Data gravity is real but competitors exist and are scaling.
- **PEGA (3):** Enterprise low-code with deep financial services penetration. Backlog exceeded $2B (+28% YoY). Virginia $2B trade secret verdict vacated removes legal overhang. But low-code is increasingly competitive (Microsoft Power Platform, Appian, ServiceNow).
- **ZETA (3):** 120% NRR is strong. 184 super-scaled customers (≥$1M, +24% YoY). Proprietary data cloud with 2B+ consumer profiles. But AI-powered marketing is a crowded space and switching costs are moderate — advertisers can test multiple platforms simultaneously.
- **TWLO (3):** Communications platform with 100% usage-based revenue. ISV ecosystem (+26% growth) and developer adoption create moderate switching costs. But CPaaS is increasingly commoditized, with carrier pass-through fees compressing margins.
- **ESTC (3):** Elasticsearch is the de facto search engine for log analytics and increasingly for AI search/RAG. 3,000+ AI customers. But open-source Elasticsearch, AWS OpenSearch, and cloud-native alternatives limit pricing power. 112% NRR is adequate but not exceptional.
- **PATH (3):** RPA installed base of $1.85B ARR creates some switching costs. But the category faces existential risk from agentic AI — if AI agents can automate workflows without RPA scripting, UiPath's moat erodes. 110% NRR is stable but not expanding.
- **APPN (2):** Low-code platform with 114% cloud NRR (new metric, limited comparability). Government wins (US Army enterprise agreement) provide some moat. But historically inconsistent execution and smaller scale than PEGA/NOW limit competitive position. Switching costs are real but replicable.
- **AI (1):** No durable moat. Revenue declining -46% YoY demonstrates customers are leaving. GAAP gross margin collapsed to 17%. Hyperscaler AI platforms (Azure AI, AWS Bedrock, Google Vertex) offer equivalent or superior functionality at lower cost with broader ecosystem integration. Core product is being bypassed. Federal/defense bookings are the sole remaining pocket of traction.

### 3a. SUB-SECTOR DYNAMICS

**AI/ML Platforms are the growth center.** The next 2-3 years of sector growth is overwhelmingly concentrated in AI infrastructure and deployment. PLTR (+70%), SNOW (+30%), DDOG (+29%), and ZETA (+30%) are all growing at rates that significantly outpace the broader software market (+14% per Gartner). The AI/ML platform sub-TAM is growing 35-40% CAGR — faster than any other segment.

**Cloud Data Warehouses/Lakehouses are consolidating into an oligopoly.** Snowflake and Databricks dominate, with the top 4 vendors holding ~68% share. The defining rivalry is SNOW vs. Databricks — both at ~$4B+ ARR, but Databricks growing 2x faster. Snowflake's response (Cortex AI, Iceberg support) is strategic but may not close the growth gap. Databricks' IPO (expected 2026) will be the most consequential sector event since Snowflake's own IPO.

**Business Intelligence is mature and being absorbed.** BI (7-9% CAGR) is the slowest-growing sub-sector. Microsoft Power BI + Salesforce/Tableau hold ~40%+ combined share. AI copilots are the new battleground, but BI is becoming a feature of data platforms rather than a standalone category. DOMO's strategic alternatives process is a symptom of this absorption.

**Data Governance is the fastest-growing compliance-driven segment.** EU AI Act (high-risk compliance deadline August 2026) is creating non-discretionary demand for governance tools. Collibra, Atlan, and Alation remain private — when they IPO or are acquired, it will validate this sub-sector's premium.

**Vector databases face "feature vs. product" risk.** The hottest emerging category (22-28% CAGR) is already being absorbed by platform vendors. Microsoft, MongoDB, Elastic, and PostgreSQL (pgvector) are embedding vector search as features, compressing pure-play premiums. Pinecone at ~$27M revenue and ~$750M valuation may never reach standalone scale.

**Platform absorption is accelerating across the stack.** Databricks (10 acquisitions since April 2024) and Snowflake (8 acquisitions) are systematically absorbing the modern data stack — from ingestion to governance to AI/ML to observability. Point solutions face existential risk. The one exception: Palantir's ontology layer creates data gravity that is additive to, not competitive with, cloud data platforms.

### 3b. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE

**Who is gaining share:**

**Key head-to-head battles:**

### 3e. CONVICTION SUMMARY

The Data Analytics / AI Platforms sector in Q1 2026 presents a paradox that is the defining analytical challenge: operationally the strongest quarter in years, yet the worst stock performance since 2022. At least 12 of the 15 companies scored beat earnings expectations in their most recent report, many by wide margins. Revenue growth is accelerating for the AI leaders (PLTR +70%, SNOW +30%, DDOG +29%, ZETA +30%), profitability inflection points have arrived at UiPath, Appian, Twilio, and Zeta — yet every single stock trades 15-40% below its 52-week high. This disconnect between operational execution and stock performance creates the opportunity set.

The highest-conviction candidates, in order, are: **ZETA** (best growth-to-value ratio in the universe at 30% growth / 2.1x NTM revenue with 18 consecutive beat-and-raises), **PEGA** (cheapest profitable enterprise software on FCF basis at 11x NTM FCF with Rule of 40 = 45), **SNOW** (RPO +42% provides the strongest forward visibility in the data platform sector at 8.3x NTM revenue), and **DDOG** (best balanced Rule of 40 = 55 profile with record bookings +37%). These four represent the strongest combination of growth quality, profitability trajectory, and valuation dislocation. PLTR is the best company in the sector but at 35x NTM revenue with massive insider selling, the valuation risk is the highest in the universe — any growth deceleration below 50% would be catastrophic for equity holders.

What would change our mind: if ZETA's organic growth ex-Marigold drops below 20% in Q1 FY26, the beat-and-raise narrative breaks. If Databricks files its S-1 at an implied valuation below $100B, SNOW faces multiple compression. If DDOG's FY26 growth actually decelerates to 19% (the guide), the 9.8x multiple is expensive. And if FHFA mandates FICO pricing concessions, the highest-margin business in the universe loses its defining characteristic. The sector-wide AI monetization skepticism currently suppressing multiples will be tested by Q1 2026 earnings beginning April 22 (IBM, NOW) — if AI product revenue (Now Assist, Cortex AI, AIP, Agentforce) collectively accelerates, the multiple compression reverses. If it doesn't, the "trough of disillusionment" thesis gains credibility and further compression is likely.
