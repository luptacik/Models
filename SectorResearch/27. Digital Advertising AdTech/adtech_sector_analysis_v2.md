### Score Rationale — Walled Garden & Social Additions

**META (24/25 — highest score in the universe).** Meta is functionally a pure-play advertising company at 97.6% ad revenue concentration. Q4 revenue grew 24.3% YoY to $58.1B with 82% gross margins and 51.6% non-GAAP operating margins. Advantage+ AI at $60B+ run-rate with the AI video generation tool alone at a $10B run-rate demonstrates that AI is not a future story but current realized revenue. 65% of advertisers are already using Advantage+. The FTC antitrust threat diminished materially in November 2025 when Judge Boasberg ruled in Meta's favor. At 5.8x EV/NTM Revenue for 23% guided growth with 21.7% FCF margin, the valuation is reasonable. The only reason Meta doesn't score 25 is Reality Labs' $19.2B annual loss, which is a persistent capital allocation drag even if separable from the ad business.

**AMZN Advertising (23/25).** Scoring on the ad segment: $68.6B TTM revenue growing 23% YoY with five consecutive quarters of 19%+ growth. Retail media is the fastest-growing major segment (18% sector growth) and Amazon captures 75-80% of US retail media share. The Complete TV tool unifying Prime Video, Netflix, and Paramount+ buying is a competitive weapon no independent can replicate. Rufus AI (300M+ users) creates a structural path to AI-native commerce advertising. The valuation score is constrained to 3 because full-company FCF is depressed by $200B projected 2026 capex — the ad business itself is growing faster than any pure-play AdTech name except APP. If you could buy the ad segment standalone at a reasonable multiple, it would score 5.

**GOOGL (22/25).** Still the dominant search advertiser at $300B TTM ad revenue, with Q4 Search ads growing +17% YoY. The moat remains extraordinary — ~86% global search share. But the tailwind score is capped at 4 because AI search disruption is real and quantifiable: AI Overviews now appear on 48% of queries, 69% of searches end without a click, and Morgan Stanley estimates choice screens alone from the search antitrust case could cost Google $15-25B in annual ad revenue. The ad-tech antitrust remedy is a direct revenue risk — Judge Brinkema could order AdX divestiture. Google is adapting (monetizing AI Overviews, AI Max) but the competitive pressure is visible in YouTube's +9% growth (below expectations) and Google Network declining -1.6%. At 7.3x EV/NTM Revenue, valuation is fair for a mega-cap with these headwinds.

**RDDT (22/25 — the glaring omission from v1).** This is arguably the second most interesting growth story in the entire universe after APP. Revenue grew +70% YoY to $726M with 91.2% gross margin (highest in the group) and 31.0% FCF margin. Advertiser count grew +75% YoY and ARPU grew +42% — both monetization vectors are expanding simultaneously, which is rare. The Reddit Max AI campaign product is delivering 17% CPA reduction in alpha tests. Data licensing revenue ($36M per quarter) is a unique second revenue stream monetizing the content moat for AI training. A $1B buyback was announced. The moat scores 4 (not 5) because Reddit's content is user-generated and theoretically replicable, though the community network effects are real. Valuation at 7.1x EV/NTM Revenue on 52-54% guided Q1 growth is a growth-adjusted multiple of 0.14x — reasonable for the growth rate. SBC at 17.6% is the primary drawback.

**PINS (15/25).** Decent business with 80% gross margin and 29.7% FCF margin, but execution stumbles force the management score down: Q4 revenue miss, Q1 EBITDA guidance missed consensus by ~20%, and the stock plunged 22% on results. Elliott Investment Management committed $1B in convertibles and secured board representation — typically a catalyst, but it also signals underlying operational problems requiring activist intervention. 15% workforce reduction in January is both a cost move and an acknowledgment of over-hiring. SBC at 20.8% is the highest in the group. The tvScientific acquisition adds CTV capability but is unproven. Watch, don't pass.

**MSFT Advertising (14/25 — DNP on ad segment only).** This scoring is on the advertising business only — Microsoft Corporation as a whole is obviously an exceptional business. But as an AdTech investment, Microsoft advertising is deteriorating: search ex-TAC growth decelerated from +21% → +16% → +10% over three quarters with Q3 guided to "high single digits." Xandr DSP was officially closed in February 2026 — advertisers migrating to TTD, DV360, and Amazon DSP. The AdTech segment lacks a coherent strategy; Microsoft is retreating to owned inventory (Bing, Xbox, LinkedIn, Copilot). DNP for AdTech exposure — if you want Microsoft, buy it for cloud and AI, not advertising.

**SNAP (11/25 — DNP).** North American DAU declining -6% is a structural problem. Q1 revenue guidance lowered below Street. 17.1% SBC dilution. Net debt of $1.24B. Irenic Capital activist campaign launched March 2026 proposing AR glasses spinoff and 21% workforce cut — but Spiegel/Murphy retain supermajority voting control, meaning activists cannot force change. The management score of 2 reflects years of execution disappointment relative to capability. DNP.

## SECTION 3: NARRATIVE REPORT (Updated highlights)

### 3a. Sub-Sector Dynamics — Walled Garden Commentary Added

The walled gardens are not monolithic. **META and AMZN advertising are accelerating** (24% and 23% ad growth respectively) while **GOOGL Search is growing in line (17%) but YouTube is decelerating (9%) and Network is shrinking (-1.6%)**. MSFT advertising is structurally weakening with search ex-TAC growth collapsing from 21% to 10% and Xandr DSP closed. This is not a uniform "Big Tech wins everything" picture — Amazon is gaining share from Google in search advertising (Amazon product search growing +17.6% vs. Google's +7.6%), Meta is gaining share in AI-automated performance advertising, and Microsoft is losing share in both search and programmatic buying.

**The triopoly share number (~55.8% of ex-China ad spend) is slightly declining within digital** as TikTok, retail media, and emerging platforms (RDDT) gain share. This is a meaningful point — the walled gardens are gaining absolute dollars but losing relative share within a growing pie. Independent AdTech is not being eliminated; it's being forced into defensible niches (CTV SSP, identity, measurement, AI performance).

### 3b. Competitive Landscape — Updated

**New share gainers with evidence:**
- **Meta:** +24% ad growth at $196B scale is remarkable; Advantage+ at $60B run-rate suggests AI is compounding advantages
- **Amazon:** +23% ad growth with 5 consecutive quarters above 19%; Complete TV tool creating cross-platform buying advantage
- **Reddit:** +70% revenue, +75% advertisers, +42% ARPU — fastest monetization expansion in the group

**New share losers with evidence:**
- **Microsoft:** Search ex-TAC growth 21% → 16% → 10% deceleration; Xandr DSP closed February 2026
- **Snap:** North American DAU declining -6%; Q1 2026 guidance lowered below Street

**Key head-to-head battles (expanded):**
- **GOOGL Search vs. ChatGPT/Perplexity** — the most consequential battle in all of advertising. ChatGPT has 800M+ WAU, Perplexity 780M monthly queries tripling YoY. ChatGPT ads launched February 2026 with Criteo as the first ad-tech partner.
- **META Advantage+ vs. APP AXON** — both are AI-driven performance engines, but META operates within its walled garden with 3B+ user data moat while APP operates on the open internet. The ecommerce expansion is APP's direct challenge to META's dominance.
- **AMZN DSP vs. TTD** — Amazon DSP is now "fully featured" post-Xandr closure, actively winning advertisers migrating from Microsoft. This is a direct threat to TTD's open-internet DSP position at the worst possible time for TTD (governance crisis).

**Observations:**
- **RDDT is the best financial profile in the group after APP** — 91.2% GM, 70% growth, 31% FCF margin. Only the SBC (17.6%) and still-early scale prevent it from matching APP's quality score.
- **META's combination of 24% growth + 82% GM + 51.6% op margin at $200B scale is genuinely exceptional** — no other company in the world delivers this.
- **SBC dispersion is enormous** — from APP/AMZN at ~3% to PINS at 20.8%. For FCF-based valuation, SBC adjustment changes rankings materially.
- **AMZN's 1.6% FCF margin is misleading** — it reflects $200B capex investment, not underlying economics. Ad segment alone likely has 40%+ operating margins.

### 3e. Conviction Summary — Revised

The full universe analysis sharpens the picture: **META and APP are the two highest-quality businesses in the group**, followed closely by AMZN advertising and RDDT. Adding the walled gardens doesn't displace APP from the top tier — it contextualizes it. APP at 87.9% gross margin and 72.1% FCF margin is operating at software economics that exceed even META (82% GM, 21.7% FCF); the difference is scale and diversification. META offers $200B of advertising revenue growing 24% at 52% operating margin with diminishing antitrust risk — this is arguably the best single business in public markets.

RDDT is the most interesting addition to the pass list. It was wrongly classified as a "benchmark only" name in v1 when in fact it fits the AdTech rubric cleanly and delivers the highest growth + highest gross margin combination in the entire universe. The 17.6% SBC dilution and 7.1x NTM Rev multiple are the two factors preventing a higher score, but the monetization trajectory (advertiser count +75%, ARPU +42% simultaneously) is genuinely rare.

The watchlist and DNP lists remain largely unchanged from v1, with the addition of PINS (activist watch), MSFT advertising (deteriorating), and SNAP (structural execution problems).
