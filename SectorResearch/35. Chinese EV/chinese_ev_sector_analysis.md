### SCORING RATIONALE — DIMENSION BY DIMENSION

#### Dimension 1: Sub-sector Tailwind

**BYD — 5.** Mass market + PHEV dominant position in the highest-volume segment. 27% domestic NEV share (#1). Exported 1.05M vehicles in FY2025 (+140% YoY), the most credible overseas story in the sector. Policy tailwind from dual credit system (BYD is largest beneficiary). Growing faster than market despite already being the leader.

**Geely — 4.** Multi-brand portfolio (Geely, Galaxy, Zeekr, Lynk & Co) covering mass to premium. NEV sales +90% YoY to 1.69M, NEV mix reached 55.8%. Second-largest automaker in China, overtaking Volkswagen. Overseas expansion credible (Zeekr in 13+ European markets). Docked from 5: EU tariff at 28.8% is a real headwind; Polestar underperforming.

**CATL — 4.** Battery manufacturing is the sector's enabling sub-sector — 39.2% global share, 9th consecutive year #1. Installations +39% YoY. Energy storage systems (ESS) providing diversification. Sodium-ion commercial production started Jan 2026. International expansion (Hungary, Indonesia). Docked from 5: overcapacity at Tier 2/3 pressures pricing; lithium price recovery (+35% YTD) is a near-term cost headwind.

**XPeng — 4.** Tech/ADAS-focused EV in a segment where autonomous driving is becoming the primary competitive battleground. Delivery growth +126% YoY (429K units). VW partnership validates technology stack. MONA sub-brand opened mass market. Docked from 5: Q1 2026 guidance was weak (deliveries -30-35% QoQ), and ADAS commoditization risk from BYD's free "God's Eye" offering.

**Leapmotor — 4.** Mass market BEV in the highest-volume price tier (<RMB 200K). Deliveries +103% YoY to 597K — fastest growth among major OEMs. Stellantis partnership provides European distribution channel (CKD plant in Spain). Docked from 5: core segment (<RMB 150K) most exposed to subsidy expiry; Poland CKD paused on political pressure.

**NIO — 3.** Premium BEV position is solid but niche. Deliveries +47% YoY to 326K, but this is below-market growth for its scaling stage. Multi-brand expansion (Onvo, Firefly) broadens TAM but dilutes brand focus. Battery swap creates unique positioning but is capital-intensive. No clear overseas strategy — Firefly Europe registrations were 54 units in Feb 2026. Premium segment faces direct competition from Xiaomi SU7, AITO, and Zeekr.

**Li Auto — 2.** EREV was a brilliant bridge technology, but PHEV/EREV growth decelerated sharply from 82% (2024) to ~20% (2025). Li Auto's FY2025 deliveries declined 18.8% YoY — the only major OEM with negative delivery growth. Seven consecutive months of YoY delivery declines. EREV competitive moat eroding as BYD DM-i/DM-p and AITO deliver comparable range-extender solutions. Pure BEV L-series has not gained traction. No meaningful international expansion.

**Xiaomi — 4.** Fastest-selling new EV brand in history: 411K units in under 2 years. SU7 outsold Tesla Model 3 in China. Consumer electronics ecosystem (IoT, phones) provides a unique acquisition funnel. 2026 target of 550K units (+34%) is credible. Docked from 5: still single-model dependent (YU7 not yet delivering at scale); no overseas EV sales.

**Seres/AITO — 3.** Huawei's HarmonyOS cockpit + ADS autonomous driving is a genuinely strong platform. AITO M9 is the #1 vehicle in the RMB 500K+ segment for 21 consecutive months. Sales +56% YoY. But: ~90% revenue dependent on Huawei, who runs the same program with 4 other OEMs (Chery, BAIC, JAC, SAIC). Seres is a hardware OEM, not a platform — Huawei captures the technology value.

**Hesai — 5.** LiDAR is in the fastest-growing sub-sector (ADAS, ~29% CAGR). Hesai is the global #1 with 61% robotaxi LiDAR share. Shipments doubling to 3.0-3.5M units in 2026. Selected as LiDAR partner for NVIDIA DRIVE AGX Hyperion 10 platform. 24 OEM design wins across 120+ vehicle programs. BYD's God's Eye uses LiDAR at scale (costs brought to ~$139/unit), which expands Hesai's TAM rather than threatening it.

**Horizon Robotics — 5.** ADAS chips are the "picks and shovels" play for the sector's defining technology trend. Journey 6P (560 TOPS) positioned against NVIDIA Orin-X. 32-49% China ADAS chip share. 42 auto brand partnerships including VW ($2.3B Carizon JV), BYD, Li Auto, Geely. L2+ penetration expected to reach 70% by 2030. Regulatory tailwind as L3 approvals begin.

#### Dimension 2: Competitive Moat

**BYD — 5.** The most vertically integrated auto company in the world. Controls: Blade Battery (world's #2 battery maker), semiconductors (IGBT/SiC), electric motors (Fudi Power), ADAS (God's Eye), die-casting, and now mining. Full ADAS hardware costs ~$2,105 vs. Tesla FSD $2,360. Achieves ~20.5% gross margin while giving ADAS away for free on RMB 100K vehicles. Battery cell cost reportedly $44/kWh — 48% below industry average. This cost advantage is the single strongest moat in the Chinese EV sector.

**Geely — 4.** Multi-brand portfolio provides optionality (Geely for mass, Galaxy for mid, Zeekr for premium, Lynk for crossover). Volvo/Zeekr heritage provides engineering credibility. 800V architecture, L3 autonomous driving license (Zeekr 9X in Hangzhou). Solid-state battery verification planned 2026. Not a 5: no single technology as dominant as BYD's vertical integration; brand portfolio complexity could dilute.

**CATL — 5.** Battery technology moat is genuine and deep. 39.2% global share is 2.4x the #2 (BYD at 16.4%). Shenxing ultra-fast charge battery, sodium-ion commercial production, Choco-Swap infrastructure. 10M+ cumulative Journey chip integration (via partnerships). R&D spending dominance creates a compounding knowledge advantage. Customer switching costs are high — qualification cycles for automotive-grade batteries are 18-24 months. The only meaningful competitive threat is BYD's vertical integration bypassing CATL entirely.

**XPeng — 4.** Full-stack XNGP autonomous driving with in-house Turing AI chip is a genuine 2-3 year technology lead over most peers. VW partnership (first Western OEM to adopt China-developed AD software) is the strongest third-party validation of any Chinese EV company's technology. VLA 2.0 system where "passengers can hardly distinguish AI from human driving" (CEO claim, unverified). Services revenue +122% YoY in Q4. Docked from 5: BYD's God's Eye commoditizes basic ADAS, and Huawei ADS 4.0 is at least comparable at the high end.

**Leapmotor — 2.** Mass market with no clear technology differentiation. Competing primarily on price in the most commoditized segment (<RMB 200K). Stellantis partnership provides distribution but not a product moat. Alibaba Qwen AI integration in A10 is table stakes. No proprietary battery, chip, or autonomous driving technology. Customers choose on price/value — no meaningful switching costs. The 14.5% gross margin, while improved, is below peers with stronger moats.

**NIO — 4.** Battery swap network (2,500+ stations, 33M+ swaps) is a genuine physical infrastructure moat that cannot be replicated without years of capital expenditure. NIO House community creates real switching costs — brand loyalty is the highest among Chinese EV buyers. In-house Shenji chip subsidiary raised RMB 2.3B at RMB 10B valuation, validating chip IP. Docked from 5: battery swap is capital-intensive and locks NIO into a physical infrastructure model that limits flexibility; open charging partnerships with competitors dilute network exclusivity.

**Li Auto — 3.** EREV technology was a genuine moat when it launched — 1,400km range eliminated range anxiety when charging infrastructure was sparse. But BYD DM-i/DM-p, AITO, and multiple competitors now offer comparable EREV solutions. Customer satisfaction was high but is declining as product lineup ages. $1B buyback signals management knows value is cheap, but buybacks don't create product moats. Pure BEV L-series has not differentiated. Cash position (RMB 101.2B) is a financial moat but not a competitive one.

**Xiaomi — 3.** Consumer electronics ecosystem (500M+ MIUI users) provides an acquisition funnel that no traditional OEM can match. HyperOS integration across phone-car-home is a legitimate platform play. In-house battery tech, motors, HyperCasting, and smart driving investment (RMB 13B R&D in 2025) is serious. But: SU7/YU7 are still fundamentally competitive products, not category-defining. 15,000 locked orders in 34 minutes for SU7 facelift shows demand, but this is hype-cycle behavior, not demonstrated long-term retention. Two fire incidents + safety recall are concerning for a brand built on consumer electronics trust.

**Seres/AITO — 2.** Seres has no meaningful moat independent of Huawei. The AITO brand, HarmonyOS cockpit, and ADS autonomous driving are all Huawei IP. Huawei runs identical programs with Chery (Luxeed), BAIC (Stelato), JAC (Maextro), SAIC. If Huawei shifts priority to a different OEM partner, Seres has minimal standalone value. The M9's #1 position in >RMB 500K segment is real, but it's Huawei's brand halo, not Seres' product moat.

**Hesai — 4.** 61% global robotaxi LiDAR market share. 120+ vehicle program design wins across 24 OEMs. NVIDIA Hyperion 10 platform partnership creates an ecosystem lock-in for next-gen ADAS architectures. Technology leadership in automotive-grade LiDAR is defensible — qualification cycles are long and OEMs don't switch mid-program. Exclusive Li Auto supply contract demonstrates stickiness. Docked from 5: LiDAR faces existential risk from camera-only approaches (Tesla, BYD partial); chipification driving costs below $200 could commoditize hardware.

**Horizon Robotics — 4.** Open platform model (chip + reference design + tools) contrasts favorably with Huawei's closed full-stack approach. Journey 5 outperforms NVIDIA Orin-X in frames-per-second efficiency despite lower raw TOPS. 42 auto brand partnerships. VW $2.3B Carizon JV is the strongest validation. But: TSMC 7nm fabrication dependence is a critical vulnerability to geopolitical disruption. Black Sesame is a credible challenger (only Chinese chip to clear US DoD review). BYD developing in-house ADAS chips reduces Horizon's TAM at the largest OEM.

#### Dimension 3: Business Model Quality

**BYD — 4.** Revenue RMB 804B but only +3.5% YoY — disappointing for the sector leader. Auto gross margin 20.5%, down 170bps YoY. Net income RMB 32.6B, -19% YoY. Net margin 4.1% (vs. 5.2% prior year). The price war is compressing even BYD's margins. However: 4.6M deliveries demonstrates scale that creates operating leverage; R&D spend of RMB 63.4B is an investment in future competitive position. Docked from 5: revenue growth nearly flat and profit declining — price war pressure is real. BYD's revenue deceleration is a concern given ~20% electronics segment exposure.

**Geely — 4.** Revenue RMB 345.2B (+25% YoY, record). Net income RMB 16.85B (beat consensus). Core net profit +36% YoY. Gross margin 16.2%. NEV mix 55.8% and rising. Deliveries 3.02M (+39% YoY). Net cash position of RMB 58.8B. Strong all-around. Docked from 5: gross margin at 16.2% is below BYD/Li Auto levels; multi-brand complexity creates cost structure questions; full FY2025 detailed P&L pending.

**CATL — 5.** Revenue RMB 423.7B (+17% YoY). Net profit RMB 72.2B (+42% YoY) — dwarfs all Chinese automakers combined. ~25.5% gross margin. Proposed ~50% dividend payout (RMB 31.5B). Installations 661 GWh (+39%). CATL is the most profitable company in the entire Chinese EV ecosystem by a wide margin. Capital allocation is excellent: dividends + reinvestment + global expansion. The only question is whether lithium price recovery (+35% YTD) pressures input costs, but CATL has consistently managed raw material cycles.

**XPeng — 3.** Revenue RMB 76.72B (+88% YoY). Q4 gross margin 21.3% (record, vs. 14.4% Q4 2024). Q4 vehicle margin 13.0%. First-ever quarterly GAAP net profit (RMB 383M in Q4). FY still a net loss of RMB 1.14B. Cash position RMB 47.66B — solid runway. Delivery growth +126% YoY is exceptional. Services revenue +122% YoY indicates software monetization beginning. But: Q1 2026 guidance is weak (deliveries -30-35% QoQ), and FY profitability not yet achieved. Still pre-FCF-positive on an annual basis.

**Leapmotor — 3.** Revenue RMB 64.73B (+101% YoY). First-ever annual net profit: RMB 540M. Gross margin 14.5%, Q4 record 15.0%. Deliveries 597K (+103% YoY). FCF positive. This is a legitimate profitability inflection — second Chinese EV startup to achieve annual profit after Li Auto. But: 14.5% gross margin is below the 15-20% range I'd want for a 4; net margin is razor-thin; core segment (<RMB 150K) has the least margin buffer in the price war.

**NIO — 3.** Q4 revenue RMB 34.65B (+76% YoY). Q4 gross margin 17.5% (vs. 11.7% Q4 2024). Q4 vehicle margin 18.1%. First-ever quarterly GAAP net profit (~$40M in Q4). Deliveries 326K (+47% YoY). Cash RMB 36.7B + recent $1.16B equity raise. The Q4 profitability inflection is real but: prior raises at distressed terms impaired existing shareholders; annual profitability not yet achieved; multi-brand expansion (Onvo, Firefly) creates new cost centers before the core brand is sustainably profitable. FCF data not available.

**Li Auto — 2.** Revenue RMB 112.3B (-22.3% YoY). FY deliveries 406K (-18.8% YoY). Q4 net income RMB 20.2M (vs. RMB 3.5B prior year — a 99.4% collapse). FY operating margin -0.5% (vs. +4.9% in 2024). FY FCF negative RMB 12.8B. Gross margin 18.7% is still decent but deteriorating (vs. 20.5% in 2024). This is the sharpest earnings deterioration in the peer group. Cash position RMB 101.2B is a lifeline but not a quality signal when FCF is deeply negative. The EREV product cycle gap and failed MEGA launch explain the decline, but -22% revenue from a growth company is a 2 on any calibration.

**Xiaomi — 3.** EV segment revenue RMB 106.1B (+224% YoY). EV segment first annual operating profit: RMB 900M. 411K deliveries. But: Q4 EPS dropped significantly QoQ; one-off RMB 13.8B gain inflated headlines; 2026 analyst EPS estimates cut ~30% post-earnings. Stock fell ~11% post-earnings. The EV business is real and growing fast, but profitability at sub-1% margin on >RMB 100B revenue is fragile. Segment-level vehicle margin not disclosed — a red flag for transparency.

**Seres/AITO — 3.** FY2024 revenue RMB 145B (+305% YoY) with RMB 5.95B net profit (turnaround from RMB -24.5B loss). Jan-Feb 2026 sales +56% YoY. AITO M9 dominates the ultra-premium segment. But: FY2025 results not yet released; estimated gross margin ~27% (if accurate, best-in-class). Score carries low confidence due to data gaps. The structural risk is the Huawei dependency — Seres captures manufacturing margin while Huawei captures technology value.

**Hesai — 4.** FY2025 revenue RMB 3.03B (+46% YoY). First-ever full-year GAAP profitability: net income RMB 436M. Gross margin ~42%. Shipments 1.6M units, guiding 3.0-3.5M in 2026 (doubling). ATX LiDAR orders exceed 4M units. The business model is scaling with strong unit economics — LiDAR is moving from loss-making to cash-generating. Docked from 5: revenue base is still small (RMB 3B); hardware commoditization risk is real as costs fall below $200/unit.

**Horizon Robotics — 1.** Revenue RMB 3.76B (+58% YoY), ~64% gross margin, but deeply unprofitable. Operating loss significant. FCF negative. IPO was Oct 2024, so the company is very early in its monetization journey. The 64% gross margin is strong for a chip company, but revenue base is tiny relative to HKD 85B market cap. Until operating profitability is demonstrated, business model quality cannot score above 1 — the market is pricing in future scale that does not yet exist.

## SECTION 3: NARRATIVE REPORT

### 3a. SUB-SECTOR DYNAMICS

**The Chinese EV sector has entered a new phase: from growth-at-all-costs to sustainable economics.** The defining event of FY2025 was not BYD's record deliveries but the profitability inflection — NIO, XPeng, Leapmotor, and Hesai all achieved first-ever profits. This signals the sector is maturing from venture-funded disruption to genuine business viability. The next 2-3 years will separate durable franchises from those that were subsidized into existence.

**Sub-sector ranking by investment attractiveness:**

1. **ADAS/Autonomous Driving components (Hesai, Horizon Robotics)** — highest growth vector (~29% CAGR). LiDAR and ADAS chips are "picks and shovels" plays on the industry's defining technology trend. L3 regulatory approvals have begun (Changan SL03 and Arcfox Alpha S approved Dec 2025). BYD's God's Eye offering ADAS for free at the low end expands TAM rather than compressing it — it drives LiDAR adoption into mass-market price points.

2. **Battery manufacturing (CATL)** — most durable value capture point. Batteries represent 25-30% of EV value, and the top 2 (CATL + BYD) control 65% of Chinese installations. CATL's 42% profit growth demonstrates pricing power even as OEMs compete away their own margins. Sodium-ion commercialization (Jan 2026) opens a new cost tier. ESS diversification reduces pure-EV dependency.

3. **Multi-brand OEM platforms (Geely, BYD)** — scale winners. The market cannot sustain 100+ EV brands. From 500+ EV manufacturers in 2019, only ~100 remain. XPeng CEO predicts "only 5 will be left." Geely and BYD are positioned as survivors through brand portfolio breadth + scale + overseas production.

4. **Tech-differentiated OEMs (XPeng, NIO)** — technology licensing is the new monetization frontier. XPeng selling its AD stack to VW is the first instance of a Chinese EV company monetizing technology independent of vehicle sales. NIO's battery swap may eventually be spun off as a standalone business. These are option values on top of the auto business.

5. **Mass-market OEMs (Leapmotor, Li Auto)** — most exposed to price war. Gross margins of 14-18% leave minimal buffer. The subsidy expiry hit this segment hardest (-26% YoY in Jan-Feb 2026). Leapmotor's Stellantis relationship and Li Auto's cash fortress are the only differentiators preventing these from being commoditized.

**PHEV/EREV growth is decelerating sharply** — from 82% in 2024 to ~20% in 2025. This is the single most important sub-sector dynamic for Li Auto, whose thesis was built on EREV dominance. BYD's DM series now accounts for 6 of the top 10 PHEV models, and AITO's Huawei-powered EREVs are directly competitive with Li Auto at similar price points.

**Exports are the growth engine.** NEV exports surged to ~2.6M units in 2025 (+104% YoY), with China accounting for 40% of global EV exports. BYD alone exported 1M+ vehicles. The EU tariff shift from punitive duties to minimum price undertakings (Jan 2026) is structurally bullish — OEMs retain margin differential rather than EU customs capturing it. However, local production (Hungary, Spain, Thailand) is the medium-term solution to tariff risk.

### 3e. CONVICTION SUMMARY

The Chinese EV sector is bifurcating. The winners — companies with genuine technology moats, scale advantages, or irreplaceable supply chain positions — are pulling away from the pack. The losers are being ground down by the most intense price war in automotive history, where overcapacity of ~20M units forces even profitable players to sacrifice margins for volume.

**Geely is the highest-conviction name in this analysis.** At 0.5x P/Sales, ~7.6x forward P/E, record revenue, record profit, +90% NEV growth, and the most disciplined multi-brand management team in Chinese auto, Geely offers the best risk/reward in the sector. The Ford partnership discussion, Zeekr European expansion, and solid-state battery development provide multiple re-rating catalysts. This is the rare case where the cheapest stock is also the best-executing company — a combination that usually doesn't persist.

**CATL and Hesai are the highest-conviction plays on the sector's structural direction** — batteries and LiDAR are the two value-capture points that remain profitable while OEMs compete away margins. CATL's RMB 72.2B net profit (larger than all Chinese automakers combined) and Hesai's profitability inflection + NVIDIA platform win represent durable competitive positions. BYD remains the sector anchor — the most vertically integrated OEM globally with a cost advantage that is nearly impossible to replicate — though near-term revenue growth stagnation and profit decline warrant monitoring.

**What would change my mind:** If Geely's Q1 2026 results show margin compression below 14% (indicating multi-brand complexity is creating cost inefficiency), the execution thesis breaks. If Li Auto's L9 refresh generates >45K deliveries in its first full quarter, the value trap becomes a value opportunity and Li Auto should be upgraded to PASS. If Horizon Robotics demonstrates a quarter of operating profit, the business model quality score improves from 1 and the NO designation should be revisited. And sector-wide: if the Jan-Feb domestic demand softness (-26% YoY) extends into Q2 without recovery, the CAAM 19M NEV forecast for 2026 is at risk, and all names in the sector should be de-risked.
