# R3 financial metrics pass: memory semiconductor sector

**The memory semiconductor sector is in the midst of a historic AI-driven supercycle.** DRAM contract prices surged **80–95% QoQ in Q1 CY2026**, NAND prices rose **55–60% QoQ**, and every major IDM reports supply sold out through CY2026 and into CY2027. Across the 16-company universe, TTM revenue growth ranges from +9.5% (Everspin) to +196% (Micron's most recent quarter YoY), with the Big 3 IDMs — Micron, Samsung, SK Hynix — posting record or near-record operating margins. HBM demand for AI accelerators, enterprise SSD growth, and structural capacity reallocation from NAND to DRAM/HBM are the dominant cycle drivers. This data pull covers the most recent available filings as of April 1, 2026.

Before the data pull, five tickers required identity verification. All were resolved:

- **SNDK**: The original SanDisk was acquired by Western Digital in 2016 and delisted. A **new SanDisk Corporation** was spun off from WDC on **Feb 24, 2025** and trades on NASDAQ under the same ticker SNDK. It is a pure-play NAND/SSD company. WDC is now HDD-only. **New CIK: 0002023554.**
- **285A**: Confirmed as **Kioxia Holdings Corporation** (formerly Toshiba Memory). IPO'd on **Tokyo Stock Exchange Prime Market on Dec 18, 2024**. Reports in JPY. Fiscal year ends March 31.
- **603986.SS**: **GigaDevice Semiconductor** (兆易创新). Shanghai main board. NOR Flash #2 globally, plus SLC NAND, niche DRAM, MCUs. Reports in CNY.
- **688008.SS**: **Montage Technology** (澜起科技). Shanghai STAR Market. World's #1 memory interconnect chip supplier (DDR5 RCD, data buffers). Comparable to Rambus. Reports in CNY.
- **300223.SZ**: **Ingenic Semiconductor** (北京君正). Shenzhen ChiNext. Memory (SRAM/DRAM/NOR/NAND/eMMC via ISSI acquisition) + SoC processors. Reports in CNY.
- **6770.TW — ⚠️ TICKER MISMATCH CONFIRMED**: 6770.TW is **Powerchip Semiconductor Manufacturing Corp**, NOT Phison Electronics. **Phison Electronics trades as 8299.TWO on TPEX.** Data below is pulled for Phison (8299.TWO). This mismatch should be corrected in the sector engine.

## Sector-wide observations on cycle positioning

**Every company in this universe is in upcycle territory**, though at different stages. The Big 3 IDMs (Micron, Samsung, SK Hynix) plus Kioxia and SanDisk are in a **historic memory supercycle** driven by AI infrastructure. DRAM contract prices surged **80–95% in Q1 CY2026** — a record quarterly increase. NAND rose **55–60% QoQ**. All major suppliers report CY2026 capacity fully sold out, with some customers negotiating CY2027–2028 allocations. The structural drivers include HBM demand consuming DRAM wafer capacity, enterprise SSD growth for AI inference, and disciplined industry capex keeping supply growth (DRAM ~16%, NAND ~17% YoY) well below demand. The Taiwanese and Chinese companies are benefiting as price-takers, with Nanya's +357% Q4 YoY growth illustrating the leverage smaller DRAM players have in a tight market. Macronix remains the laggard — still unprofitable — but is expected to turn profitable in CY2026 as NOR Flash pricing catches up. **Cycle peak risk is real**: multiple analysts flag that $25B+ annual capex commitments by Micron and Samsung could create future oversupply if AI demand moderates.
