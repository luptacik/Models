## SECTION 3: NARRATIVE REPORT

### 3a. Sub-Sector Dynamics

The optics/connectivity sector is experiencing its most powerful demand cycle in history, driven by hyperscaler AI capex guided at $635-665B for 2026 — a 60-70% increase over 2025. Datacom optics is a ~$18B market growing at ~20% CAGR, but the growth is concentrated in a few sub-sectors that are dramatically outperforming.

**Pluggable transceivers (800G/1.6T) are the center of gravity.** This sub-segment commands ~$16-18B and is where the most intense competition, the highest growth rates (+60% in 2025), and the most extreme valuations are concentrated. The 800G→1.6T transition is the defining product cycle. Zhongji Innolight commands ~80% of global 1.6T market share and ~50% of 800G — the single most dominant position in the sector. Among US-listed names, Coherent (~20%+ NVIDIA wallet share) and Lumentum (EML laser supply monopoly) hold the most defensible positions.

**Silicon photonics is the cross-cutting infrastructure thesis.** SiPh penetration in 800G is ~30% and rising to ~50% in 1.6T. Tower Semiconductor's SiPho revenue doubled (+115% YoY) and new capacity is >70% reserved through 2028. BESI's photonics packaging orders are surging. The SiPh supply chain (TSEM, COHR's SiPh foundry, GlobalFoundries/AMF) is a choke point that determines 1.6T-and-beyond production scalability. Samsung's March 30 SiPh foundry announcement injected competitive uncertainty — this is the most important structural development to monitor.

**Coherent/DCI is the fastest-growing systems segment.** DCI represented ~40% of optical transport revenue in 2025, growing 40-50% YoY. Direct cloud provider WDM equipment purchases grew ~50%. Ciena's WaveLogic 6 Extreme (only 1.6Tb/s coherent solution) and Nokia's PSE-6s are the technology leaders. Pluggable coherent (400ZR/ZR+) is displacing embedded — Microsoft deployed 50,000+ 400ZR modules.

**Co-packaged optics (CPO) is the 3-5 year structural threat.** CPO is currently ~$98-121M market (tiny), growing at ~36% CAGR. Broadcom's Tomahawk 6 with integrated CPO is deployed at Meta — the first real commercial CPO. Near-term impact is minimal (Cignal AI: "no material impact to pluggable in next 3 years"), but CPO threatens the existential business model of pure module assemblers long-term. Companies with SiPh foundry capability (COHR, TSEM) or laser positions (LITE) can pivot to supply CPO optical engines; pure assemblers cannot.

**Where the next 2-3 years of growth is concentrated:** (1) 1.6T pluggable transceiver ramp — exceeding 5M units in 2026, (2) SiPh foundry capacity expansion — the bottleneck for 1.6T volumes, (3) DCI coherent systems — 800ZR entering volume, (4) EML laser production — constrained through 2027+, and (5) early CPO deployments — Broadcom/Meta leading.

### 3b. Competitive Landscape

**Share gainers:**
- *Lumentum (LITE):* From near-trough to record revenue in 12 months. EML output up 8x. NVIDIA designated LITE as the Vera Rubin light engine supplier. Revenue growing +65% — the fastest rate among profitable US-listed names.
- *Tower Semiconductor (TSEM):* SiPh revenue from $106M to $228M in one year. Every major transceiver company needs SiPh foundry access, and TSEM is the leading independent option. 400Gbps/lane demo with Coherent is a technology validation event.
- *Marvell (MRVL):* Custom AI silicon doubled. Coherent DSP leadership maintained with Ara X 1.6T. Celestial AI acquisition creates photonic fabric differentiation no competitor can match without years of development.

**Share losers / challenged positions:**
- *Nokia (NOK):* Optical Networks +17% CC in Q4 sounds solid, but 2026 guidance disappointed (operating profit midpoint below consensus, stock fell 6-10%). Post-Infinera integration creates execution risk. Optical is a fraction of total Nokia and gets diluted by mobile networks weakness.
- *Semtech (SMTC):* Only +9% overall growth in the strongest optical demand cycle ever. Data center segment growing fast (+58%) but from a small base, overwhelmed by legacy Sierra Wireless drag. Goodwill impairments signal prior M&A mistakes.
- *AAOI:* Gaining orders (+83% FY2025 growth) but not yet gaining profitability. 91% customer concentration is the weakest position in the group. Still GAAP loss-making. Gaining transceiver market share but without the moat to sustain it.

**Key head-to-head battles:**
- **COHR vs. LITE:** Not direct competitors — COHR is multi-platform (SiPh + InP + VCSEL modules + materials) while LITE is laser/light-engine dominant. They are complementary more than competitive, which is why NVIDIA invested $2B in each. The real question is which technology platform (EML vs. CW+SiPh) dominates at 1.6T. If CW+SiPh wins, COHR benefits more. If EML remains dominant, LITE benefits more. Both are positioned for either outcome.
- **TSEM vs. Samsung vs. GlobalFoundries:** The SiPh foundry war is the most important competitive battle for the sector's infrastructure layer. TSEM has first-mover advantage with PH18 and customer prepayments. Samsung announced SiPh foundry capability March 30 — triggered sector-wide selloff. GlobalFoundries acquired AMF (largest pure-play SiPh foundry). Winner determines who supplies the wafers for 1.6T and CPO.
- **Innolight vs. COHR/LITE (Western vs. Chinese):** Chinese vendors hold >60% of 800G transceiver market at 20-25% lower ASPs. But NVIDIA is actively building Western optical supply chain redundancy (LITE/COHR investments). If BIS extends Entity List to Chinese optical firms, ~60% of NVIDIA's transceiver supply would be disrupted. This is the most important tail risk in the sector.

**M&A read:**
- *Likely acquirors:* Marvell (track record — Celestial AI, XConn), NVIDIA (could escalate from investments to acquisitions), Broadcom (CPO integration), Applied Materials/Lam Research (BESI.AS — reported Reuters, March 13).
- *Likely targets:* AAOI ($2-3B market cap, US-based vertically integrated, ideal strategic acquisition at $4-5B), BESI.AS (speculated €270-300/share premium, Morgan Stanley advising), ams-OSRAM (post-divestiture pure-play photonics at CHF 808M market cap — cheap), POET Technologies (small-cap optical interposer).
- *BESI M&A is the highest-probability near-term event:* Reuters reported Applied Materials and Lam Research interest. Applied already owns 9%. Morgan Stanley advising BESI. If confirmed, expect 40-60% premium to current price.
