# Optics / Connectivity R2 catalyst sweep: 25 names across an AI supercycle

The Optics / Connectivity sector is experiencing its most powerful demand cycle in history, driven by hyperscaler AI infrastructure capex projected to exceed **$470B in 2026**. All 25 companies in this universe reported earnings beats in their most recent quarters, with 20 of 25 raising guidance. The NVIDIA $4B strategic investment split between Coherent and Lumentum (March 2, 2026) served as a sector-wide validation event. However, **insider selling is pervasive** — 22 of 25 companies show net insider sales over the past 90 days — and valuations across the group are historically stretched, with many names trading at 50–100x+ trailing earnings after 100–500% rallies. The primary risk vector is a tariff escalation (Section 232 deadline April 14) combined with Samsung's March 30 silicon photonics foundry announcement, which triggered a sector-wide 9–14% selloff into quarter-end.

## Sector sentiment summary

**The Optics / Connectivity sector is in the grip of a generational AI-driven demand supercycle, with virtually every company reporting accelerating revenue growth and order backlogs extending into 2027.** The March 2, 2026 NVIDIA $4B investment split between Coherent and Lumentum was the single most important sector catalyst in the past 90 days, serving as a definitive endorsement of the optical supply chain's centrality to AI infrastructure. OFC 2026 (March 17–19) further validated the 1.6T-to-3.2T transceiver roadmap, with multiple companies demonstrating 400G/lane technology. However, the March 30 Samsung silicon photonics foundry announcement and persistent tariff uncertainty (Section 232 deadline April 14) have introduced a new competitive and regulatory risk dimension, triggering a sharp late-quarter selloff that shaved 9–14% from most names.

**Sharpest fundamental-vs-price divergences to monitor:**

- **ams-OSRAM (AMS.SW) — Potential deep value.** Market cap just CHF 808M despite €5B+ annual design wins, restructuring execution ahead of schedule, and €670M in imminent divestiture proceeds. RSI at 16.4 (severely oversold). The Infineon deal close in Q2 2026 (~€570M) is a clear catalyst, yet JPMorgan's Underweight rating and "transition year" guidance are keeping sentiment depressed. If Simplify program delivers margin expansion, the stock is deeply mispriced relative to the restructured earnings power by 2028.

- **AAOI — Potential trap or breakout.** Stock up +265% in 4 months on the back of >$200M in 1.6T orders, yet the company is still essentially breakeven (non-GAAP EPS -$0.01 in Q4). The $1B+ 2026 revenue guide requires flawless execution on factory ramp, with top 3 customers representing 91% of revenue and 15 insider sales / 0 purchases. A single customer delay or tariff shock could crater the stock.

- **Sumitomo Electric (5802.T) — Value with catalyst.** Pulled back 27% from ATH to ~¥8,380 while all 7 analysts maintain Buy with avg PT ¥11,446 (+37% upside). Guidance raised 39% on EPS. The pullback appears to be ex-dividend/profit-taking rather than fundamental deterioration.

- **Luxshare Precision (002475.SZ) — Lagging the sector.** Flat over 90 days while peers rallied 40–100%+. Unanimous Buy consensus with 34% upside to avg PT. Hong Kong IPO (mid-2026) and AI terminal project are clear catalysts. The OpenAI manufacturing loss to Foxconn may be overstated as a negative.

- **TFC Optical (300394.SZ) and Yuanjie Semi (688498.SH) — Froth warning.** Both trading 23%+ above analyst consensus targets after 100%+ and 1,300%+ rallies respectively. Fundamentals are extraordinary (1.6T light engines, InP laser monopoly), but any demand hiccup at these multiples invites violent drawdowns. TFC's 2.16 beta and Yuanjie's 514x TTM PE make these the highest-risk names in the universe.
