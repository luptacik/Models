# Cybersecurity sector financial metrics compendium — 16 companies benchmarked

**Bottom line: The cybersecurity sector entered a sharp valuation correction in Q1 2026, with median EV/NTM revenue compressing to ~3.6x across this peer set — down roughly 40–50% from late-2025 peaks.** Growth quality remains bifurcated: CrowdStrike, Rubrik, Cloudflare, and Netskope sustain 20–46% revenue growth, while legacy and VM players (Check Point, Rapid7, Trend Micro) stagnate at low-single-digit or negative growth. Profitability leaders include Qualys (45% FCF margin), Check Point (43%), and Fortinet (33%), but the market is increasingly pricing platform breadth and AI positioning over near-term margins. All data below sourced from the most recent earnings releases, SEC filings (10-K, 10-Q, 20-F, 8-K, S-1), and cross-referenced with financial data providers. Confidence flags applied throughout.

## The sector's valuation reset reveals a stark bifurcation

The March 2026 cybersecurity sell-off — triggered by AI disruption fears (Anthropic's Mythos model, Databricks LakeWatch) — compressed valuations across the board, but the damage was not evenly distributed. **Cloudflare still commands a 26x NTM multiple** because the market views it as AI infrastructure, not just security. CrowdStrike at ~16x reflects its status as the dominant endpoint/XDR platform, though that's down ~35% from November highs.

The most striking signal in this data set is the **divergence between growth and valuation.** Rubrik (+46% revenue growth) trades at only 5.3x NTM — barely above Check Point (+6% growth) at 5.8x — revealing the market's heavy discount for unprofitable high-growers. Meanwhile, Qualys at 4.1x with **45.5% FCF margins** and a Rule of 40 score of 55 appears to be the peer set's most anomalous value, though its low growth (10%, decelerating to ~7%) explains the discount.

**Three structural themes** emerge from this data:

- **Platformization premium is real but narrowing.** PANW's 18-point gap between NGS ARR growth (33%) and total revenue growth (15%) shows the friction cost of business model transformation. The market is paying ~8x NTM for the promise that subscription conversion will eventually close this gap. FTNT, by contrast, is growing hardware and services simultaneously without forcing a transition — its 4.1% SBC ratio versus PANW's 14% tells the SBC cost of aggressive platform bets.

- **Capital efficiency is the new moat.** The SBC-adjusted profitability picture changes rankings dramatically. Fortinet (SBC 4.1% of revenue), Check Point (~5.9%), and Qualys (~11.5%) look far more attractive when SBC is treated as a real cost versus CrowdStrike (22.8%), SentinelOne (29.7%), and Zscaler (~27%). Netskope's extraordinary ~75–80% SBC-to-revenue ratio is a temporary IPO artifact but highlights how equity dilution subsidizes many "profitable" SaaS stories.

- **Rapid7's distress is the canary.** With market cap ($376M) less than half its enterprise value ($909M), ARR flat, revenue guided to decline, $525–600M in convertible notes due in 12 months, and a goodwill impairment warning issued — RPD represents the tail risk in cybersecurity of a company that failed to build a differentiated platform before the market shifted. Its 1.1x EV/NTM multiple prices in significant restructuring or strategic change.

The peer set's **median Rule of 40 score is ~42**, suggesting the cybersecurity sector broadly delivers acceptable growth-adjusted returns, but the range — from Zscaler and Qualys at 55 to Rapid7 at 17 — underscores that generalized "cybersecurity basket" investing increasingly misses the winners-and-losers dynamic of a consolidating market.
