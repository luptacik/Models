## PRE-FLIGHT — SECTOR-DEFINING CONTEXT

**Everything in this report reads through three filters. State them upfront.**

1. **§25D Residential ITC terminated December 31, 2025** (OBBBA / H.R. 1, P.L. 119-21). Not phased — dead. This single legislative event reorders the residential universe. Homeowner-owned purchases lose the 30% credit; only TPO (lease/PPA via §48E, preserved through 2027) retains federal support. The sector is now bifurcated into "TPO-compatible" and "obsolete under current policy."
2. **§45X manufacturing credit preserved** — but entirely exposed to Treasury rulemaking under FEOC sourcing thresholds. FSLR recognized $1.6B in 2025 and guides $2.1B for 2026. Strip out 45X and FSLR gross margin is ~7%. The single most important number in the sector and the least-discussed.
3. **The real growth story has migrated out of residential solar entirely**: ESS (CATL, Sungrow, Fluence), AI data-center grid (Eaton, Schneider, Delta), and international manufacturing (Waaree). Point-solution residential hardware is a value-destruction zone; integrated grid/storage/electrification compounders are where the P&L lives.

A note on ticker integrity: R2_1's "#14 TCL Zhonghuan (688599.SS)" is incorrect — 688599.SS is **Trina Solar**; TCL Zhonghuan is 002129.SZ. The financial profile described in R2_1 (RMB 29B revenue, 15.1 GW module shipments, −12.9% segment GM, 210mm wafer focus) matches TCL Zhonghuan. R3 correctly treats 688599.SS as Trina. This report uses Trina's R3 data for 688599.SS and flags TCL Zhonghuan as a separate-but-weaker name not independently scored.

## SECTION 3 — NARRATIVE REPORT

### 3a. Sub-sector dynamics

**Residential solar is not the solar sector anymore.** It's the *smallest* piece of a solar/storage/grid complex whose three real growth vectors are (a) utility-scale BESS aggregation, (b) AI data-center grid infrastructure, and (c) international manufacturing under protective trade walls. The R1 landscape shows residential at ~$55–65B ex-storage globally, but the segments actually driving incremental P&L are ESS (~$18–22B growing 14–18%), VPP software (growing 22–28%), and grid equipment backlog — all of which the residential installer/inverter names touch only at the margins.

**Platform integration is the emerging moat, but integration only matters where demand exists.** Sunrun's integrated TPO+VPP+installer stack is intact in the US; Enphase's IQ ecosystem is technically sound but built for a market that §25D just took offline. 1KOMMA5° and Enpal in Europe have impressive integration stories but remain private. The listed integration plays worth owning are:
- **Sungrow** (inverter + ESS + overseas + data center), where all four vectors align
- **CATL** (battery cells + ESS + solid-state pipeline), where scale + margin + tech advantage compound
- **Eaton / Schneider / Delta** (grid equipment + data center integration), where the AI buildout is the demand engine

**Platform absorption status:**
- SunPower, Sunnova, Mosaic all bankrupt → Sunrun is the sole US residential TPO pure-play survivor
- Meyer Burger → Waaree ($18.5M, distressed); Arcadium Lithium → Rio Tinto (March 2025 — not solar but adjacent); Chinese module Top 4 Big Four losses >RMB 27B combined FY25 drives consolidation via attrition not M&A
- SEDG's ecosystem strategy collapsed explicitly (shut storage division, exited EV); now retreating to point-solution inverter co. This is the clearest example of platform strategy *failure* in the universe
- Schneider + SMA acquisition rumor remains unverified but logically consistent (Schneider wants scale in European inverters; SMA is restructuring; strategic fit exists)

**Chinese module consolidation is happening through attrition, not M&A.** Antitrust blocked the formal polysilicon JV in April 2026. VAT export rebates eliminated April 1 (+6–9% export cost). 70+ GW capacity terminated. The "OPEC-like" coordination is real but structurally fragile — price rebounds have been partial (CNY 0.6/W → ~0.9/W) and cost pressure is still winning.

### 3b. Competitive landscape

**Winning share:**
- **Sungrow** — gaining in inverter + ESS simultaneously while Chinese peers destruct
- **Waaree** — gaining in India + US under tariff protection; $5.6B order book
- **First Solar** — gaining (or at least holding) as the US-protected Cd-Te outlier; but 8.3 GW of gross debookings in 2025 is a share loss at the backlog level

### 3e. Conviction summary

**The sector has split into two cleanly differentiated universes, and almost everything worth owning is on one side of the line.** On the "own" side: integrated ESS/inverter scale operators (Sungrow, CATL), US manufacturing with genuine policy protection (FSLR, Waaree, Shoals), and AI data-center grid infrastructure (Eaton, Schneider, Delta). On the "avoid" side: US residential point-solution hardware (ENPH, SEDG, GNRC, SMA HBS), Chinese commodity module makers (all of them — JKS, JA Solar, Trina, LONGi, Tongwei), and micro-cap turnaround pivots (OTOVO). The §25D termination on July 4, 2025 was the decisive break — it didn't kill the sector but it killed the residential-hardware narrative, and the consensus is still under-appreciating how completely that narrative is dead. ENPH trading at 2.9x EV/Rev feels cheap by historical standards but is cheap for a reason: the Q1 2026 revenue guide implies a further 12–21% sequential decline just as the legal cloud (class actions, CFO stock sales) opens.

**Highest-conviction names are Sungrow, FSLR, Waaree, and Shoals among solar-adjacent; Eaton, CATL, Schneider, and Delta as "benchmark/pickaxe" plays.** Sungrow is the sharpest fundamental-vs-price divergence in the universe — 22% profit growth punished by ~19% tariff-panic drawdown in early April, with 34 analysts pointing to CNY 187 vs CNY 123 trading. FSLR is the only US name where 5-year cash flow is reasonably defensible without optimistic policy assumptions — provided the §45X framework survives FEOC implementation. Waaree is the single best growth profile in the universe but requires resolution of the CEO/CFO departure and the April 2026 CBP ruling. Shoals offers the cleanest risk-reward on US utility-scale solar/BOS at ~8–9x 2026 EBITDA with backlog-covered guidance. Eaton, CATL, Schneider, and Delta are all "AI data-center grid" plays that happen to touch solar at the edges — own them for the primary thesis, not the solar exposure.

**What would change my mind:** On FSLR, a Q1 2026 debooking print above 2 GW (April 28 earnings) would crack the backlog integrity thesis and force a reassessment — currently on the PASS side only because backlog appears durable. On Sungrow, a meaningful ESS gross margin compression in H1 2026 (below 30%) would signal that Chinese inverter+ESS commoditization is arriving faster than expected. On RUN, if Q1 2026 (May 6) shows continued subscriber-addition contraction with no meaningful §48E-driven share gain, the watchlist becomes a do-not-pursue — the thesis rests entirely on §48E translating to TPO market share against bankrupt homeowner-owned competitors, and if that isn't visible in H1 2026, it isn't coming. On Waaree, a final CBP ruling that enforces retroactive tariff-evasion liabilities beyond the already-acknowledged ₹2.95B would be a serious break in what is currently the cleanest India-ex-US thesis.
