## Framing note

R1 covers the full earth drilling universe (offshore, US land, international land, mineral, OEMs, integrated OFS). R2 and R3 deep-dive only on the mineral exploration drilling sub-sector anchored on MDI.TO. The scoring below is therefore restricted to the 8 mineral drillers where full financial and catalyst data is available. Offshore and US land drilling names are flagged in Section 3a for comparative macro context but are not scored — that requires a separate R2/R3 cycle.

A sector-wide observation that frames every score: the sub-sector is at a **bifurcated inflection** where revenue growth and margin delivery have decoupled. Every single company in the group reported positive demand signals (record backlogs, senior miner budgets up 30%+, gold at record highs) AND every single company flagged margin compression from labor inflation, FX, mobilization costs, or tax issues. This is a textbook early-upcycle dynamic — the question on each name is not *whether* the upcycle is real but *which companies will convert top-line into EBITDA fastest*.

### Scoring rationale highlights

- **Tailwind 5s** (CAPD, GEO) — both have ~80–90% gold exposure at a moment when gold is at record highs and gold exploration budgets are leading the cycle. CAPD's production-phase mine-site drilling model is structurally more stable than exploration-phase work.

Multiple signals cluster: first-ever dividend deferral citing geopolitical uncertainty while reporting record revenue, Technical Director Jordaan's resignation, utilization *contracting* from 73% → 65% while 77% of capex is going to expansion, and Morningstar's quantitative model flagging a 130% premium to fair value. Founder/CEO tenure (since 1986) normally earns credit but the divergence signals demand caution.
- **Valuation 2** (MDI.TO) — 14.8x TTM EV/EBITDA against a peer median of ~4x is an extreme premium. Not a 1 because the underlying fleet quality is genuinely best-in-class, but a premium requiring a sustained upcycle to justify. On normalized NTM EBITDA (~12–13x) the premium narrows but remains significant.

## SECTION 3 — NARRATIVE REPORT

### 3a. Sub-sector dynamics

The mineral exploration drilling sub-sector is a ~US$3.3–3.7B global market (2025), with a projected CAGR of 5.0–5.7% through 2033. Within the broader ~US$175–185B earth drilling universe that R1 maps, it is one of the smaller sub-sectors by revenue but structurally more attractive than day-rate oil drillers because: per-metre contracts are less volatile than day-rates, the commodity exposure is to energy-transition metals (copper, gold, uranium, lithium) rather than oil, and the supply-side constraint on rigs and skilled crews is tighter than in offshore.

**Where the growth is concentrated, 2026–2028:**

1. **Gold-leveraged exploration and mine-site drilling.** Gold above US$2,900/oz (and the R2 file's reference to US$4,600/oz spot) is the dominant demand driver. Gold exploration budgets rose 11% to claim 50% of all exploration spend, and major miners (Barrick, Agnico Eagle, AngloGold) each committed >US$300M to exploration for the first time in 12 years. Names with direct gold exposure (CAPD 90%, GEO 70–80%, PRN group 76%, MDI.TO 39%) are the primary beneficiaries.

2. **Copper exploration tailwind, lagging gold by 1–2 quarters.** Copper exploration exceeded US$3.2B in 2024 (first time above US$3B since 2013) driven by AI/grid/EV demand narratives. Names with meaningful copper exposure (MDI.TO 32%, FAR 23%, CAPD diversifying from 90% gold toward copper) will pick up as the cycle broadens.

3. **Tier-1 customer consolidation.** Mining majors are concentrating work with larger, safer, technologically capable drilling partners. This is visible in the customer mix shift: GEO.TO transitioning from junior-heavy to Tier-1, FAR 90% Tier-1, CAPD 90% Tier-1, MDI.TO 90% senior/intermediate. Small regional players are being squeezed out.

**On the horizon, NOT covered by R2/R3:** Offshore drilling (R1's most detailed sub-section) and US land drilling. Offshore is entering a consolidation endgame with the pending Transocean-Valaris merger (US$5.8B equity, ~US$17B EV) creating a 73-rig dominant entity. Day-rate pressure is emerging (jackups -20.6%, drillships -8.2% in 2025 per Westwood) as Aramco's jackup suspensions flood the global market. These are separate scoring exercises — the mineral exploration cycle is largely insulated from offshore day-rate dynamics and WTI oil price moves.

**Platform absorption status in mineral exploration:** Consolidation is beginning but the sub-sector remains fragmented. The biggest structural move was Boart Longyear going private to American Industrial Partners (April 2024) — removing the former #1 public pure-play and leaving MDI.TO as the unambiguous public scale leader. Perenti's DDH1/Swick acquisitions (2023) created a 312-rig Australian platform. MDI.TO's Explomin acquisition (US$85M, Nov 2024) added 92 rigs across Peru/Colombia/Dominican Republic. The pattern: disciplined strategic acquirors buying specialty/geographic-gap assets, not roll-up frenzy. Small regional operators are acquisition targets or slow-fade candidates.

### 3b. Competitive landscape

**Share gainers (with evidence):**

- **CAPD.L** — FY2026 guidance +23%, MSALABS +68% YoY, named wins from AngloGold/Montage/Equinox/IAMGOLD/Reko Diq. Multiple Tier-1 contract signatures in a single 90-day window is the clearest share-gain signal in the group.
- **GEO.TO** — FY2025 revenue +29.3%, Chile fleet doubled 8 → 18 rigs and fully utilized, customer mix upgrading to Tier-1.
- **Perenti drilling segment** — drilling revenue +30% FY25 / +9% H1 FY26, first-ever US project (Goldrush), Nevada franchise being built.

**Share losers / at risk of losing:**

- **FAR.TO** — FY2025 revenue -12%. The record backlog is the recovery case but the current-year trailing number is the worst in the group. Whether this represents cyclical timing (mobilization delays) or genuine share loss will be clear by Q2 2026.
- **MDI.JO** — utilization contracting (73 → 65% raise bore, 28% slim drill); African operations "in a state of flux"; major project on hold. The record pipeline number is growing while actual rig deployment shrinks — that gap usually closes by working down pipeline, not by ramping up.
- **MSV.AX** — FY2025 revenue -17% (coal weakness + weather + disruptions). Recovering in FY26 but the base was eroded.

**Head-to-head battles:**

- **MDI.TO vs. CAPD.L** on **Reko Diq (Barrick, Pakistan copper mega-project)** — CAPD is named as the driller ramping to full run-rate H2 2026. MDI.TO does not appear in the Reko Diq work. This is the single most consequential copper exploration project in the sub-sector and CAPD has it.
- **PRN.AX vs. MDI.TO** on **Nevada Gold Mines work.** Perenti's Barminco has the A$120M Goldrush contract (Barrick/Newmont JV) and a Fourmile LOI. MDI.TO has historical Nevada presence. The pending NGM dispute (Newmont vs. Barrick) may reshape this — a Newmont win could affect Barrick's operatorship and reshuffle drilling contracts.
- **FAR.TO vs. MDI.TO** on **Canadian Tier-1 gold contracts.** FAR's three-year Tier-1 Canada/Chile wins (~US$150M) came in Oct 2025, directly overlapping MDI's Canada–US geography that grew +56.7% YoY in Q3. FAR appears to be competing for work MDI historically captured — or the market is big enough for both.
- **CAPD.L vs. MSALABS** (internal optionality) — CAPD is essentially fighting a two-front battle: its drilling segment competes with GEO/MDI in Africa, while MSALABS competes with external lab services (SGS, ALS) for PhotonAssay-based contracts. MSALABS hitting profitability is the asymmetric upside on this name.

**M&A read:**

- **Likely acquirors:** MDI.TO (Explomin playbook — disciplined targeted bolt-ons in specialty/geographic-gap assets); Perenti (DDH1/Swick model; active but integration-digesting); CAPD (lab + drilling integration plays). Note that all three consolidators are also on the PASS/WATCHLIST, which is unusual and suggests the sub-sector's most disciplined operators are also its highest-quality investments.
- **Likely targets:** GEO.TO (~US$130M market cap, Africa+Chile specialist, fragmented sub-sector, clean balance sheet — logical MDI or CAPD target); FAR.TO (French-origin, mid-size, struggling with leverage — logical MDI target); OGD.TO and MSV.AX (micro-caps that could be absorbed by Perenti or a US/Canadian platform builder).
- **Valuation at which targets clear:** Boart Longyear's private transaction (April 2024, reportedly ~US$1B enterprise value on ~US$1B revenue = ~1x revenue, estimated ~5–6x EBITDA on depressed margins) sets the floor comp. Explomin cleared at ~US$85M on ~US$95M revenue and US$16M EBITDA (~5.3x EBITDA). Expect 5–7x EBITDA for targets in a cycle-up environment, with premium names (CAPD's MSALABS) commanding higher.

### 3c. Risk register

**Sector-level risks (top 5):**

1. **Gold price reversal (HIGH).** Gold above US$2,900 (R3) or US$4,600 (R2) is doing the heaviest lifting for demand. Every single name in the group is at least 16% gold-exposed; five are >60% gold-exposed. A 20% gold price pullback would compress exploration budgets within 6 months and hit CAPD/GEO/MDI.JO/PRN disproportionately.

2. **Margin compression as labor inflation outpaces price increases (HIGH).** Universal signal across all 8 companies. 40% of the global drilling workforce expected to retire within a decade; labor shortages are acute and are the primary explanation for margin compression even in a revenue upcycle. If pricing doesn't recover fast enough, margins could stay depressed through FY2026.

3. **Jurisdictional tax shocks (MEDIUM-HIGH).** Côte d'Ivoire tax charge (GEO US$8.4M), Tanzania mining services tax ruling (CAPD 60% ETR), Indonesia slowdown (MDI.TO largest customer): three different jurisdictional surprises in a single cycle. Each was idiosyncratic but the pattern tells you the sub-sector's Tier-2/3 jurisdiction risk is permanent, not cyclical.

4. **Junior miner counterparty risk (MEDIUM).** GEO.TO's equity-for-debt swap (US$7.7M) is a leading indicator. As junior financings boom (R2 notes +100% to US$21.4B in 2025), credit quality declines. If gold rolls over, junior failures will leave drillers with bad receivables.

5. **Capital allocation mismatch in a trough-to-recovery transition (MEDIUM).** MDI.JO's 77% expansion capex while utilization contracts, MDI.TO's capex cut below the C$70M target, CAPD's US$40M equity raise during a guidance raise — each name is making different bets on the cycle shape. Wrong capex timing is the #1 historical destroyer of shareholder value in drilling (Boart Longyear 2012–2016 is the reference disaster).

**Company-specific risks (top 1–2 per name, non-overlapping with sector risks):**

- **MDI.TO** — (1) Indonesia largest-customer slowdown continuing without named replacement; (2) valuation multiple compression risk as peer group re-rates at lower multiples even if MDI's absolute earnings recover.
- **FAR.TO** — (1) Net debt 1.56x leaves the least room for cycle surprises; deleveraging priority limits growth capex even as backlog explodes; (2) single covering analyst = thin liquidity for re-rating signals.
- **GEO.TO** — (1) Ghanaian Cedi FX sensitivity (+43% appreciation crushed FY2025 gross margin) is a recurring rather than one-time risk; (2) concentrated geographic footprint (5 countries) magnifies single-country surprises.
- **OGD.TO** — (1) Zero credible analyst coverage means the name can go stale regardless of fundamentals; (2) founder share sale history suggests ongoing float pressure.
- **CAPD.L** — (1) Tanzania 60% ETR if the court ruling is not revised in 2026; (2) investment portfolio value (US$97.5M+) is a non-operational revenue stream that will unwind if gold rolls over, and currently represents >25% of market cap.
- **MDI.JO** — (1) Dividend deferral combined with expansion capex creates a cash crunch scenario if African operations deteriorate further; (2) Morningstar quant model flagging 130% premium to fair value (contrary to 2.6x EV/EBITDA headline) suggests the earnings quality is lower than reported — investigate before taking a position.
- **PRN.AX** — (1) Barrick-Newmont NGM court outcome (May 2026 hearings) is binary for the Nevada growth story; (2) conglomerate structure means drilling segment can be masked by contract mining underperformance.
- **MSV.AX** — (1) Steelmaking coal exposure (33% revenue) is a structural decliner; (2) micro-cap liquidity and insider selling near highs limits position sizing.

### 3e. Conviction summary

Two PASS candidates — CAPD.L and GEO.TO — both earned their scores the same way: high gold exposure at a moment of peak gold pricing, combined with strong operating momentum (CAPD's raised guidance, GEO's +29% revenue), solid balance sheets, and trading at low-single-digit EV/EBITDA multiples. The two names are structurally different enough that they are complements rather than substitutes in a portfolio: CAPD's production-phase mine-site drilling and MSALABS lab services business is defensive and cash-generative; GEO's exploration-stage Africa-plus-Chile exposure is higher-beta and more leveraged to the exploration upcycle. **If I had to concentrate capital, CAPD is the higher-conviction name** because the business model quality (production-phase > exploration-phase, lab recurring revenue, investment portfolio optionality) is structurally more durable than GEO's, and CAPD management has demonstrated the most credible guidance discipline in the group (raised twice in FY2025, then raised again for FY2026).

The three most interesting watchlist names are **MDI.TO, FAR.TO, and PRN.AX**. MDI.TO has the cleanest moat story in the group and the scale to dominate consolidation, but the 14.8x trailing multiple is a genuine problem — at 3x peer median, the name needs both earnings recovery AND multiple compression to work from here. FAR.TO is the purest cyclical setup: record backlog against a deteriorated trailing P&L, 4–5x forward P/E, insider buying, record contract wins — if execution matches backlog, the re-rating is large; if margins don't normalize, it's a value trap and the 1.56x leverage stings. PRN.AX is cheap and returning cash but suffers conglomerate dilution and faces the binary NGM court outcome in May. **The MDI.JO watchlist designation is a reluctant one** — the combination of dividend deferral, Technical Director resignation, utilization contraction, near-zero FCF, and Morningstar's quant model flagging a 130% premium (against a 2.6x EV/EBITDA headline) is too many red flags clustered at once. It's on watchlist only because the insider accumulation is substantial enough to suggest someone with information sees something the screens miss — but "someone with information" is not a position-sizing rationale.

**What would change my mind:**
- A sustained pullback in gold to below US$2,600/oz would compress the gold-exposed PASS candidates (CAPD, GEO) and likely move them to watchlist pending budget guidance from the senior miners.
- FAR.TO delivering one clean Q1 2026 beat with EBITDA margin >20% would move it to PASS regardless of the leverage — backlog conversion is the dominant variable.
- MDI.TO's multiple compressing to <10x EV/NTM EBITDA (either via earnings recovery or price correction) would move it to PASS given the best-in-class moat.
- Any evidence of a broad junior miner counterparty default cycle (after GEO's equity-for-debt swap precedent) would move CAPD and MDI.TO to watchlist pending receivables review.
