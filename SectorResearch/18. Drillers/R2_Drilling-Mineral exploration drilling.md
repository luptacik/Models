# R3 Financial Metrics: Mineral Exploration Drilling Services

Eight specialty contract drillers spanning five exchanges show a sector at an inflection point. **Gold above $4,600/oz and copper above $13,000/ton** create the strongest macro backdrop since 2012, yet global exploration budgets declined for three consecutive years through 2025. The disconnect is closing: junior mining financings doubled in 2025 to $21.4B, major miners are releasing higher exploration budgets, and several drillers report record backlogs entering 2026. Valuations range dramatically — from **2.6x EV/EBITDA (Master Drilling) to 14.8x (Major Drilling)** — reflecting divergent growth trajectories, geographic exposures, and cycle positioning.

## 3. Sector synthesis

The mineral exploration drilling sector sits at a **bifurcated inflection point** entering mid-2026. Gold prices above $4,600/oz and copper above $13,000/ton create the strongest commodity price backdrop since 2012, yet S&P Global data shows global nonferrous exploration budgets declined three consecutive years through 2025 to ~$12.4B — still **42% below the 2012 peak** of $21.5B. The disconnect is beginning to close. Junior mining financings doubled in 2025 to a near-record $21.4B, gold exploration budgets rose 11% to claim 50% of all exploration spend, and major miners like Barrick, Agnico Eagle, and AngloGold each committed $300M+ to exploration for the first time in twelve years. Multiple companies in this universe — Foraco (record US$404M backlog), Capital Limited (twice-raised guidance), and Major Drilling (TSX-V mining financings +53%) — report accelerating demand signals with 6-month lags now converting to contracted work. The structural constraint is **supply-side**: the global rig fleet is roughly unchanged from 2016, labor shortages are acute (40% of the drilling workforce expected to retire within a decade), and adding capacity takes 12–18 months. This supports pricing power as utilization tightens.

Geographically and by commodity, the eight companies exhibit meaningful differentiation. **Capital Limited and Geodrill** have the highest gold sensitivity (90% and 70–80%, respectively), making them direct beneficiaries of the gold super-cycle but vulnerable to gold price reversals. **Major Drilling** is the most diversified by commodity (39% gold, 32% copper) and geography (20+ countries), but trades at a **dramatic premium** — 14.8x EV/EBITDA versus a peer median of ~4x — reflecting its scale advantage, record fleet of 697 rigs, and anticipated margin recovery from trough levels. At the other extreme, **Master Drilling (2.6x) and Mitchell Services (2.85x)** trade at deep discounts, though for different reasons: Master Drilling's utilization is declining and it faces geopolitical headwinds requiring a dividend deferral, while Mitchell is a micro-cap with zero analyst coverage and a single-country footprint. **Foraco at 6.4x** commands a premium to smaller peers on its record backlog and multi-year contract visibility but faces the highest leverage (1.56x net debt/EBITDA) in the group. Balance sheets are broadly healthy — six of eight companies carry net debt/EBITDA below 1.0x, and three are in net cash positions.
