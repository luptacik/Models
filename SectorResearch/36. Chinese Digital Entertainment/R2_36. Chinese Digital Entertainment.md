## Sector observations and cross-cutting themes

**Gaming is the highest-margin sub-sector by a wide margin.** Giant Network's 89.2% gross margin and Sanqi Interactive's 76.8% illustrate the operating leverage of established game franchises. Among pure-play gaming names, Century Huatong stands out with **+75% YoY revenue growth** driven by *Whiteout Survival*'s global dominance ($3.5B+ lifetime) and the rapid scaling of *Kingshot*. Giant Network's *Supernatural Task Force* generated a similar breakout dynamic domestically, with CNY 13.3B in deferred revenue signaling substantial future recognition. NetEase remains the quality compounder — **43% FCF margin** on CNY 112.6B in revenue with net cash of CNY 163.5B, combining steady franchise economics with new title optionality from *Marvel Rivals* and *Where Winds Meet*.

**The overseas revenue story is accelerating across gaming names.** Tencent's international games grew 33% to CNY 77.4B (now 32% of games revenue, up from ~25% two years ago). Century Huatong derives >52% of revenue overseas. XD Inc earns 38% internationally. Hello Group's overseas revenue surged 71% to CNY 2B. This diversification matters given periodic domestic regulatory uncertainty and a maturing domestic market.

**Streaming economics are diverging sharply.** Bilibili's first full-year GAAP profit in 2025 — on the back of 23% ad revenue growth and the PUGV community model — contrasts starkly with iQIYI's margin collapse (operating margin from 6.2% to 0.8%, FCF from CNY 2B to near-zero). Content-heavy models (iQIYI at 56.6% content cost/revenue) face structural headwinds vs. user-generated content platforms. TME shows a third path — music streaming with **29% core operating margins** and 24% paying ratio, the highest monetization rate in the universe.

**Cash-rich social platforms trade at absurd discounts.** Both JOYY (net cash $3.26B vs. $2.95B market cap) and Hello Group (net cash CNY 8.7B vs. CNY 7.0B market cap) have **negative enterprise values**, meaning the market assigns negative value to their operating businesses despite both generating positive operating income and returning capital aggressively. This reflects deep skepticism about the sustainability of live-streaming revenue, which is declining at both companies (−6.6% and −14% respectively in Q4).

**AI is the dominant strategic narrative across the entire universe.** Tencent is deploying AI for ad targeting, game content creation, and cloud growth. Baidu's AI-powered revenue hit CNY 40B (+48%). Kuaishou's Kling AI reached ~$240M ARR by December. Kunlun Tech has pivoted entirely to AI/AIGC, accepting near-term losses. Alibaba is investing $52B+ in AI infrastructure. China Literature launched AI-animated dramas generating CNY 100M+ in H2. Giant Network integrated DeepSeek into its Zhengtu franchise. The intensity of AI investment is compressing near-term margins at Alibaba, Baidu, and Kunlun while potentially creating significant optionality.

**The conglomerates present a "sum of parts" puzzle.** Kingsoft trades at 0.92x EV/Revenue despite owning a majority stake in WPS (669M monthly active devices, 16% revenue growth) and a gaming franchise. The stock's EV is just CNY 9.8B against CNY 24.1B in net cash. Similarly, Alibaba's ~1.9x EV/Revenue on CNY 1T+ in revenue reflects ongoing margin compression from quick-commerce and AI investments, though cloud revenue is growing 36%. Baidu's GAAP metrics are distorted by a one-time CNY 16.2B impairment; non-GAAP P/E of ~14x is more representative.

**Film is a hit-driven, binary sector.** Enlight Media's entire 2025 story is Ne Zha 2 (CNY 15.44B domestic box office, highest in Chinese history). Q1 2025 operating cash flow of CNY 4.9B was 14x its entire FY2024 OCF. But TTM margins of 70%+ are not sustainable — FY2024 normalized net margin was ~18%. China Film benefits structurally as the near-monopoly distributor (~48% market share) but generates razor-thin margins despite its privileged position, suggesting operational inefficiencies typical of state-owned enterprises.

**Regulatory environment appears stabilized but watchful.** China issued 467 game licenses in Q1 2026 (+22% YoY), a constructive signal. However, Hello Group cited "tax regulation headwinds" hitting domestic live streaming, and the broader tech sector remains subject to periodic scrutiny. The market's persistent valuation discount on Chinese internet names (Tencent at ~14x forward P/E, Alibaba at ~14x, NetEase at 13x vs. US mega-cap peers at 25–35x) reflects this ongoing risk premium.
