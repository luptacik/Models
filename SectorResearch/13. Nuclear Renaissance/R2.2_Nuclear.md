## Key sector-level observations

The Nuclear Renaissance universe splits into three distinct tiers. **Operating utilities** (CGN Power, KEPCO) generate real cash flows with EBITDA margins of 28–42% but carry heavy leverage (4.8–6.3x Debt/EBITDA) typical of regulated infrastructure. **Services and components companies** (Mirion, AtkinsRéalis, Doosan, JSW, Rolls-Royce) are experiencing record backlogs and accelerating order intake — Mirion's backlog grew 36% YoY, AtkinsRéalis's nuclear revenue surged 53% organically, and Doosan's nuclear order book nearly doubled. **Upstream and development-stage names** (UUUU, DNN, YCA, SLX, NNE) consume cash and trade on NAV or speculative future production value, with NNE's $487M EV resting entirely on optionality against $577M in cash.

The most notable data point across the universe is the **explosion in nuclear services backlogs**: AtkinsRéalis at CAD 21.2B (+22.6%), Mirion at $1.1B (+36%), Doosan nuclear orders at KRW 10.5T (+77%), and Rolls-Royce Defence at £17.4B. This backlog wave — combined with Westinghouse's $80B U.S. government partnership and CGN's 20 units under construction — confirms the nuclear buildout is transitioning from policy aspiration to contracted reality. The primary risk flag is leverage concentration: KEPCO's KRW 127.7T net debt and CGN's HKD 212.5B net debt are sector outliers that constrain financial flexibility despite strong operating metrics.
