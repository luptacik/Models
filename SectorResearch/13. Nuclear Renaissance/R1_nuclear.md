# Nuclear Renaissance — R1 Sector Analyst Landscape Sweep

**BOTTOM LINE UP FRONT:** The global nuclear energy sector is experiencing its strongest policy, capital, and demand tailwinds in 40+ years, driven by the convergence of AI data center power demand (>13 GW contracted by US hyperscalers), the COP28 tripling pledge (33 countries), bipartisan US policy support (ADVANCE Act + IRA + 4 Trump EOs targeting 400 GW by 2050), and geopolitical energy security imperatives. The overall nuclear power market is **$37–245B** depending on scope definition, growing at **3–5% CAGR** with sub-sectors like SMRs, uranium enrichment, and decommissioning growing materially faster. ~440 reactors operate globally today producing a record **2,667 TWh in 2024**, with **70+ under construction** adding 77 GWe. China dominates new builds (~27 under construction at ~$2.7B/unit in 5 years); the West is re-learning how to build. The critical execution question: can Western nuclear deliver on-time and on-budget at scale? Only China has consistently done so.

### Top 5 growth drivers

**DRIVER 1: AI DATA CENTER POWER DEMAND** — The single most powerful near-term catalyst. IEA projects global DC electricity from **460 TWh (2024) → ~945 TWh by 2030** (equivalent to Japan's total consumption). US data centers to account for ~9% of US electricity by 2030 (EPRI). **US tech companies have signed contracts for >10 GW of new nuclear capacity** (Goldman Sachs). Combined hyperscaler nuclear commitments: Meta ~7.7 GW, Amazon ~2.4 GW+, Microsoft ~835 MW+, Google 500 MW, Oracle ~1 GW (speculative) = **>13 GW total contracted**.

**DRIVER 2: ENERGY SECURITY / GEOPOLITICS** — COP28 Declaration: **33 countries** pledged to triple nuclear by 2050. China targeting **120 GW by 2030** (from ~61 GW today), approved 10 new reactors in April 2025 (~$27.4B). India targeting **100 GW by 2047**. France: 6 new EPR2s. South Korea: 2 new large reactors + 700 MW SMR by 2038. **Trump admin target: 400 GW US nuclear by 2050** (quadrupling current ~100 GW).

**DRIVER 3: DECARBONIZATION MANDATES** — Nuclear provides ~10% of global electricity and is the 2nd largest carbon-free source (after hydro). IPCC 1.5°C pathways show nuclear approximately tripling by 2050. EU Taxonomy inclusion (July 2022) unlocks green finance for nuclear. 14 major banks pledged nuclear support at Climate Week (Sept 2024).

**DRIVER 4: GOVERNMENT SUBSIDIES** — US IRA §45U: **$15/MWh PTC** for existing nuclear (2024–2031/32). §45Y/48E: **$25/MWh PTC** or **30% ITC** for new builds. $6B Civil Nuclear Credit Program. $2.7B domestic enrichment investment. UK: Sizewell C RAB model. France: €72.8B EPR2 program with 60% state loan. Canada: C$2B+ nuclear investment package.

**DRIVER 5: SMR TECHNOLOGY MATURATION** — **80+ SMR designs** under development globally (IAEA). NuScale: first NRC-certified SMR (77 MWe approved May 2025). TerraPower Natrium: NRC construction permit approved March 2026 — first commercial non-LWR permit in 40+ years. Kairos Power Hermes: construction permits received, demo targeted 2026. OPG BWRX-300 (Canada): first SMR construction license in a G7 country (April 2025).

### Top 3 headwinds

**HEADWIND 1: CONSTRUCTION COST OVERRUNS** — The single biggest credibility challenge.

| Project | Original Cost | Final Cost | Overrun | Schedule Delay |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Vogtle 3&4 (US, AP1000) | $14B | **$36.8B** | ~163% | 7 years |
| Hinkley Point C (UK, EPR) | £18B | **£49B+** | ~172% | 5+ years (ongoing) |
| Olkiluoto 3 (Finland, EPR) | €3B | **€11B** | ~267% | 14 years |
| Flamanville 3 (France, EPR) | €3.3B | **€10.5B+** | ~218% | 12+ years |

V.C. Summer (US) cancelled in 2017 causing Westinghouse bankruptcy. **Contrast:** China achieves ~5-year construction at ~$2.7B/unit.

**HEADWIND 2: REGULATORY APPROVAL TIMELINES** — NRC traditional licensing: ~14 years (two-step). Combined license: ~7–10 years. Design certification alone: 4–6 years. ADVANCE Act and Trump EOs are accelerating but reforms take 2–3 years to materialize. Cross-jurisdictional fragmentation complicates SMR deployment.

**HEADWIND 3: WASTE DISPOSAL & SUPPLY CHAIN** — ~430,000 MT of spent fuel globally; **~70% in temporary storage** with no permanent disposal. Finland's Onkalo is the only near-operational deep geological repository (trial run March 2025). US has no active permanent repository program (Yucca Mountain abandoned). HALEU supply is the critical bottleneck for advanced reactors — Russia is the only commercial-scale producer; US investing $2.7B+ but years from adequate capacity.

## 5. PLATFORM CONSOLIDATION DYNAMICS

### Vertically integrated platforms emerging

**Cameco + Brookfield/Westinghouse** is the most vertically integrated nuclear platform in the Western world: Uranium mining (#2 globally) → UF6 conversion (Port Hope) → Laser enrichment (49% of GLE, option to 75%) → Fuel fabrication (Westinghouse) → Reactor OEM/services (Westinghouse AP1000). The **$80B US government partnership** (Oct 2025) makes this a quasi-sovereign nuclear infrastructure platform. Likely next acquisitions: increase GLE stake, uranium juniors with tier-one Athabasca deposits.

**Constellation Energy** is the nuclear fleet/power marketing champion: 22 GW nuclear + 23 GW gas (Calpine) + 2.5M retail accounts + 3/4 of Fortune 100 as customers = **55 GW total**. Data center PPA strategy (Microsoft TMI, Meta Clinton, GSA $1B) is the growth vector. License extensions to 80 years + uprates provide incremental capacity.

**BWXT** is consolidating nuclear services/equipment/fuel: Acquired A.O.T. and Kinectrics in 2025. Launched BWXT Advanced Fuels (TRISO). Moving from primarily government/naval to integrated commercial nuclear services. Record $7.4B backlog (+119% YoY).

### Sub-sectors remaining fragmented and why

**Uranium exploration (TSX/TSXV/ASX juniors):** Hundreds of small companies with individual deposits. Fragmented because deposits are geographically dispersed, junior mining model relies on exploration optionality, many are pre-revenue. Consolidation accelerating (Paladin/Fission, IsoEnergy/Toro).

**Advanced SMR developers:** 30+ companies with varied technology approaches. Fragmented because pre-revenue, government funding supports multiple designs simultaneously. **Expect shakeout** as only a few achieve commercial deployment. NANO Nuclear's $8.5M acquisition of bankrupt USNC is a preview.

**Nuclear decommissioning/waste:** Specialized regulatory/liability environment limits consolidation. Niche players persist.

**Western enrichment:** National security sensitivities, massive capital requirements, and government ownership structures keep players separate. But Russia decoupling is driving new investment.

## 6. MACRO & AI LINKAGES

### Recession behavior

Nuclear capex is **largely counter-cyclical or non-cyclical** — construction spans 7–15+ years, making investment decisions decoupled from short-term cycles. Government support provides sustained funding. Current AI demand is secular, not cyclical. However, **merchant nuclear operators' earnings** are modestly pro-cyclical due to wholesale power price sensitivity. Utility stocks showed **lower drawdowns** in 2008-09 and 2020 than cyclical sectors. Nuclear fuel contracts (3–15 years, commonly 5–10 years) plus data center PPAs (17–20 years with investment-grade counterparties) provide exceptional revenue visibility.

### Critical timeline mismatch

Data centers need power **NOW** (2025–2027). TMI restart: 2027–2028. Existing nuclear PPAs: 2026 onward. First SMRs: **2030 at earliest**. TerraPower first units: 2030–2031. Widespread SMR deployment: **mid-2030s**. Natural gas is filling the gap, creating tension with corporate clean energy commitments.

### Key risks

- **SMR cost risk:** NuScale UAMPS cancelled Nov 2023 after costs soared to $9.3B / ~$20,139/kW (roughly equal to Vogtle). X-energy SMR price effectively doubled to $4.75–5.75B
- **HALEU supply:** Russia is only commercial-scale producer. 150-fold demand increase projected by 2035. US capacity years away. **Highest-priority bottleneck for advanced reactors**
- **Construction execution:** Western projects consistently 2–3x over budget (Vogtle, Hinkley, Olkiluoto, Flamanville)
- **Russian enrichment dependency:** Without new Western capacity, global enrichment needs exceed non-Russian supply by **6–20% through 2038** (Third Way)
- **FERC co-location policy:** Rejection of behind-the-meter model reshapes deal structures; benefits incumbents over new entrants
