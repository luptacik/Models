# Chinese Internet / Tech Sector — Batch 1 Financial Metrics

**Bottom line: All 15 companies in this coverage universe have reported Q4 CY2025 or full-year 2025 results.** The sector is navigating a three-front war — a brutal food-delivery subsidy battle compressing margins at Meituan, JD.com, and Alibaba; a massive AI infrastructure capex cycle led by Alibaba (RMB 380B/3yr), Tencent, and Kuaishou; and persistent US-China geopolitical overhang including HFCAA re-scrutiny and tariff risk to PDD's Temu model. Despite this, the group holds aggregate net cash exceeding **$250 billion**, and several names trade at single-digit forward P/E multiples with substantial buyback programs.

**Currency note:** All native figures reported in RMB unless noted. Consolidated USD conversions use **CNY/USD = 7.25** (approximate late March 2026 spot). HKD/USD = 7.78. Filing-date rate was 6.9931 (Dec 31, 2025). Individual company blocks use the filing rate where sourced from filings; the comparison table normalizes at 7.25.

## Three themes that define this coverage universe right now

**The food delivery war is the single largest margin destroyer.** Meituan swung from +10.9% operating margin to -6.9% in one year. JD.com's FCF collapsed from RMB 43.7B to RMB 6.5B. Alibaba's S&M jumped from 15% to 25% of revenue. China's March 2026 regulatory crackdown on delivery subsidies may force a ceasefire — a critical catalyst to monitor. The combined margin destruction across these three companies alone represents roughly **RMB 100B+ in annualized profit suppression**.

**AI capex is the next earnings headwind — and opportunity.** Alibaba has committed $53B over three years. Tencent is doubling AI spend. Kuaishou's 70% capex increase guidance triggered a 14% stock drop. Baidu's AI-powered revenue already represents 43% of its core business. The market is rewarding AI monetization proof points (Baidu, Kuaishou's Kling AI at $240M ARR) but punishing pure capex commitments without clear revenue visibility. Among this group, **Tencent's approach — AI as a multiplier on existing platforms rather than a standalone business — appears to carry the least execution risk**.

**Capital return divergence is striking.** Vipshop returned $944M (8.8% shareholder yield) to investors in 2025. JD.com returned ~10% via buybacks and dividends. Tencent spent $14.4B on buybacks alone. Meanwhile, PDD sits on $60B in cash — **42% of its market cap** — and returns nothing to shareholders, despite trading at 8x forward earnings. Meituan likewise pays no dividend and runs no buyback while burning cash in delivery subsidies. This capital allocation divergence, combined with the HFCAA/VIE overhang (PDD and Vipshop most exposed as US-only listings), creates meaningful dispersion in risk-adjusted returns across the group.
