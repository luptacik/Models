# Batch 3: Chinese fintech, healthcare, education, and software coverage

**Fifteen Chinese-listed companies across five sub-sectors now have FY2025 results available, revealing sharply divergent trajectories.** The online brokerages (FUTU, TIGR) posted extraordinary growth exceeding 50% driven by a bull market in HK/US equities, while consumer credit (QFIN) hit a wall on deteriorating asset quality. Healthcare platforms crossed into sustained profitability. Education names continue their post-"double reduction" recovery with non-academic pivots. Enterprise SaaS (Kingdee) reached its first-ever IFRS profit, marking a genuine inflection.

All financials below reflect FY2025 (calendar year ended December 2025) unless otherwise noted. Currency conversions use **CNY/USD = 7.25** and **HKD/USD = 7.78**. Confidence flags: **✓** confirmed from filings, **~** estimated/derived, **?** missing or unverifiable.

## What emerges from these fifteen names

The starkest valuation anomaly sits in the fintech/lending cohort. **QFIN at 2.0x trailing P/E** and **TIGR at 6.5x** appear to price in existential risk, yet both generate substantial cash and carry net cash positions covering 35-60% of market cap. YMM's net cash at 52% of market cap creates a similar dynamic. The China discount and regulatory overhang are clearly reflected in prices, but the cash generation argues these are not structurally impaired businesses.

The **healthcare platform convergence** is notable: JD Health, Ali Health, and Ping An HC are all moving in the same direction — expanding gross margins, crossing profitability thresholds, and showing operating leverage. JD Health is furthest along with 8.9% adjusted net margin and **RMB 50B in net cash** (more cash than its entire EV).

In enterprise software, **Kingdee's first-ever IFRS profit** marks a genuine transition milestone. With subscription revenue crossing 50% of total, ARR growing 19% vs. total revenue at 12%, and NDR above 100% for large enterprise, the SaaS economics are beginning to compound. Yonyou remains 2-3 years behind and is still burning cash, yet trades at a higher EV/Revenue (5.5x vs. 3.5x) — a misalignment worth flagging.

Legend Biotech's **CARVYKTI at $1.9B in net trade sales** and approaching operating profitability in 2026 stands in stark contrast to its $3.1B market cap — roughly 1.6x current NTS for a product J&J guides to **>$5B peak sales**. The December 2026 anito-cel PDUFA date is the single most important competitive catalyst to monitor.
