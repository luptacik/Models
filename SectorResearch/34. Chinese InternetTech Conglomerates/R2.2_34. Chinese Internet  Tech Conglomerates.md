# China EV / Semi / Hardware: Batch 2 Standardized Financial Metrics

**Bottom line: China's EV sector delivered 9.8 million vehicles across the seven covered names in FY2025 (+33% weighted average growth), but a brutal price war compressed margins across the board. BYD's net profit fell 19% despite record revenue. The semiconductor trio — SMIC, Cambricon, and Hygon — all benefited enormously from U.S. export controls driving forced domestic substitution, with Cambricon posting its first-ever profit (+453% revenue growth) and all three trading at extreme valuations reflecting strategic scarcity premiums.**

All data as of March 30, 2026. Currency reported in RMB and USD at CNY/USD = 7.25 unless noted. Confidence flags: ✓ confirmed from official source | ~ estimated/approximate | ? unable to find or unverifiable.

## Cross-cutting observations across the universe

Three structural themes dominate this batch. First, **the China EV price war intensified through late 2025 into early 2026**, compressing margins across the board: BYD's gross margin fell 170bp, Li Auto's vehicle margin dropped 190bp, and even margin-expanding names like NIO and XPeng achieved profitability only in Q4 after aggressive cost optimization. BYD, the market leader, saw six consecutive months of YoY delivery declines starting October 2025 — a notable inflection after years of relentless growth.

Second, **U.S. export controls created a paradoxical bonanza for Entity List semiconductor companies**. All three covered semi names sit on the Entity List, yet Cambricon's revenue grew 453%, Hygon's grew 57%, and SMIC's grew 16%. Beijing's forced domestic substitution policy — requiring state-owned data centers to use domestic AI chips — effectively guaranteed demand for these companies. Cambricon and Hygon trade at 66x and 38x EV/Revenue respectively, pricing in years of compounding growth driven by this artificial demand channel. The key risk is any easing of U.S.-China tensions or approved NVIDIA export sales, which would immediately erode these scarcity premiums.

Third, **the startup EV hierarchy reshuffled dramatically in 2025**. Leapmotor overtook both Li Auto and NIO to become the highest-volume Chinese EV startup at 597K units, while XPeng's 126% delivery growth pushed it past Li Auto as well. Li Auto, the 2024 startup leader, saw deliveries decline 19% as its model cycle troughed. The key differentiator going forward is international expansion: Leapmotor (Stellantis JV), BYD (own factories), and Geely (Volvo network) have the most credible global strategies, while NIO, Li Auto, and Xiaomi remain almost entirely domestic.

**Valuation dispersion is extreme.** Geely at 8.5x forward P/E and 0.37x EV/Revenue looks deeply undervalued relative to its 39% delivery growth and improving NEV mix. By contrast, Cambricon at 209x trailing P/E and 66x EV/Revenue demands near-perfection. The semiconductor names carry strategic premium that makes conventional valuation frameworks largely inapplicable — these are national security assets priced as such.
