## SECTION 4: KEY SCORE JUSTIFICATIONS (companies not covered in original report)

### PASS Names — Key Rationale

**Geely (22/25):** Highest score in the entire universe. Overtook BYD as China's #1 seller in Jan 2026. 3.02M deliveries (+39%). Record net profit RMB 16.85B. NEV penetration reached 55.8% (+90% NEV delivery growth). Zeekr privatization completed. Multi-brand strategy (Geely, Zeekr, Lynk, Galaxy) with Volvo/Polestar international network is the most credible global platform among Chinese OEMs. At 8.5x forward P/E and 0.37x EV/Revenue, this is the cheapest EV name in the universe despite the strongest execution. The discount likely reflects legacy-auto perception that hasn't caught up with Geely's NEV transition — Galaxy alone sold 1.24M units (+150%).

**BeOne/BeiGene (21/25):** BRUKINSA is now the global #1 BTK inhibitor by revenue at $3.9B (+49% YoY), surpassing AbbVie's Imbruvica. First-ever GAAP profitability at $287M net income. Redomiciled to Switzerland (de-risking China structure). 2026 guidance of $6.2-6.4B implies continued 20%+ growth. Sonrotoclax FDA accelerated approval anticipated mid-2026 could be the next blockbuster. This is a fundamentally transformed business from the loss-making Chinese biotech of 2022.

**Legend Biotech (21/25):** The most extreme valuation disconnect in the 53-ticker universe. Market cap $3.1B versus CARVYKTI net trade sales of $1.9B — roughly 1.6x current NTS for a product J&J guides to >$5B peak sales. CARVYKTI franchise achieved full-year profitability. 10,000+ patients treated with 97% manufacturing success rate. Company-wide operating profit expected in 2026. The December 2026 anito-cel PDUFA date (Gilead/Arcellx) is the primary competitive risk. Stock at $22 vs consensus PT $67+ represents the widest analyst-to-price gap in the universe.

**NIO (19/25):** First-ever quarterly GAAP profit (Q4, RMB 282.7M). Vehicle margin expanded from 10.2% (Q1) to 18.1% (Q4) — the strongest trajectory in the EV cohort. Multi-brand strategy (NIO/ONVO/Firefly) working — ONVO reached 31% of Q4 deliveries. Q1 2026 guidance of +90-97% YoY delivery growth. Nomura and HSBC both upgraded to Buy (first Nomura Buy in ~3 years). Battery swap network (unique physical moat) now supplemented by in-house Shenji 5nm chip. Balance sheet thin (~$0.7B net cash) is the primary risk.

**Leapmotor (19/25):** #1 Chinese EV startup by volume at 596,555 deliveries (+103%), overtaking Li Auto and NIO. First-ever annual profit (RMB 538M). Stellantis JV provides the most credible European production pathway (B10 in Spain from Oct 2026, circumventing EU tariffs). FAW acquired 5% stake — strategic validation. 2026 target of 1.05M vehicles (+76%) would be another doubling. A10 launching below RMB 100K with LiDAR could be transformative for ultra-value BEV segment.

**FUTU (21/25):** FY2025 revenue +68%, net margin 49.5% — among the highest of any global brokerage. AUC reached $158.4B (+66%). Funded account growth of 954K surpassed 800K guidance by 19%. Goldman Sachs upgraded to Buy. Overseas markets (Moomoo) now ~55% of funded accounts — genuine international diversification. The bear case is cyclicality — these results were boosted by a HK/US equity bull market. A market downturn would materially compress both revenue and margins.

**BOSS Zhipin (18/25):** Online recruitment leader with earnings +72.6%, MAU 60.7M (+14.5%), and a committed 50%+ payout floor via dividends + buybacks ($400M buyback authorized). Trading at 52-week low despite strong fundamentals — the discount reflects China macro/employment anxiety rather than company-specific issues. Consensus PT $23 vs ~$13 stock price (73% upside). The risk is that China's youth unemployment (16.9%) structurally caps recruitment platform growth.

**YMM / Full Truck (18/25):** Digital freight platform with extraordinary unit economics — 36% FCF margin, 35.7% net margin, 88.7% gross margin. Net cash of $4.3B = 52% of market cap. Commission penetration at 88.6% shows the platform is becoming essential infrastructure for Chinese trucking. Operating margin expanded from 22% to 33% in one year. Q4 EPS miss ($0.01 vs $0.14) and rising NPL in the small credit business are the concerns.

## SECTION 5: CONVICTION SUMMARY

This 53-ticker universe reveals a Chinese tech ecosystem in the most capital-intensive and competitively intense phase since the mobile internet era. Three dynamics define the current moment: a $100B+ AI capex supercycle compressing near-term margins across the largest platforms, a food delivery subsidy war destroying ~RMB 100B in annual profits across three major companies, and a first-ever profitability wave among previously loss-making companies (NIO, XPeng, Leapmotor, Bilibili, Cambricon, Kingdee, BeOne, Legend Biotech). Stocks are broadly 20-45% off October 2025 highs, creating the widest gap between analyst targets and market prices in years.

The 14 PASS names cluster into four distinct conviction tiers. **Tier 1 (highest conviction):** Tencent (quality compounder with unassailable moat), Geely (best value in EV at 8.5x P/E), and Legend Biotech (most extreme valuation disconnect at 1.6x NTS). **Tier 2 (strong conviction with specific thesis):** BeOne (global oncology leader reaching profitability), FUTU (best fintech unit economics), Trip.com (highest-growth travel with 81% GM), and BOSS Zhipin (recruitment leader at 52-week low). **Tier 3 (conviction with higher cyclical/execution risk):** NIO and Leapmotor (EV profitability inflection but cyclical), YMM (digital freight monopoly but thin trading volume), JD Health (healthcare platform leader). **Tier 4 (narrowest margin of safety):** TAL (education regulatory tail risk persists).

The 7 DO NOT PURSUE names include two conditional re-entries worth monitoring: Meituan (if food delivery regulation enforced, re-scores to 16-17 and joins WATCHLIST) and Baidu (if Kunlunxin IPO at $16B+ unlocks value). Cambricon's extreme valuation makes it uninvestable through a fundamental lens despite strong revenue growth.

**What would change my mind on the overall universe:** Enforcement of the March 2026 food delivery regulatory ceasefire is the single highest-impact catalyst — it would simultaneously unlock margin recovery at Meituan, JD.com, and Alibaba (three of the seven largest companies). For EV names, reversal of BYD's six-month delivery decline streak would signal the price war is abating. For the semiconductor trio, any approved NVIDIA H20 export sales to China would immediately erode the scarcity premium that sustains their extreme valuations.
