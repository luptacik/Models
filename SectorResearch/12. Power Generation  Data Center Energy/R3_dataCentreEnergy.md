## SECTOR SENTIMENT SUMMARY

The overall tone across this 39-company group is **decisively bullish**, anchored by the strongest demand signal most of these companies have seen in their histories — AI/data center power demand. **Record backlogs are near-universal**: VRT $15B, ETN $19.6B, PWR $44B, FIX $12B, EME $13.3B, GEV $150B total, Siemens Energy €146B, and Schneider €25.4B. Every major E&C contractor (FIX, EME, STRL, PWR) reported record or near-record backlogs with data center work now comprising 40–70% of revenue, and all guided to double-digit 2026 growth.

**Potential catalyst watch — names where narrative diverges from price action**: Bloom Energy (BE) trades above median analyst PT with a deeply divided Street and ~790% run from lows, creating potential trap risk if 2026 execution falters. Conversely, First Solar (FSLR) at ~10x forward PE has been punished for 2026 tariff headwinds but retains $2.1B+ in 45X credits and domestic manufacturing advantages that could catalyze a sharp recovery if policy clarity improves. NuScale (SMR) trades near 52-week lows with no binding contracts and relentless Fluor selling, yet holds the only NRC-certified SMR design — a divergence that will resolve one way or the other with TVA progress. IESC's $98.7M of insider selling by Executive Chairman Gendell (84 transactions, zero purchases) stands out as the most aggressive insider selling in the entire group despite strong fundamental momentum.

**Cross-cutting themes shaping the sector**: (1) **Nuclear renaissance is real but bifurcated** — CEG, BWXT, and RR.L are monetizing nuclear assets today, while OKLO and SMR remain pre-revenue development plays with massive order books that are entirely non-binding; (2) **Natural gas is the bridge fuel for AI** — EQT's 2.6 Bcf/d of data center gas commitments and WMB's $7.3B power innovation pipeline represent a structural demand shift, with GEV's gas turbine backlog "sold out through 2030" confirming the supply-side tightness; (3) **Tariffs are a double-edged catalyst** — benefiting domestic manufacturers (POWL, ETN, GNRC, FSLR's U.S. plants) while pressuring import-dependent supply chains (NVT ~$80M hit, MNTR.ST margin contraction, FSLR's SE Asian idling); (4) **Hyperscaler capex expected >$700B in 2026** (+50% YoY) is the macro accelerant underpinning the entire group, with direct contract visibility now extending to utilities (CEG-Microsoft, VST-Meta/Amazon, TLN-AWS), fuel cell providers (BE-AEP-Oracle), and infrastructure services (FIX, STRL, PWR data center backlogs doubling YoY).
