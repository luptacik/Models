## BATCH 2 — KEY OBSERVATIONS

**PWR is the standout** — $44B record backlog with 27% growth, driven by grid modernization and data center power demand. The company is investing $500M–$700M to vertically integrate transformer manufacturing, which is a supply-chain bottleneck play. Valuation is stretched at 26x NTM EBITDA, but the backlog visibility is exceptional and the secular tailwind (AI/data center power demand, grid hardening) is multi-decade. The low gross margin (~15%) is structural for an EPC business — EBITDA margin (~10%) is the right metric.

**GNRC is in transition** — residential business is cyclically weak (outage-dependent), but the C&I pivot into data center backup power is the thesis. The $400M data center backlog is early but growing, and hyperscaler pilot programs could meaningfully expand backlog if MSAs are signed. The key gating question is whether data center C&I revenue can offset residential cyclicality at scale.

**FLNC is a backlog/growth story with margin risk** — $5.5B record backlog and a $30B pipeline, but Q1 FY2026 gross margin of 4.9% and -$52M EBITDA is concerning. Management insists this is discrete project costs (non-systemic), and the rolling 12-month adj. gross margin of 12.3% supports that view. The full-year EBITDA guide of $40–60M implies massive margin expansion in H2 — this is a show-me story. EV/Revenue of 1.1x is the cheapest in the sector if margins normalize.

## BATCH 3 — KEY OBSERVATIONS

**TT is a compounding machine pivoting into data center cooling** — the Applied Solutions bookings surge (+120% Q4, 200% book-to-bill) is the tell. The Stellar Energy acquisition for modular data center cooling is the strategic move to watch. Record $7.8B backlog with $1B+ extending beyond 2027 provides exceptional visibility. The weak spots (residential HVAC, transport refrigeration, China) are cyclical, not structural. Valuation at ~21x NTM EBITDA reflects the quality premium — this is a "pay up for quality" name with improving mix toward higher-margin, higher-growth data center exposure.

**ON is a classic downcycle value story** — revenue down 15% FY2025, gross margins 700bps below target due to 68% utilization, but FCF margin hit a record 24% through disciplined capex cuts (FabRight strategy reduced capacity 12%). The thesis is cyclical recovery: auto inventory digestion is "largely behind us," industrial just posted its first YoY increase in 8 quarters, and AI data center revenue doubled to $250M. The key question is how fast utilization recovers to mid-70s+ to close the 700bps underutilization gap — every 100bps of utilization improvement flows through directly to gross margin. At ~4.2x EV/Revenue, ON is priced for modest recovery, not a full re-rating to 53% target margins.

**SBC Note**: ON is the first name in this universe where SBC is explicitly quantifiable from guidance disclosures (~$144M annualized, or ~2.4% of revenue). This is reasonable for a semiconductor IDM.

## BATCH 4 — KEY OBSERVATIONS

**SU.PA is the large-cap quality anchor** — €40B+ revenue milestone, €25B backlog (+18%), 30% of revenue from data centers with >10% CAGR outlook. The 18.7% EBITDA margin with 50bps expansion and 111% FCF conversion is best-in-class for this peer group. FX headwind (€850–950M revenue impact) and H1 gross margin caution are the near-term risks.

**ENR.DE is the turnaround story with the biggest backlog** — €138B order backlog is staggering, with 500bps of margin improvement in FY2025. The thesis is Gamesa reaching breakeven (FY2026 target) unlocking group-level margin expansion to 14–16% by FY2028. At ~33x EV/NTM EBITDA and ~€132B market cap (up 199% in one year), the stock is pricing in near-perfect execution. The transformer factory investments and US brownfield expansion position it for DC power/grid demand.

**RR.L is the most transformed European industrial** — from near-bankruptcy to £3.27B FCF and £1.9B net cash. The Power Systems division (+20% revenue, +45% data center) is the energy thesis, while Civil Aerospace (24.9% margin) is the cash engine. SMR program (sole UK provider, ČEZ partnership) is long-duration optionality. The £7–9B buyback program (2026–2028) signals management confidence. At ~2.4x EV/Revenue and ~11x EV/operating profit, this is arguably the cheapest quality name in the entire European batch.

**MNTR.ST is the purest data center cooling play but margins are under pressure** — >200% organic DCT growth and SEK 2.9B in Q4 record orders are exceptional demand signals. However, EBITDA margins fell from 18.2% to 13.6% in Q2 due to dual-site costs, AirTech underutilization, and FX. The 1.6x book-to-bill suggests the revenue growth will come — the question is whether margins recover to the 13–16% target range as new facilities ramp.

**2308.TW (Delta Electronics) is the AI/data center power supply juggernaut** — 32% FY2025 revenue growth, 21.3% EBITDA margin, and 147% operating profit growth in Q4. The company is riding the GPU power supply wave (GB300 shipments) and liquid cooling ramp (~9% of revenue). The Thailand factory buildout and AI Data Center Microgrid Solution position it for continued share gains. The key risk is valuation: at ~$120B market cap on $17B revenue (~7x EV/Rev, ~30x EV/EBITDA), the stock prices in substantial continued growth. Mobility/EV segment weakness (-5% QoQ) is a secondary drag.

**267260.KS (HD Hyundai Electric) is the purest transformer play** — 27.6% Q4 operating margin (record) driven by ultra-high voltage transformer demand from AI data centers and grid modernization. The $6.73B backlog extending through 2031 provides exceptional visibility. The selective order strategy (prioritizing high-value projects over volume) is driving margin expansion. The Ulsan + Alabama capacity expansion (+30% output by 2026) is the execution play. At ~$25B market cap and <0.5x net debt/EBITDA, the balance sheet is pristine. The risk is concentration: this is a single-product company in a cyclical industry, and at ~30x EV/EBITDA the valuation leaves no room for a demand pullback.
